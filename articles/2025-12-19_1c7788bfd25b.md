## Abstract
Neural network have been widely applied for solving partial differential equations (PDEs), but they still exhibit limitations. Recently, transformer-based operators, which have demonstrated exceptional performance in fields like computer vision, have been adapted for PDE solving. We propose a dynamic sparse physics-aware network (DSPAN) based on a physics-slicing mechanism and a TopK selection architecture. First, the input features are partitioned into slices to capture the intrinsic physical states. Then, these slices are scored for importance, and only the top K most critical slices are selected for attention computation. This architecture effectively captures the underlying physical information in PDEs while improving computational efficiency, achieving dual gains in both accuracy and speed. Experiments show that DSPAN achieves a relative improvement 15.35% on eight benchmark data sets, with a particularly notable 33% enhancement in plasticity equations. This robustly validates the algorithm’s capability to deliver highly accurate and resource-efficient solutions for complex PDE systems.

## 摘要（中文）
神经网络已广泛应用于求解偏微分方程（PDE），但它们仍然存在局限性。最近，基于变压器的算子在计算机视觉等领域表现出了卓越的性能，已适用于偏微分方程求解。我们提出了一种基于物理切片机制和 TopK 选择架构的动态稀疏物理感知网络（DSPAN）。首先，将输入特征划分为切片以捕获内在的物理状态。然后，对这些切片的重要性进行评分，并且仅选择前 K 个最关键的切片进行注意力计算。该架构有效地捕获了偏微分方程中的底层物理信息，同时提高了计算效率，实现了精度和速度的双重增益。实验表明，DSPAN 在 8 个基准数据集上实现了 15.35% 的相对改进，尤其是可塑性方程提高了 33%。这有力地验证了该算法为复杂的偏微分方程系统提供高度准确且资源高效的解决方案的能力。
