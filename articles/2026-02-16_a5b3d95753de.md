## Abstract
arXiv:2303.08107v3 Announce Type: replace Abstract: Social network structures play an important role in the lives of animals by affecting individual fitness and the spread of disease and information. Nevertheless, we still lack a good understanding of how these structures emerge from the behavior of individuals. Generative network models provide a powerful approach that can help close this gap. Empirical research has shown that trait-based social preferences (preferences for social partners with certain trait values, such as sex, body size, relatedness etc.) play a key role in the formation of social networks across species. Currently, however, we lack a good understanding of how such preferences affect network properties. In this study: 1) we develop a general and flexible generative network model that can create artificial (simulated) networks where social connection is affected by trait-based social preferences; 2) we use this model to investigate how different trait-based social preferences affect social network structure and function. We find that the preferences can affect the efficiency of the networks in terms of transmitting disease and information, and their robustness against fragmentation when individuals disappear, with the effects often - but not always - going in the direction of slower transmission and lower robustness. Furthermore, the extent and form of the effects depend on both the type of preference and the type of trait it is used with. The findings lead to new insights about the potential mechanisms driving the structural diversity of animal social networks, the importance of trait value distributions for social structure, the degree distributions of social networks, and the detectability of trait effects from network data. Overall, the study shows that trait-based social preferences can have far-reaching consequences for populations.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2303.08107v3 公告类型：替换 摘要：社交网络结构通过影响个体健康以及疾病和信息的传播，在动物的生活中发挥着重要作用。然而，我们仍然缺乏对这些结构如何从个体行为中产生的良好理解。生成网络模型提供了一种强大的方法，可以帮助缩小这一差距。实证研究表明，基于特质的社会偏好（对具有某些特质值的社会伙伴的偏好，例如性别、体型、相关性等）在跨物种社交网络的形成中发挥着关键作用。然而，目前我们对这些偏好如何影响网络属性缺乏充分的了解。在本研究中：1）我们开发了一种通用且灵活的生成网络模型，可以创建人工（模拟）网络，其中社会联系受到基于特征的社会偏好的影响； 2）我们使用这个模型来研究不同的基于特质的社会偏好如何影响社交网络的结构和功能。我们发现，偏好会影响网络在传播疾病和信息方面的效率，以及当个体消失时网络对碎片化的鲁棒性，其影响通常（但并非总是）朝着传播速度减慢和鲁棒性降低的方向发展。此外，影响的程度和形式取决于偏好的类型及其所使用的特征类型。这些发现带来了关于驱动动物社交网络结构多样性的潜在机制、特征值分布对社会结构的重要性、社交网络的程度分布以及网络数据特征效应的可检测性的新见解。总体而言，该研究表明，基于特质的社会偏好会对人群产生深远的影响。
