## Abstract
arXiv:2509.14146v2 Announce Type: replace Abstract: In many cases, the predictions of machine learning interatomic potentials (MLIPs) can be interpreted as a sum of body-ordered contributions, which is explicit when the model is directly built on neighbor density correlation descriptors, and implicit when the model captures the correlations through non-linear functions of low body-order terms. In both cases, the "effective body-orderedness" of MLIPs remains largely unexplained: how do the models decompose the total energy into body-ordered contributions, and how does their body-orderedness affect the accuracy and learning behavior? In answering these questions, we first discuss the complexities in imposing the many-body expansion on ab initio calculations at the atomic limit. Next, we train a curated set of MLIPs on datasets of hydrogen clusters and reveal the inherent tendency of the ML models to deduce their own, effective body-order trends, which are dependent on the model type and dataset makeup. Finally, we present different trends in the convergence of the body-orders and generalizability of the models, providing useful insights for the development of future MLIPs.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2509.14146v2 公告类型：替换 摘要：在许多情况下，机器学习原子间势（MLIP）的预测可以解释为体序贡献的总和，当模型直接建立在邻居密度相关描述符上时，这是显式的，而当模型通过低体序项的非线性函数捕获相关性时，这是隐式的。在这两种情况下，MLIP 的“有效体有序性”在很大程度上仍然无法解释：模型如何将总能量分解为体有序贡献，以及它们的体有序性如何影响准确性和学习行为？在回答这些问题时，我们首先讨论在原子极限下对从头计算进行多体展开的复杂性。接下来，我们在氢簇数据集上训练一组精选的 MLIP，并揭示 ML 模型推断其自身有效体序趋势的固有趋势，这些趋势取决于模型类型和数据集构成。最后，我们提出了体阶收敛和模型的普遍性方面的不同趋势，为未来 MLIP 的发展提供了有用的见解。
