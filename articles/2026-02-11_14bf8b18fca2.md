## Abstract
arXiv:2602.09491v1 Announce Type: new Abstract: Sensitivity to small changes in the environment is crucial for many real-world tasks, enabling living and artificial systems to make correct behavioral decisions. It has been shown that such sensitivity is maximized when a system operates near the critical point of a phase transition. However, proximity to criticality introduces large fluctuations and diverging timescales. Hence, to leverage the maximal sensitivity, it would require impractically long integration periods. Here, we analytically and computationally demonstrate how the optimal tuning of a recurrent neural network is determined given a finite integration time. Rather than maximizing the theoretically available sensitivity, we find networks attain different sensitivities depending on the available time. Consequently, the optimal dynamic regime can shift away from criticality when integration times are finite, highlighting the necessity of incorporating finite-time considerations into studies of information processing.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2602.09491v1 公告类型：新 摘要：对环境中微小变化的敏感性对于许多现实世界的任务至关重要，它使生命和人工系统能够做出正确的行为决策。事实证明，当系统在相变临界点附近运行时，这种灵敏度会最大化。然而，接近临界点会带来巨大的波动和不同的时间尺度。因此，为了利用最大灵敏度，需要不切实际的长积分周期。在这里，我们通过分析和计算方式演示了如何在给定有限积分时间的情况下确定循环神经网络的最佳调整。我们发现网络并没有最大化理论上可用的灵敏度，而是根据可用时间获得不同的灵敏度。因此，当积分时间有限时，最优动态机制可能会偏离临界性，这凸显了将有限时间考虑因素纳入信息处理研究的必要性。
