## Abstract
Author(s): A. Shen, J. Chen, R. Kaji, S. Yamamoto, H. Sasakura, T. Uemura, and S. Adachi Here, the authors observe a large nuclear magnetic field, B N , exceeding 1 T in bulk n -AlGaAs via time-resolved Kerr rotation measurements, exhibiting a two-stage formation process comprising a rapid initial rise followed by gradual saturation. Based on experimental results, the authors modify the nuclear spin polarization formation model and conclude that a large B N forms due to the combined effects of increased electron localization sites from aluminum incorporation and the suppression of quadrupole-induced relaxation by a strong magnetic field. [Phys. Rev. B 113, 035202] Published Thu Jan 15, 2026
