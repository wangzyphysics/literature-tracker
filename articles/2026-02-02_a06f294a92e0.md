## Abstract
arXiv:2601.23017v1 Announce Type: new Abstract: Functionalization of nanoporous metallic materials enables the tailoring of surface chemistry and morphology in nanostructured materials, optimising their performance for electrocatalytic and sensor applications. Liquid phase chemical functionalization is governed by liquid solid interfaces. Yet, these interfaces remain poorly understood due to the challenges of characterising the liquid phase at high spatial and chemical resolutions. To elucidate pathways for functionalizing nanoscale metals, it is crucial to measure the distribution of species, including light elements, across the liquid solid interface, capturing both reactants and products. Here, we employ cryogenic atom probe tomography to directly analyse frozen liquid solid reaction interfaces at near atomic resolution. Focusing on the interaction of iodide and sodium ions with nanoporous gold, we observe the formation of iodine containing complexes on gold nanoligament surfaces and subsurfaces. These findings reveal aspects of the gold iodide system that were previously hidden, including the reaction mechanism between iodide and gold atoms on the surface, and the multiple gold iodide complexes forming. Our work demonstrates that cryogenic atom probe tomography can provide unprecedented visualisation and characterisation of nanoscale interfaces during chemical and electrochemical reactions, with potential implications for modern manufacturing, energy technologies, and sustainable materials development.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2601.23017v1 公告类型：新 摘要：纳米多孔金属材料的功能化能够定制纳米结构材料的表面化学和形态，优化其电催化和传感器应用的性能。液相化学官能化受液固界面控制。然而，由于在高空间和化学分辨率下表征液相的挑战，这些界面仍然知之甚少。为了阐明纳米级金属功能化的途径，测量包括轻元素在内的物质在液固界面上的分布、捕获反应物和产物至关重要。在这里，我们采用低温原子探针断层扫描以接近原子分辨率直接分析冷冻液固反应界面。重点关注碘离子和钠离子与纳米多孔金的相互作用，我们观察到金纳米韧带表面和次表面上含碘络合物的形成。这些发现揭示了以前隐藏的碘化金系统的各个方面，包括碘化物与表面金原子之间的反应机制，以及形成多个碘化金络合物。我们的工作表明，低温原子探针断层扫描可以在化学和电化学反应过程中提供前所未有的纳米级界面可视化和表征，对现代制造、能源技术和可持续材料开发具有潜在影响。
