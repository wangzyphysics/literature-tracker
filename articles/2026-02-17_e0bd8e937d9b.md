## Abstract
arXiv:2507.19127v2 Announce Type: replace Abstract: Non-reciprocal interactions are a defining feature of many complex systems, biological, ecological, and technological, often pushing them far from equilibrium and enabling rich dynamical responses. These asymmetries can arise at multiple levels: locally, in the dynamics of individual units, and globally, in the topology of their interactions. In this work, we investigate how these two forms of non-reciprocity interact in networks of neuronal populations. At the local level, each population is modeled by a non-reciprocally coupled set of excitatory and inhibitory neural populations exhibiting transient amplification and reactivity. At the network level, these populations are coupled via directed, asymmetric connections that introduce structural non-normality. Since non-reciprocal interactions generically lead to non-normal linear operators, we frame both local and global asymmetries in terms of non-normal dynamics. Using a modified Wilson-Cowan framework, we analyze how the interplay between these two types of non-normality shapes the system's behavior. We show that their combination leads to emergent collective dynamics, including fluctuation-driven transitions, dimensionality reduction, and novel nonequilibrium steady states. Our results provide a minimal yet flexible framework to understand how multi-scale non-reciprocities govern complex dynamics in neural and other interconnected systems.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2507.19127v2 公告类型：替换 摘要：非互惠相互作用是许多复杂系统（生物、生态和技术）的一个定义特征，常常使它们远离平衡并实现丰富的动态响应。这些不对称性可能出现在多个层面：局部的，在单个单元的动态中，以及全局的，在它们相互作用的拓扑中。在这项工作中，我们研究了这两种形式的非互惠如何在神经元群体网络中相互作用。在局部水平上，每个群体都由一组非相互耦合的兴奋性和抑制性神经群体建模，表现出短暂的放大和反应性。在网络层面，这些群体通过定向、不对称连接进行耦合，从而引入结构非正态性。由于非互易相互作用通常会导致非正态线性算子，因此我们根据非正态动力学来构建局部和全局不对称性。使用修改后的 Wilson-Cowan 框架，我们分析了这两种非正态性之间的相互作用如何塑造系统的行为。我们表明，它们的组合会导致出现集体动态，包括波动驱动的转变、降维和新颖的非平衡稳态。我们的结果提供了一个最小但灵活的框架来理解多尺度非互易性如何控制神经系统和其他互连系统中的复杂动态。
