## Abstract
Alternating currents (ACs) offer precise synthetic control through potential modulation, yet theoretical understanding of the underlying mechanisms remains scarce as the simulated outcomes are generally reproducible by direct currents (DCs). Further computational explorations of the chemical space are also limited by the multiscale nature of the electrochemical dynamics. In this work, we propose to interpret AC-electrolytic outcomes as Floquet states. This narrows the simulation domain to a single AC period, which reduces computational costs, while decomposing the coupled electrochemical kinetics into interpretable subprocesses, providing physical intuition for future synthetic designs. Notably, we explore how non-electrochemical steps may create Floquet AC outcomes with no DC analogues, strengthening the role of AC in electrosynthesis. Experimentally, we demonstrate how AC facilitates mass transport for selective methyl thiophene-2-carboxylate electroreduction via coarse-graining effects captured in the Floquet states. Ultimately, given the success of Floquet engineering in quantum condensed matter systems and photonic architectures, we hope to inspire similar programmes in AC electrochemistry.

## 摘要（中文）
交流电 (AC) 通过电势调制提供精确的合成控制，但对基本机制的理论理解仍然很少，因为模拟结果通常可以通过直流电 (DC) 重现。化学空间的进一步计算探索也受到电化学动力学的多尺度性质的限制。在这项工作中，我们建议将交流电解结果解释为 Floquet 状态。这将模拟域缩小到单个交流周期，从而降低了计算成本，同时将耦合电化学动力学分解为可解释的子过程，为未来的合成设计提供物理直觉。值得注意的是，我们探索非电化学步骤如何在没有直流类似物的情况下产生 Floquet AC 结果，从而加强交流在电合成中的作用。通过实验，我们证明了交流电如何通过 Floquet 态中捕获的粗粒效应促进选择性噻吩-2-甲酸甲酯电还原的质量传递。最终，鉴于 Floquet 工程在量子凝聚态系统和光子结构中的成功，我们希望能够激发交流电化学领域的类似项目。
