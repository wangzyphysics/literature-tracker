## Abstract
arXiv:2502.18522v2 Announce Type: replace-cross Abstract: Imaging and hyperspectral data analysis is central to progress across biology, medicine, chemistry, and physics. The core challenge lies in converting high-resolution or high-dimensional datasets into interpretable representations that enable insight into the underlying physical or chemical properties of a system. Traditional analysis relies on expert-designed, multistep workflows, such as denoising, feature extraction, clustering, dimensionality reduction, and physics-based deconvolution, or on machine learning (ML) methods that accelerate individual steps. Both approaches, however, typically demand significant human intervention, including hyperparameter tuning and data labeling. Achieving the next level of autonomy in scientific imaging requires designing effective reward-based workflows that guide algorithms toward best data representation for human or automated decision-making. Here, we discuss recent advances in reward-based workflows for image analysis, which capture key elements of human reasoning and exhibit strong transferability across various tasks. We highlight how reward-driven approaches enable a shift from supervised black-box models toward explainable, unsupervised optimization on the examples of Scanning Probe and Electron Microscopies. Such reward-based frameworks are promising for a broad range of applications, including classification, regression, structure-property mapping, and general hyperspectral data processing.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2502.18522v2 公告类型：replace-cross 摘要：成像和高光谱数据分析是生物学、医学、化学和物理学进步的核心。核心挑战在于将高分辨率或高维数据集转换为可解释的表示形式，从而能够深入了解系统的底层物理或化学特性。传统分析依赖于专家设计的多步骤工作流程，例如去噪、特征提取、聚类、降维和基于物理的反卷积，或者依赖于加速各个步骤的机器学习 (ML) 方法。然而，这两种方法通常都需要大量的人工干预，包括超参数调整和数据标记。要在科学成像领域实现更高水平的自主性，需要设计有效的基于奖励的工作流程，引导算法实现人类或自动决策的最佳数据表示。在这里，我们讨论基于奖励的图像分析工作流程的最新进展，该工作流程捕获人类推理的关键要素，并在各种任务中表现出强大的可移植性。我们强调奖励驱动的方法如何使扫描探针和电子显微镜的示例从受监督的黑盒模型转向可解释的、无监督的优化。这种基于奖励的框架有望得到广泛的应用，包括分类、回归、结构-属性映射和一般高光谱数据处理。
