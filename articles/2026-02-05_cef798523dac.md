## Abstract
Hydrogen bubble formation within nanoscale voids is a critical mechanism underlying the embrittlement of metallic materials, yet its atomistic origins remains elusive. Here, we present an accurate and transferable machine-learned potential (MLP) for the tungsten-hydrogen binary system within the neuroevolution potential (NEP) framework, trained through active learning on extensive density functional theory data. The developed NEP-WH model reproduces a wide range of lattice and defect properties in tungsten systems, as well as hydrogen solubility, with near first-principles accuracy, while retaining the efficiency of empirical potentials. Crucially, it is the first MLP capable of capturing hydrogen trapping and H2formation in nanovoids, with quantitative fidelity. Large-scale machine-learning molecular dynamics simulations reveal a distinct aggregation pathway where planar hydrogen clusters nucleate and grow along {100} planes near voids, with hexagonal close-packed structures emerging at their intersections. Under uniaxial tension, these aggregates promote bubble fracture and the development of regular {100} cracks, suppressing dislocation activity and resulting in brittle fracture behavior. This work provides detailed atomistic insights into hydrogen bubble evolution and fracture in nanovoids, enables predictive modeling of structural degradation in extreme environments, and advances fundamental understanding of hydrogen-induced damage in structural metals.

## 摘要（中文）
纳米级空隙内氢气泡的形成是金属材料脆化的关键机制，但其原子起源仍然难以捉摸。在这里，我们在神经进化电位（NEP）框架内为钨-氢二元系统提出了一个准确且可转移的机器学习电位（MLP），通过对广泛的密度泛函理论数据的主动学习进行训练。开发的 NEP-WH 模型以接近第一原理的精度再现了钨系统中的各种晶格和缺陷特性以及氢溶解度，同时保留了经验势的​​效率。至关重要的是，它是第一个能够以定量保真度捕获纳米空隙中的氢捕获和 H2 形成的 MLP。大规模机器学习分子动力学模拟揭示了一种独特的聚集途径，其中平面氢簇在空隙附近沿 {100} 平面成核和生长，并在其交叉点处出现六边形密堆积结构。在单轴拉伸下，这些聚集体促进气泡破裂和规则的{100}裂纹的发展，抑制位错活动并导致脆性断裂行为。这项工作提供了对纳米空隙中氢气泡演化和断裂的详细原子学见解，能够对极端环境中的结构退化进行预测建模，并增进对结构金属中氢引起的损伤的基本理解。
