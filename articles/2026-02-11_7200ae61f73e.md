## Abstract
arXiv:2602.10041v1 Announce Type: new Abstract: Heat conduction in weakly collisional, magnetised plasma is challenging to model accurately due to multifaceted physics governing heat-carrying electrons, including microinstabilities that scatter electrons and modify heat transport. Capturing these effects requires multidimensional kinetic theory simulations, which are computationally expensive. Experimental constraints overcome this issue, resulting in improved understanding of thermal transport in systems such as the intra-cluster medium of galaxy clusters, and the hot-spot in inertial confinement fusion. In this paper, we present a new experimental platform that produces a weakly collisional high-\b{eta} plasma expected to be susceptible to the whistler heat-flux instability. This platform, to be fielded on the Orion laser, enables characterisation of whistler-regulated thermal conductivity. The platform design is assessed using radiation-magnetohydrodynamics simulations with the code FLASH. Simulations using three thermal conduction models predict conductivity suppression by over an order of magnitude relative to the Spitzer value at whistler saturation, demonstrating the efficacy of the platform.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2602.10041v1 公告类型：新 摘要：由于控制载热电子的多面物理场（包括散射电子和改变热传输的微观不稳定性），弱碰撞磁化等离子体中的热传导很难准确建模。捕捉这些效应需要多维动力学理论模拟，这在计算上是昂贵的。实验限制克服了这个问题，从而提高了对系统中热传输的理解，例如星系团的团内介质和惯性约束聚变的热点。在本文中，我们提出了一个新的实验平台，该平台产生弱碰撞高β等离子体，预计容易受到惠斯勒热通量不稳定性的影响。该平台将部署在猎户座激光器上，能够表征哨子调节的热导率。该平台设计通过辐射磁流体动力学模拟和代码 FLASH 进行评估。使用三个热传导模型进行的模拟预测，相对于惠斯勒饱和时的斯皮策值，电导率抑制会超过一个数量级，从而证明了该平台的功效。
