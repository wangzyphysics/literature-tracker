## Abstract
arXiv:2510.05339v2 Announce Type: replace Abstract: We present the first computational framework for molecular dynamics simulation of MoS2 doped with 25 elements spanning metals, non-metals, and transition metals using Meta's Universal Model for Atoms machine learning interatomic potential (MLIP). Benchmarking against density functional theory calculations demonstrates the accuracy of the MLIP for simulating doped-MoS2 systems and highlights opportunities for improvement. Using the MLIP, we perform heating-cooling simulations of doped-MoS2 supercells. The simulations capture complex phenomena including dopant clustering, MoS2 layer fracturing, interlayer diffusion, and chemical compound formation at orders-of-magnitude reduced computational cost compared to density functional theory. This work provides an open-source computational workflow for application-oriented design of doped-MoS2, enabling high-throughput screening of dopant candidates and optimization of compositions for targeted tribological, electronic, and optoelectronic performance. The MLIP bridges the accuracy-efficiency gap between first-principles methods and empirical potentials, and the framework offers unprecedented opportunities for large-scale materials discovery in two-dimensional doped material systems.

arXiv:2510.05339v2 公告类型：替换 摘要：我们使用 Meta 的通用原子模型机器学习原子间势 (MLIP) 提出了第一个用于掺杂 25 种金属、非金属和过渡金属元素的 MoS2 分子动力学模拟的计算框架。密度泛函理论计算的基准测试证明了 MLIP 用于模拟掺杂 MoS2 系统的准确性，并强调了改进的机会。使用 MLIP，我们对掺杂 MoS2 超级电池进行加热-冷却模拟。与密度泛函理论相比，模拟捕获了复杂的现象，包括掺杂剂聚集、MoS2 层破裂、层间扩散和化合物形成，计算成本降低了几个数量级。这项工作为面向应用的掺杂 MoS2 设计提供了一个开源计算工作流程，能够对候选掺杂剂进行高通量筛选，并针对目标摩擦学、电子和光电性能优化成分。 MLIP 弥合了第一原理方法和经验潜力之间的精度与效率差距，该框架为二维掺杂材料系统中的大规模材料发现提供了前所未有的机会。
