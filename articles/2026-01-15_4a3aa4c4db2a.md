## Abstract
Photoswitches provide the opportunity to remotely and precisely control matter on the nanoscopic scale. For many materials and biological applications, photoswitches with long wavelength response are essential, however few switches offer inherent response to red/near infra-red light. Previous works have described the use of intermolecular interactions as a method to redshift the activation wavelength of photoswitches and to improve thermal half-life, however these systems are limited in their application due to the inherent bimolecularity of this strategy preventing its use in dilute or complex environments. Herein, we describe the use of topologically constrained supramolecular interactions to improve the switching properties of an indigo photoswitch within a [2]-rotaxane. This enabled photoswitching with near-IR (730 nm) light, as well as a 100-fold increase in thermal half-life and double the population of the metastable state under constant irradiation. This surpasses previous attempts at using supramolecular interactions to increase the thermal half-life by >10-fold. This novel strategy towards the redshifting and fine-tuning of these molecular photoswitches has implications for the design of molecular machines and applied switching technologies. We anticipate that our insights into the design of such molecules will unlock new applications for mechanically interlocked molecules.

## 摘要（中文）
光电开关提供了在纳米尺度上远程精确控制物质的机会。对于许多材料和生物应用来说，具有长波长响应的光电开关是必不可少的，但很少有开关能够对红光/近红外光提供固有响应。以前的工作描述了使用分子间相互作用作为红移光开关激活波长和提高热半衰期的方法，然而，由于这种策略固有的双分子性阻碍了其在稀释或复杂环境中的使用，这些系统的应用受到限制。在此，我们描述了使用拓扑约束的超分子相互作用来改善[2]-轮烷内靛蓝光开关的开关特性。这使得近红外（730 nm）光的光开关成为可能，并且热半衰期增加了 100 倍，并且在持续照射下亚稳态的数量增加了一倍。这超过了之前利用超分子相互作用将热半衰期延长 10 倍以上的尝试。这种对这些分子光开关进行红移和微调的新颖策略对分子机器的设计和应用开关技术具有重要意义。我们预计，我们对此类分子设计的见解将开启机械互锁分子的新应用。
