## Abstract
arXiv:2602.13708v1 Announce Type: new Abstract: Symmetry is often treated in philosophy of physics as an interpretive problem. A particularly lively dispute concerns local symmetries: do they indicate surplus structure that ought to be expunged, or are they merely a harmless redundancy? One influential response favours the second option for certain theories -- those dubbed internally sophisticated. And indeed, in much of physics practice, local symmetries are left implicit: one simply works "up to isomorphism'' without pausing over invariance. But not always. In some settings, local symmetry and invariance become pressing practical concerns for physicists. Yet philosophical discussions of sophistication have paid little sustained attention to when, and why, this happens. Surveying textbook general relativity (GR) and gauge theory, I identify the settings in which diffeomorphism invariance or gauge invariance must be handled explicitly. (Here a setting is a choice of representational framework or background assumptions within which one formulates and uses the theory -- for instance, linearisation, an initial-value formulation, or a Hamiltonian $3+1$ formalism.) I propose an operational criterion -- background-relative sophistication (BRS) -- and argue that it accounts well for the pattern: it marks just where symmetry can stay implicit and where it must be made explicit. Quantum and subsystem settings raise a further difficulty: there, certain tasks (superposition and gluing) force symmetry into view even for theories that are BRS.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2602.13708v1 公告类型：新 摘要：对称性在物理学哲学中经常被视为一个解释问题。一个特别激烈的争论涉及局部对称性：它们是否表明应该消除的剩余结构，或者它们仅仅是无害的冗余？一种有影响力的回应支持某些理论的第二种选择——那些被称为内部复杂的理论。事实上，在许多物理实践中，局域对称性都是隐含的：人们只是简单地“达到同构”，而不会暂停不变性。但并非总是如此。在某些情况下，局域对称性和不变性成为物理学家迫切需要考虑的实际问题。然而，复杂性的哲学讨论很少持续关注这种情况何时以及为何发生。纵观教科书广义相对论（GR）和规范理论，我确定了微分同胚的设置。不变性或规范不变性必须明确处理（这里的设置是一种表达框架或背景假设的选择，在其中制定和使用理论——例如，线性化、初始值公式或哈密顿$3+1$形式主义。）我提出了一个操作标准——背景相对复杂度（BRS）——并认为它很好地解释了这种模式：它标记了对称性可以在哪里保持隐式以及在哪里必须明确的量子和子系统设置。另一个困难是：即使对于 BRS 理论，某些任务（叠加和粘合）也会迫使人们看到对称性。
