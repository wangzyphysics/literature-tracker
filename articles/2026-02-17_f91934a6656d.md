## Abstract
arXiv:2602.14773v1 Announce Type: new Abstract: The El Ni\~no Southern Oscillation (ENSO) is the dominant driver of interannual global climate variability and can lead to extreme weather events such as droughts or flooding. Recently, we have developed several statistical approaches for early ENSO forecasting, in particular, its El Ni\~no phase. The climate network-based approach allows forecasting the onset of an El Ni\~no event or its absence about 1 year ahead [1]. The complexity-based approach allows additionally to forecast the magnitude of an upcoming El Ni\~no event in the calendar year before the onset [2]. Additionally, we have developed methods for forecasting the type (Eastern Pacific or Central Pacific) of an El Ni\~no [3] and for probabilistic forecasting of La Ni\~na and neutral events [4], also by the end of the calendar year before the event. Here we present the forecasts of these methods for 2026. The climate network and the complexity-based approach do not provide concurring signals for this year. The combined forecast indicates that a neutral event is more likely than an El Ni\~no. If an El Ni\~no develops in 2026, the complexity-based approach predicts a weaker event with a magnitude of $0.84\pm0.36${\deg}C.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2602.14773v1 公告类型：新 摘要：厄尔尼诺南方涛动（ENSO）是年际全球气候变化的主要驱动因素，可能导致干旱或洪水等极端天气事件。最近，我们开发了几种早期 ENSO 预报的统计方法，特别是厄尔尼诺相。基于气候网络的方法可以预测厄尔尼诺事件的发生或大约提前一年不发生[1]。基于复杂性的方法还可以预测即将到来的厄尔尼诺事件在发生前一年的强度[2]。此外，我们还开发了预测厄尔尼诺现象类型（东太平洋或中太平洋）的方法[3]以及拉尼娜现象和中性事件的概率预测方法[4]，也是在事件发生前的日历年年底进行。在这里，我们介绍了这些方法对 2026 年的预测。气候网络和基于复杂性的方法并未提供今年的一致信号。综合预测表明，中性事件比厄尔尼诺事件更有可能发生。如果厄尔尼诺现象在 2026 年发生，基于复杂性的方法将预测震级为 $0.84\pm0.36${\deg}C 的较弱事件。
