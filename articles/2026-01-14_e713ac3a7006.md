## Abstract
arXiv:2601.08298v1 Announce Type: new Abstract: A major unaddressed challenge for food science remains the accurate characterisation of contents in sealed containers with a non-invasive method. This issue is particularly pressing for tackling fraud in the red wine industry, valued at billions of dollars globally, where product authenticity, brand reputation, and consumer trust are paramount. Whilst many techniques exist for authenticating wine externally, to date performing accurate classification of the contents within unopened bottles remains elusive. Using only a single near-infrared optical excitation source operating at a wavelength of 785 nm, in combination with a bespoke geometry to circumvent the confounding signal of the glass, we demonstrate that through-bottle fluorescence spectra can distinguish between twenty different red wines in their original, intact bottles. All twenty wine bottles were correctly classified with linear discriminant analysis (LDA) and principal component analysis (PCA) revealed strong varietal grouping. This non-invasive and rapid technique has the potential to enable on-site, routine wine authentication to combat the growing issue of wine fraud. The geometry itself is applicable across multiple fields for the analysis of other high-value products through their packaging, where authenticity verification is critical.

arXiv:2601.08298v1 公告类型：新 摘要：食品科学面临的一个尚未解决的主要挑战仍然是采用非侵入性方法准确表征密封容器中的内容物。这个问题对于解决红酒行业的欺诈问题尤为紧迫，该行业在全球价值数十亿美元，产品的真实性、品牌声誉和消费者信任至关重要。虽然存在许多从外部鉴定葡萄酒的技术，但迄今为止，对未开封的瓶子内的内容物进行准确分类仍然难以实现。仅使用波长为 785 nm 的单个近红外光学激发源，结合定制的几何形状来规避玻璃的混杂信号，我们证明穿瓶荧光光谱可以区分原始、完整瓶子中的 20 种不同的红酒。通过线性判别分析 (LDA) 和主成分分析 (PCA)，所有 20 个葡萄酒瓶都被正确分类，显示出很强的品种分组。这种非侵入式快速技术有可能实现现场例行葡萄酒认证，以应对日益严重的葡萄酒欺诈问题。几何形状本身适用于多个领域，通过包装分析其他高价值产品，其中真实性验证至关重要。
