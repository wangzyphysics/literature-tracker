## Abstract
arXiv:2602.11728v1 Announce Type: cross Abstract: The superconducting Transition-Edge Sensor (TES) is a critical technology for next-generation X-ray spectrometers, known for its exceptional energy resolution. In the last decade, TESs based on AlMn alloy films have been extensively used in several cosmic microwave background (CMB) experiments. The advantages of simple fabrication process and easily tunable critical temperature make them an alternative to bilayer TESs. However, they have rarely been applied to X-ray detection until now. We developed an annular AlMn TES for X-ray detection and tested it in a dilution refrigerator with a Superconducting Quantum Interference Device (SQUID) amplifier, achieving an Full Width at Half Maximum (FWHM) of 12.1 +- 0.3 eV at 17.48 keV. To the best of our knowledge, this is the first demonstration of an AlMn TES achieving an energy resolution below 0.1%, highlighting its potential for high-resolution X-ray detection.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2602.11728v1 公告类型：cross 摘要：超导过渡边缘传感器 (TES) 是下一代 X 射线光谱仪的关键技术，以其卓越的能量分辨率而闻名。在过去的十年中，基于AlMn合金薄膜的TES已广泛应用于多个宇宙微波背景（CMB）实验中。简单的制造工艺和易于调节的临界温度的优点使其成为双层 TES 的替代品。然而，迄今为止，它们很少应用于 X 射线检测。我们开发了一种用于 X 射线检测的环形 AlMn TES，并在带有超导量子干涉器件 (SQUID) 放大器的稀释制冷机中对其进行了测试，在 17.48 keV 下实现了 12.1 ± 0.3 eV 的半高全宽 (FWHM)。据我们所知，这是 AlMn TES 首次演示能量分辨率低于 0.1%，凸显了其高分辨率 X 射线检测的潜力。
