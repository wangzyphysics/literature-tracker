## Abstract
This purely computational study reports the in silico design and preliminary evaluation of a novel μ-opioid receptor agonist scaffold with potential dual modulation of calcium channels.[1,2,3]. Docking studies were performed on the deacetylated (active) form of the ligand, revealing favorable binding to μ-opioid receptor residues critical for activation, including hydrophobic contacts with TRP242, ILE245, VAL249, and key hydrogen bonds and a stabilizing salt bridge with HIS246 [1,2]. Interaction profiling against a voltage-gated calcium channel (8IF3) showed multiple hydrophobic and hydrogen-bond contacts, suggesting potential dual-target engagement [3,4]. Physicochemical and ADMET analysis identified limited lipophilicity, raising concerns for blood–brain barrier penetration [5]. To address this, a prodrug approach via selective acetylation of hydroxyl groups is proposed to enhance CNS penetration; enzymatic hydrolysis in situ is expected to release the active, deacetylated ligand capable of receptor engagement [5,6]. These results establish a foundation for further computational and experimental studies toward multifunctional analgesic agents [1,2,3].

## 摘要（中文）
这项纯粹的计算研究报告了一种新型 μ-阿片受体激动剂支架的计算机设计和初步评估，该支架具有钙通道的潜在双重调节作用。[1,2,3]。对配体的脱乙酰化（活性）形式进行了对接研究，揭示了与对激活至关重要的 μ-阿片受体残基的有利结合，包括与 TRP242、ILE245、VAL249 的疏水接触、关键氢键以及与 HIS246 的稳定盐桥 [1,2]。针对电压门控钙通道 (8IF3) 的相互作用分析显示了多个疏水性和氢键接触，表明潜在的双靶点参与 [3,4]。物理化学和 ADMET 分析发现亲脂性有限，引起了对血脑屏障渗透的担忧 [5]。为了解决这个问题，提出了一种通过选择性乙酰化羟基的前药方法来增强中枢神经系统的渗透；原位酶水解预计会释放出能够与受体结合的活性脱乙酰配体[5,6]。这些结果为多功能镇痛剂的进一步计算和实验研究奠定了基础[1,2,3]。
