## Abstract
arXiv:2602.06760v1 Announce Type: new Abstract: Vibrational spectroscopy provides a powerful connection between molecular dynamics (MD) simulations and experiment, but its routine use in condensed-phase systems remains limited. We introduce mimyria, a modular and automated framework that orchestrates electronic-structure reference calculations, trains atom-resolved machine-learning response models, and generates IR and Raman spectra from MD trajectories within a unified workflow. We introduce the polarizability gradient tensor (PGT) as a novel atom-resolved machine-learning target property for Raman spectroscopy, complementing the established atomic polar tensor (APT) for IR spectroscopy. As a necessary prerequisite, we demonstrate how both PGTs and APTs can accurately be computed from electronic-structure theory, validate them across formally equivalent derivative formulations, and thereby benchmark their numerical consistency. We then employ machine learning as an efficient surrogate to represent the validated APT and PGT response functions on aqueous benchmark systems. We validate the trained models directly at the level of the spectrum against explicit ab initio reference calculations and find that IR and Raman spectra converge with surprisingly small training sets. Moreover, spectral agreement improves more rapidly than the root-mean-square error (RMSE). While RMSE is straightforward to compute, statistically converged reference spectra are generally impractical to obtain, motivating the need to relate model-level errors to observable-level accuracy. By connecting these complementary error measures, we provide practical guidelines and early-stopping criteria for achieving sufficient spectral fidelity. By integrating response-tensor learning, automated training, and spectral-domain validation into a unified workflow, mimyria enables data-efficient and quantitatively reliable vibrational spectroscopy.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2602.06760v1 公告类型：新 摘要：振动光谱提供了分子动力学 (MD) 模拟和实验之间的强大联系，但其在凝聚相系统中的常规应用仍然有限。我们引入了 mimyria，这是一个模块化的自动化框架，它可以协调电子结构参考计算，训练原子解析的机器学习响应模型，并在统一的工作流程中从 MD 轨迹生成红外和拉曼光谱。我们引入极化率梯度张量（PGT）作为拉曼光谱的新型原子分辨机器学习目标属性，补充了已建立的红外光谱原子极性张量（APT）。作为必要的先决条件，我们演示了如何根据电子结构理论准确计算 PGT 和 APT，在形式上等效的导数公式中验证它们，从而对它们的数值一致性进行基准测试。然后，我们使用机器学习作为有效的替代方法来表示水性基准系统上经过验证的 APT 和 PGT 响应函数。我们直接在光谱级别上根据明确的从头开始参考计算验证训练模型，并发现红外和拉曼光谱与令人惊讶的小训练集收敛。此外，光谱一致性比均方根误差 (RMSE) 提高得更快。虽然 RMSE 计算起来很简单，但获得统计上收敛的参考谱通常是不切实际的，因此需要将模型级误差与可观测级精度联系起来。通过连接这些互补的误差测量，我们提供了实用指南和早期停止标准，以实现足够的光谱保真度。通过将响应张量学习、自动化训练和谱域验证集成到统一的工作流程中，mimyria 实现了数据高效且定量可靠的振动光谱。
