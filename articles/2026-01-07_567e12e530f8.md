## Abstract
The brain’s connectome1,2,3and the vascular system4are examples of physical networks whose tangible nature influences their structure, layout and, ultimately, their function. The material resources required to build and maintain these networks have inspired decades of research into wiring economy, offering testable predictions about their expected architecture and organization. Here we empirically explore the local branching geometry of a wide range of physical networks, uncovering systematic violations of the long-standing predictions of wiring minimization. This leads to the hypothesis that predicting the true material cost of physical networks requires us to account for their full three-dimensional geometry, resulting in a largely intractable optimization problem. We discover, however, an exact mapping of surface minimization onto high-dimensional Feynman diagrams in string theory5,6,7, predicting that, with increasing link thickness, a locally tree-like network undergoes a transition into configurations that can no longer be explained by length minimization. Specifically, surface minimization predicts the emergence of trifurcations and branching angles in excellent agreement with the local tree organization of physical networks across a wide range of application domains. Finally, we predict the existence of stable orthogonal sprouts, which are not only prevalent in real networks but also play a key functional role, improving synapse formation in the brain and nutrient access in plants and fungi.

## 摘要（中文）
大脑的连接组1、2、3和血管系统4是物理网络的例子，其有形本质影响其结构、布局，并最终影响其功能。构建和维护这些网络所需的物质资源激发了数十年来对布线经济的研究，提供了有关其预期架构和组织的可测试的预测。在这里，我们凭经验探索各种物理网络的局部分支几何形状，揭示了对布线最小化长期预测的系统性违反。这导致了这样的假设：预测物理网络的真实材料成本需要我们考虑其完整的三维几何形状，从而导致一个非常棘手的优化问题。然而，我们发现了弦理论中表面最小化到高维费曼图的精确映射5,6,7，预测随着链路厚度的增加，局部树状网络会转变为不再可以用长度最小化来解释的配置。具体来说，表面最小化预测了三叉和分支角度的出现，与广泛应用领域的物理网络的本地树组织非常一致。最后，我们预测稳定的正交芽的存在，它不仅在真实网络中普遍存在，而且发挥着关键的功能作用，改善大脑中的突触形成以及植物和真菌中的营养获取。
