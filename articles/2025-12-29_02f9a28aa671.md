## Abstract
Self-driving laboratories accelerate materials discovery by autonomously designing and executing experiments through closed-loop integration of robotics and artificial intelligence. Active learning with Gaussian processes has enabled efficient phase diagram mapping, reducing required measurements by approximately 80\% compared to conventional grid sampling. However, current approaches treat each system independently, discarding accumulated knowledge despite systematic similarities across related materials families. Here we introduce PhaseTransfer, a transfer learning framework that leverages previously characterized phase diagrams to accelerate mapping of new systems. PhaseTransfer combines a target model trained on current data with source models from related diagrams using spatially varying reliability assessments and focusing sampling on regions where transferred knowledge proves unreliable. Validation across synthetic benchmarks and experimental implementation on an autonomous microfluidic platform for biological condensate screening demonstrates in the order of 50\% reduction in sampling requirements compared to conventional active learning. By enabling knowledge reuse across investigations, transfer learning substantially enhances both the efficiency and generality of autonomous materials discovery.

## 摘要（中文）
自动驾驶实验室通过机器人和人工智能的闭环集成自主设计和执行实验，加速材料发现。使用高斯过程的主动学习实现了高效的相图映射，与传统的网格采样相比，所需的测量减少了约 80%。然而，当前的方法独立地对待每个系统，尽管相关材料家族之间存在系统相似性，但丢弃了积累的知识。在这里，我们介绍 PhaseTransfer，这是一种迁移学习框架，它利用先前表征的相图来加速新系统的映射。 PhaseTransfer 将基于当前数据训练的目标模型与相关图表中的源模型相结合，使用空间变化的可靠性评估并将采样集中在转移的知识被证明不可靠的区域。在用于生物冷凝物筛选的自主微流体平台上进行的综合基准验证和实验实施表明，与传统的主动学习相比，采样要求减少了 50%。通过在研究中实现知识重用，迁移学习大大提高了自主材料发现的效率和通用性。
