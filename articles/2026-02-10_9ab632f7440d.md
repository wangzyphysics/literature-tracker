## Abstract
arXiv:2512.09468v2 Announce Type: replace-cross Abstract: The association and dissociation of ion pairs in water are fundamental to physical chemistry, yet their reaction coordinates are complex, involving not only interionic distance but also solvent-mediated hydration structures. These processes are often represented by free-energy landscapes constructed from collective variables (CVs), such as interionic distance and water bridging structures; however, it remains uncertain whether such representations reliably capture the transition pathways between the two associated and dissociated states. In this study, we employ deep learning to identify reaction coordinates for NaCl ion pair association and dissociation in water, using the committor as a quantitative measure of progress along the transition pathway through the transition state. The solvent environment surrounding the ions is encoded through descriptors based on atom-centered symmetry functions (ACSFs), which serve as input variables for the neural network. In addition, an explainable artificial intelligence technique is applied to identify ACSFs that contribute to the reaction coordinate. A comparative analysis of their correlation with CVs representing water bridging structures, such as interionic water density and the number of water molecules coordinating both ions, further provides a molecular-level interpretation of the ion association-dissociation mechanism in water.

arXiv:2512.09468v2 公告类型：replace-cross 摘要：水中离子对的缔合和解离是物理化学的基础，但它们的反应坐标很复杂，不仅涉及离子距离，还涉及溶剂介导的水合结构。这些过程通常由集体变量（CV）构建的自由能景观来代表，例如离子间距离和水桥结构；然而，仍然不确定这种表示是否可靠地捕获了两种关联和分离状态之间的过渡路径。在这项研究中，我们采用深度学习来识别水中 NaCl 离子对缔合和解离的反应坐标，使用提交体作为沿着过渡态的过渡途径进展的定量测量。离子周围的溶剂环境通过基于原子中心对称函数 (ACSF) 的描述符进行编码，该函数充当神经网络的输入变量。此外，应用可解释的人工智能技术来识别对反应坐标有贡献的 ACSF。对它们与代表水桥结构的 CV（例如离子间水密度和协调两种离子的水分子数量）的相关性进行比较分析，进一步提供了水中离子缔合-解离机制的分子水平解释。
