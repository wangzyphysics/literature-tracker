## Abstract
arXiv:2601.02129v1 Announce Type: cross Abstract: The recent asteroseismic observations constitute a great challenge for rotating stellar evolution models, which predict too fast internal rotation rates when only hydrodynamic processes are included. This suggests the absence of one or several unidentified angular momentum transport processes in these models. Transport by large-scale and strong magnetic fields in the radiative zone is a promising candidate to explain the observations. While these fields may have a fossil origin, a dynamo driven by the Tayler instability in a shear flow constitute a primary mechanism to form the necessary magnetic fields. Despite recent numerical studies, this mechanism remains poorly known. Motivated by this, we investigate the Tayler-Spruit dynamo through a new set of three-dimensional direct numerical simulations. We model the radiative zone as a Boussinesq stably stratified fluid whose differential rotation is maintained by a volumetric body force. We report for the first time the existence of two bistable dynamo branches, which mainly differ by the magnetic field location (near the equator and the polar axis). While the equatorial branch is driven by the magnetorotational instability, we mainly investigate the newly identified polar branch, which is driven by the Tayler instability. We show that this branch can still operate and transport angular momentum efficiently in a very strong stratification regime. We extract new scaling laws for the different magnetic field components, transports processes, and the minimum shear to trigger the Tayler instability-driven dynamo. Finally, we roughly constrain the signature of the generated magnetic fields on asteroseismic modes propagating in main-sequence and evolved stars. Thus, our results fosters new studies using stellar evolution models including our prescriptions and the search of asteroseismic signals impacted by large-scale azimuthal magnetic fields.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2601.02129v1 公告类型：cross 摘要：最近的星震观测对旋转恒星演化模型提出了巨大的挑战，这些模型在仅包括流体动力学过程时预测的内旋转速率过快。这表明这些模型中不存在一个或多个未识别的角动量传输过程。辐射区中大范围强磁场的传输是解释观测结果的一个有希望的候选者。虽然这些磁场可能具有化石起源，但由剪切流中泰勒不稳定性驱动的发电机构成了形成必要磁场的主要机制。尽管最近进行了数值研究，但这种机制仍然鲜为人知。受此启发，我们通过一组新的三维直接数值模拟研究了泰勒-斯普鲁特发电机。我们将辐射区建模为 Boussinesq 稳定分层流体，其差动旋转由体积力维持。我们首次报告了两个双稳态发电机分支的存在，其主要区别在于磁场位置（赤道和极轴附近）。虽然赤道支是由磁旋转不稳定性驱动的，但我们主要研究新发现的极支，它是由泰勒不稳定性驱动的。我们表明，该分支仍然可以在非常强的分层状态下有效地运行和传输角动量。我们为不同的磁场分量、传输过程和最小剪切提取新的标度定律，以触发泰勒不稳定驱动的发电机。最后，我们粗略地约束了在主序和演化恒星中传播的星震模式上产生的磁场的特征。因此，我们的结果促进了使用恒星演化模型的新研究，包括我们的处方和对受大尺度方位磁场影响的星震信号的搜索。
