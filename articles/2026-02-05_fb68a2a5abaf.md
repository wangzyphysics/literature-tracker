## Abstract
arXiv:2602.04829v1 Announce Type: new Abstract: We propose a computational framework for modeling opinion dynamics in electoral competitions that combines two realistic features: voter memory and exogenous shocks. The population is represented by a fully-connected network of agents, each holding a binary opinion that reflects support for one of two candidates. First, inspired by the classical voter model, we introduce a memory-dependent opinion update: each agent's probability of adopting a neighbor's stance depends on how many times they agreed with that neighbor in the agent's past $m$ states, promoting inertia and resistance to change. Second, we define an electoral shock as an abrupt external influence acting uniformly over all agents during a finite interval $[t_0, t_0+\Delta t]$, favoring one candidate by switching opinions with probability $p_s$, representing the impact of extraordinary events such as political scandals, impactful speeches, or sudden news. We explore how the strength and duration of the shock, in conjunction with memory length, influence the transient and stationary properties of the model, as well as the candidates' advantage. Our findings reveal a rich dynamical behavior: memory slows down convergence and enhances system resilience, whereas shocks of sufficient intensity and duration can abruptly realign collective preferences, particularly when occurring close to the election date. Conversely, for long memory lengths or large election horizons, shock effects are dampened or delayed, depending on their timing. These results offer insights into why some sudden political events reshape electoral outcomes while others fade under strong individual inertia. Finally, a qualitative comparison with real electoral shocks reported in opinion polls illustrates how the model captures the competition between voter inertia and abrupt external events observed in actual elections.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2602.04829v1 公告类型：新 摘要：我们提出了一种计算框架，用于对选举竞争中的意见动态进行建模，该框架结合了两个现实特征：选民记忆和外生冲击。人口由完全连接的代理网络代表，每个代理都持有二元意见，反映对两名候选人之一的支持。首先，受经典选民模型的启发，我们引入了一种依赖于记忆的意见更新：每个智能体采用邻居立场的概率取决于他们在过去 $m$ 状态中与该邻居达成一致的次数，从而促进了惰性和对变革的抵制。其次，我们将选举冲击定义为在有限时间间隔 $[t_0, t_0+\Delta t]$ 内对所有代理人统一作用的突然外部影响，通过以 $p_s$ 概率改变观点来支持一位候选人，代表政治丑闻、有影响力的演讲或突发新闻等异常事件的影响。我们探讨了冲击的强度和持续时间以及记忆长度如何影响模型的瞬态和静态特性以及候选人的优势。我们的研究结果揭示了丰富的动态行为：记忆减慢了收敛速度并增强了系统的弹性，而足够强度和持续时间的冲击可以突然重新调整集体偏好，特别是在临近选举日期时。相反，对于较长的记忆长度或较大的选举范围，冲击效应会被减弱或延迟，具体取决于其发生的时间。这些结果让我们深入了解为什么一些突发的政治事件会重塑选举结果，而另一些事件则会在强烈的个人惯性下逐渐消失。最后，与民意调查中报告的真实选举冲击进行定性比较，说明该模型如何捕捉选民惯性与实际选举中观察到的突发外部事件之间的竞争。
