## Abstract
arXiv:2601.01690v1 Announce Type: new Abstract: The rapid scaling of deep neural networks comes at the cost of unsustainable power consumption. While optical neural networks offer an alternative, their capabilities remain constrained by the lack of efficient optical nonlinearities. To address this, we propose an all-optical deep learning architecture by embedding quantum emitters in inverse-designed nanophotonic structures. Due to their saturability, quantum emitters exhibit exceptionally strong nonlinearity compared with conventional materials. Using physics-aware training, we demonstrate that the proposed architecture can solve complex tasks, including nonlinear classification and reinforcement learning, which have not been realized in all-optical neural networks. To enable fair comparison across different platforms, we introduce a framework that quantitatively links nonlinearity to a network's expressive power. Analysis shows that our quantum activation, operating below nW/{\mu}m^2 intensity, reduces the power budget by seven orders of magnitude. System-level estimates show that the optical power required for large language models scales sublinearly with model size, enabling watt-level operation. Our results indicate that quantum nanophotonics provides a route toward sustainable AI inference.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2601.01690v1 公告类型：新 摘要：深度神经网络的快速扩展是以不可持续的功耗为代价的。虽然光学神经网络提供了一种替代方案，但它们的能力仍然受到缺乏有效光学非线性的限制。为了解决这个问题，我们提出了一种全光学深度学习架构，将量子发射器嵌入逆向设计的纳米光子结构中。由于其饱和性，与传统材料相比，量子发射器表现出异常强的非线性。通过物理感知训练，我们证明了所提出的架构可以解决复杂的任务，包括非线性分类和强化学习，这些任务在全光神经网络中尚未实现。为了实现不同平台之间的公平比较，我们引入了一个框架，该框架将非线性与网络的表达能力定量联系起来。分析表明，我们的量子激活在低于 nW/{\mu}m^2 强度的情况下运行，可将功率预算降低七个数量级。系统级估计表明，大型语言模型所需的光功率与模型大小呈次线性关系，从而实现瓦特级操作。我们的结果表明，量子纳米光子学为可持续人工智能推理提供了一条途径。
