## Abstract
arXiv:2602.08648v1 Announce Type: new Abstract: Understanding the thermal behaviour of superconducting accelerator magnets is essential to ensure stable and reliable operation. This work presents an extension of the Finite Element Quench Simulator (FiQuS) Multipole module to include collar and pole regions of accelerator magnets, which influences the overall thermal response. A thermal thin shell approximation (TSA), which is shown to be effective in previous works, is employed to model thermal insulation layers efficiently, replacing an insulation surface mesh. The main novelty of this work lies in the development of a method to model the thermal connection between the magnet winding and the collar and pole regions via the TSA. To assess the accuracy and computational efficiency of the novel method, temperature and field variations are computed for a current ramp-up scenario. The thermal solution is coupled to a fully resolved magnetodynamic solution to capture the interaction between thermal and electromagnetic behaviour. The results obtained with the TSA are then compared to classical finite element (FE) solutions with explicitly meshed insulation domains. The TSA predicts the maximum temperature within 2-4% of the reference solution while substantially reducing mesh complexity and achieving up to a 5 times speed-up in computation time. While the TSA has traditionally been employed for short-duration quench simulations with high heat fluxes between magnet turns, these results demonstrate its reliability and efficiency for current ramp scenarios with low heat fluxes, significantly expanding its application range beyond what has been previously reported in the literature. To illustrate potential applications of this new functionality, conduction cooling through the collar region is studied, comparing different cooling configurations and collar materials.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2602.08648v1 公告类型：新 摘要：了解超导加速器磁体的热行为对于确保稳定可靠的运行至关重要。这项工作提出了有限元淬火模拟器 (FiQuS) 多极模块的扩展，包括加速器磁体的轴环和磁极区域，这会影响整体热响应。热薄壳近似（TSA）在之前的工作中被证明是有效的，用于有效地模拟隔热层，取代隔热表面网格。这项工作的主要新颖之处在于开发了一种通过 TSA 对磁体绕组与轴环和磁极区域之间的热连接进行建模的方法。为了评估新方法的准确性和计算效率，计算了当前加速场景的温度和场变化。热解决方案与完全解析的磁动力学解决方案耦合，以捕获热行为和电磁行为之间的相互作用。然后将 TSA 获得的结果与具有明确网格绝缘域的经典有限元 (FE) 解决方案进行比较。 TSA 预测的最高温度与参考解的误差在 2-4% 范围内，同时大幅降低网格复杂性并实现计算时间最多 5 倍的加速。虽然 TSA 传统上用于磁体匝间高热通量的短时失超模拟，但这些结果证明了其在低热通量的电流斜坡场景中的可靠性和效率，显着扩展了其应用范围，超出了之前文献报道的范围。为了说明这一新功能的潜在应用，研究了通过轴环区域的传导冷却，比较了不同的冷却配置和轴环材料。
