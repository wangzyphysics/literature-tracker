## Abstract
Author(s): Junmo Jeon, Harukuni Ikeda, and Shiro Sakai Here, the authors show that the conventional belief that any disorder localizes all states in one dimension breaks down when long-wavelength fluctuations in the potential are suppressed, a property known as hyperuniformity. In tight-binding chains, strongly hyperuniform disorder interpolates between random and periodic potentials, inducing a sharp delocalization transition and the emergence of mobility edges. This shows that reducing large-scale irregularities alone is sufficient to control localization and transport in low-dimensional systems. [Phys. Rev. B 113, L020201] Published Thu Jan 15, 2026
