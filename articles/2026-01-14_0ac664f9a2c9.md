## Abstract
arXiv:2601.08720v1 Announce Type: new Abstract: Political corruption is inherently an affiliation process linking agents to corruption cases; yet it is often studied via one-mode projections that connect co-offenders within the same scandal, implying a loss of information that potentially confounds properties of agents and cases. Here, we adopt a bipartite representation to analyze datasets of corruption scandals in Brazil and Spain spanning nearly three decades. By tracking the temporal growth of these networks, we quantify density and redundancy measures to capture partner reuse and co-occurrence across cases. Networks in both countries become progressively sparser over time, and agent redundancy is systematically higher than case redundancy, indicating a small cadre of recidivists who recombine largely with novice partners rather than forming durable co-offending ties. These networks exhibit near-exponential degree distributions, reflecting low recidivism and likely high coordination costs and secrecy constraints of large-scale scandals. Our bipartite view further reveals a moderate cross-mode disassortative degree mixing between agents and cases, with high-degree agents distributing their activity across small cases and large scandals mainly comprising low-degree participants. Finally, identifying atypical individuals within the bipartite structure reveals criminal trajectories marked by a gradual rise in network embeddedness that can appear ordinary in agent-projected networks.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2601.08720v1 公告类型：新 摘要：政治腐败本质上是一个将代理人与腐败案件联系起来的隶属过程；然而，它通常是通过单一模式预测来研究的，该预测将同一丑闻中的同案犯联系起来，这意味着信息的丢失可能会混淆特工和案件的性质。在这里，我们采用双边表示法来分析巴西和西班牙近三十年的腐败丑闻数据集。通过跟踪这些网络的时间增长，我们量化密度和冗余措施，以捕获合作伙伴的重用和跨案例的共现。随着时间的推移，这两个国家的犯罪网络逐渐变得稀疏，特工冗余系统性地高于案件冗余，这表明一小部分惯犯主要与新手伙伴重新组合，而不是形成持久的同案关系。这些网络表现出接近指数的程度分布，反映出累犯率较低，而且可能存在较高的协调成本以及大规模丑闻的保密限制。我们的二分观点进一步揭示了代理人和案件之间适度的跨模式不协调程度混合，高级别代理人将其活动分布在小案件和主要由低级别参与者组成的大型丑闻中。最后，识别二分结构中的非典型个体揭示了以网络嵌入性逐渐上升为标志的犯罪轨迹，这在代理投影网络中可能显得很常见。
