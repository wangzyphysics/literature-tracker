## Abstract
arXiv:2602.04481v1 Announce Type: new Abstract: The dynamics of herd immunity depend crucially on the interaction between collective social behavior and disease transmission, but the role of heterogeneity in this context frequently remains unclear. Here, we dissect this co-evolutionary feedback by coupling a public goods game with an epidemic model on complex networks, including multiplex and real-world networks. Our results reveals a dichotomy in how heterogeneity shapes outcomes. We demonstrate that structural heterogeneity in social networks acts as a powerful catalyst for cooperation and disease suppression. This emergent effect is driven by highly connected hubs who, facing amplified personal risk, adopt protective strategies out of self-interest. In contrast, heterogeneity in individual infection costs proves detrimental, undermining cooperation and amplifying the epidemic. This creates a ``weakest link'' problem, where individuals with low perceived risk act as persistent free-riders and disease reservoirs, degrading the collective response. Our findings establish that heterogeneity is a double-edged sword: its impact is determined by whether it creates an asymmetry of influence (leverage points) or an asymmetry of motivation (weakest links), recommending disease intervention policies that facilitate cooperative transition in hubs (strengthening the leverage point) and homogenize incentives to weakest links.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2602.04481v1 公告类型：新 摘要：群体免疫的动态很大程度上取决于集体社会行为与疾病传播之间的相互作用，但异质性在这种情况下的作用往往仍不清楚。在这里，我们通过将公共物品博弈与复杂网络（包括多重网络和现实世界网络）上的流行病模型结合起来，剖析了这种共同进化反馈。我们的结果揭示了异质性如何影响结果的二分法。我们证明，社交网络中的结构异质性是合作和疾病抑制的强大催化剂。这种新兴效应是由高度互联的枢纽驱动的，这些枢纽面临着放大的个人风险，出于自身利益而采取保护策略。相比之下，个体感染成本的异质性被证明是有害的，会破坏合作并加剧疫情。这就产生了“最薄弱的环节”问题，即感知风险较低的个人充当持续的搭便车者和疾病储存库，从而降低了集体应对能力。我们的研究结果表明，异质性是一把双刃剑：其影响取决于是否造成影响力不对称（杠杆点）或动机不对称（最薄弱环节）、推荐促进中心合作转型（加强杠杆点）和对最薄弱环节的激励同质化的疾病干预政策。
