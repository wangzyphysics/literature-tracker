## Abstract
arXiv:2601.08243v1 Announce Type: new Abstract: Topology provides a unifying framework for understanding robust transport through protected edge states arising from nontrivial wavenumber topology. Extending these concepts to active matter, however, remains largely unexplored experimentally, with realizations limited to systems composed of chiral active particles. Here, we realize topological edge states in dense bacterial suspension, which represents a prototypical active matter system, using microfabricated geometrical structures with nontrivial wavenumber topology. Inspired by previous theoretical studies, we constructed a directional kagome network composed of ratchet-shaped channels that induce unidirectional bacterial flow. In this network, we found clear edge localization of bacterial density. A steady-state analysis based on the bacterial transport model and experimentally measured velocity field reveals how the characteristic collective flow generates edge localization. The model also uncovers the topological origin of the observed edge states. By tuning the geometry of the microfabricated networks, we identified directional channel design and network chirality as the key design features essential for the emergence of the edge state. Our results pave the way for establishing a control and design principle of topological transport in such active matter systems.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2601.08243v1 公告类型：新 摘要：拓扑提供了一个统一的框架，用于通过非平凡波数拓扑产生的受保护边缘状态来理解鲁棒传输。然而，将这些概念扩展到活性物质，在很大程度上仍未通过实验进行探索，其实现仅限于由手性活性粒子组成的系统。在这里，我们使用具有非平凡波数拓扑的微加工几何结构，实现了致密细菌悬浮液中的拓扑边缘态，这代表了典型的活性物质系统。受先前理论研究的启发，我们构建了一个由棘轮形通道组成的定向戈薇网络，可诱导单向细菌流动。在这个网络中，我们发现细菌密度的边缘定位清晰。基于细菌传输模型和实验测量的速度场的稳态分析揭示了特征集体流如何产生边缘定位。该模型还揭示了观察到的边缘态的拓扑起源。通过调整微制造网络的几何形状，我们确定定向通道设计和网络手性是边缘态出现所必需的关键设计特征。我们的结果为建立此类活性物质系统中拓扑输运的控制和设计原理铺平了道路。
