## Abstract
arXiv:2601.08601v1 Announce Type: cross Abstract: This Ph.D. thesis reports on progress in rigorously establishing hydrodynamic principles from the microscopic Hamiltonian dynamics of quantum many-body systems in a general, non-model-specific manner. Using the C*-algebra framework of statistical mechanics, we treat systems directly in the thermodynamic limit, primarily focusing on quantum lattice models where tools such as Lieb-Robinson bounds yield rigorous statements. We thus provide a proof-of-principle that large-scale behaviours can indeed be seen as emerging from microscopic dynamics, with mathematical proof. We first report on ergodicity results in short-range models with exponentially decaying or finite-range interactions. We show that time-averaged observables converge to their ensemble averages and decorrelate from all other observables almost everywhere within the light-cone defined by Lieb-Robinson bounds. This relaxation property indicates the loss of information at large scales, from which we prove a Boltzmann-Gibbs principle: at the Euler scaling limit of large time and distance, observables project onto hydrodynamic modes (extensive conserved quantities), within correlation functions. These results hold independently of microscopic details, capturing the physical idea that such details are lost at large space-time scales. Regarding finer scales of hydrodynamics, we discuss rigorous lower bounds on the strength of diffusion. We establish a general result on the clustering of n-th order connected correlations within C* dynamical systems. These results are applied to obtain a strictly positive lower bound on the diffusion constant of chaotic open quantum spin chains with nearest-neighbor interactions. This thesis underlines the universality of hydrodynamic principles, provides a framework for establishing them rigorously, and sets the stage for future progress toward the goal of proving the hydrodynamic equations.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2601.08601v1 公告类型：交叉 摘要：本博士。论文报告了以一般的、非模型特定的方式从量子多体系统的微观哈密顿动力学严格建立流体动力学原理的进展。使用统计力学的 C* 代数框架，我们直接在热力学极限下处理系统，主要关注量子晶格模型，其中 Lieb-Robinson 界限等工具可以产生严格的陈述。因此，我们提供了一个原理证明，即大规模行为确实可以被视为从微观动力学中产生，并有数学证明。我们首先报告具有指数衰减或有限范围相互作用的短程模型的遍历性结果。我们证明，时间平均可观测量收敛于它们的整体平均值，并且在李布-罗宾逊边界定义的光锥内几乎所有地方都与所有其他可观测量去相关。这种松弛性质表明大尺度上的信息丢失，由此我们证明了玻尔兹曼-吉布斯原理：在大时间和距离的欧拉标度极限下，可观测量在相关函数内投射到流体动力学模式（广泛的守恒量）。这些结果与微观细节无关，捕捉到了这些细节在大时空尺度上丢失的物理概念。关于更精细的流体动力学尺度，我们讨论了扩散强度的严格下限。我们建立了 C* 动态系统内 n 阶关联相关性聚类的一般结果。这些结果用于获得具有最近邻相互作用的混沌开放量子自旋链的扩散常数的严格正下界。本文强调了流体动力学原理的普遍性，提供了严格建立流体动力学原理的框架，并为未来实现证明流体动力学方程的目标奠定了基础。
