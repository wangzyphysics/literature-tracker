## Abstract
arXiv:2601.09009v1 Announce Type: new Abstract: Molecular simulations of interfacial polar media routinely employ periodic boundary conditions parallel to the interface. We show that this geometry injects a uniform plane mode ($q_{\parallel}=0$) that converts the plane-averaged electrostatic potential into a cumulative sum of plane-dipole increments, a random walk in $z$. Consequently, the variance of the plane-averaged potential grows linearly with depth in semi-infinite slabs and follows a parabolic Brownian-bridge profile in finite cells with both ends fixed, with an amplitude inversely proportional to the cell's lateral area. Hence, at any finite area, the variance diverges with slab thickness, a 2D-mode catastrophe. In contrast, a pure 1D chain (no lateral replication) and a fully 3D, nonperiodic medium both exhibit bounded fluctuations that saturate with distance. The mechanism is generic to any solver of Poisson's equation with 2D periodicity, so the apparent growth and ultimate divergence in potential fluctuation are artifacts of boundary conditions rather than material response, and we provide a simple scaling criterion for choosing slab sizes that keeps these artifacts under quantitative control.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2601.09009v1 公告类型：新 摘要：界面极性介质的分子模拟通常采用平行于界面的周期性边界条件。我们证明，该几何形状注入了均匀平面模式 ($q_{\parallel}=0$)，将平面平均静电势转换为平面偶极子增量的累积和，即 $z$ 中的随机游走。因此，平面平均电势的方差在半无限平板中随深度线性增长，并在两端固定的有限单元中遵循抛物线布朗桥轮廓，其幅度与单元的横向面积成反比。因此，在任何有限区域，方差随板厚而发散，这是一种二维模式灾难。相比之下，纯 1D 链（无横向复制）和完全 3D、非周期性介质都表现出随着距离而饱和的有界波动。该机制对于具有 2D 周期性的泊松方程的任何求解器都是通用的，因此潜在波动的表观增长和最终发散是边界条件的伪影而不是材料响应，并且我们提供了一个简单的缩放标准来选择板尺寸，以将这些伪影保持在定量控制之下。
