## Abstract
Synthetic hydrogels rarely form the porous, cell-permissive architectures required for effective tissue regeneration. Here, we report a poly(ethylene glycol) (PEG) sponge hydrogel whose macroporous network is pre-programmed through gel–gel phase separation (GGPS) and stabilized by freeze–thaw processing. This mechanism generates fully interconnected pores up to 150 μm in size, larger than those typically achieved in conventional PEG hydrogels, while preserving mechanical resilience and eliminating swelling. The resulting architecture supports infiltration and three-dimensional expansion of human mesenchymal stem cells and directs lineage specification through pore-geometry–dependent mechanotransduction. When functionalized with osteoinductive cues, the sponge promotes robust regeneration of critical-sized cranial defects. By coupling polymer phase behavior with precise structural control, this strategy establishes a scalable route to architecturally defined synthetic scaffolds for regenerative medicine.

## 摘要（中文）
合成水凝胶很少形成有效组织再生所需的多孔、细胞允许的结构。在这里，我们报道了一种聚乙二醇（PEG）海绵水凝胶，其大孔网络通过凝胶-凝胶相分离（GGPS）进行预编程，并通过冻融处理进行稳定。这种机制产生完全互连的孔径高达 150 μm，比传统 PEG 水凝胶通常实现的孔径更大，同时保持机械弹性并消除膨胀。由此产生的结构支持人类间充质干细胞的浸润和三维扩张，并通过孔隙几何依赖的机械转导指导谱系规范。当用骨诱导信号进行功能化时，海绵可促进临界尺寸颅骨缺损的强劲再生。通过将聚合物相行为与精确的结构控制相结合，该策略为再生医学的建筑定义的合成支架建立了一条可扩展的途径。
