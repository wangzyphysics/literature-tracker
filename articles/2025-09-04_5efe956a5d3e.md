## Abstract
Author(s): B. Acharya et al. Approximately 90% of the stars in the Milky Way are on the main sequence, fusing hydrogen into helium through a network of nuclear reactions. This includes the nearest star, our Sun. A precise understanding of hydrogen burning is crucial to predicting its luminosity, neutrino production, and helioseismology. This review describes the theoretical and experimental work of the last decade that has advanced our understanding of the nuclear physics of hydrogen burning. It describes the plasma and atomic physics that influences the solar environment in which the nuclear reactions take place, as well as the diagnostics probes—including solar neutrinos and helioseismology—that allow us to test our resulting model of the solar interior.
