## Abstract
There is increasing concern around the potential environmental and public health impacts of tire wear transformation products even as significant data gaps exist on the environmental fate and potential ecological and human health impacts. Here, we provide a preliminary review and synthesis of existing information concerning five specific tire wear transformation products, including 6PPDQ, 4 ADPA, two newly identified constituents in snow and wastewater, TP 249 and TP 259_2, as well as the parent compound, 6PPD. We conduct a systematic review, relying on data sources and analytical models ranging from in silico to in vitro to in vivo, including read-across and quantitative structure activity relationships (QSAR), and data gap filling techniques based on data from chemically similar analogues. We summarize the available data and modeling results for these constituents to identify commonalities and differences across them and to evaluate whether these methods provide insights into potential behavior in the environment and toxicity mechanisms. Assay results are available from the US Environmental Protection Agency Computational Toxicology dashboard for 6PPD and 4 ADPA and show that the majority of activated assays relate to cell viability and cycling, cytotoxicity, and cell proliferation and energetics, consistent with targeted in vivo and in vitro studies from the literature. Generalized read-across and other QSAR-based models for 6PPDQ, TP 249, and TP 259_2 show very few or no statistically-significant predictions, but do suggest a set of assays in common across all constituents with consistent cellular and organ targets, including nuclear receptor activation and cell energetics across genes largely expressed in the kidney and liver, supporting limited in vivo and in vitro data from the literature. No clear conclusions can be drawn around observed differences in acute mortality and sensitivity across fish species following exposure to 6PPDQ, but likely cellular and subcellular targets suggest activation of genes related to DNA repair, cell proliferation, and oxidative stress. Overall, existing data are insufficient to make conclusive evaluations of the potential impact of these tire wear particles to human health or the environment. Additional, targeted studies such as specific in vitro tests together with limited in vivo testing, and emerging transcriptomic methods as proposed by the US Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) could help fill these data gaps.

## 摘要（中文）
尽管在环境命运以及潜在的生态和人类健康影响方面存在重大数据差距，但人们越来越关注轮胎磨损转化产品对环境和公共健康的潜在影响。在这里，我们对五种特定轮胎磨损转化产品的现有信息进行了初步审查和综合，包括 6PPDQ、4 ADPA、雪和废水中新发现的两种成分 TP 249 和 TP 259_2，以及母体化合物 6PPD。我们依靠从计算机到体外再到体内的数据源和分析模型进行系统评价，包括跨读和定量结构活性关系（QSAR），以及基于化学相似类似物数据的数据间隙填充技术。我们总结了这些成分的可用数据和建模结果，以识别它们之间的共性和差异，并评估这些方法是否可以深入了解环境中的潜在行为和毒性机制。美国环境保护局计算毒理学仪表板提供了 6PPD 和 4 ADPA 的检测结果，表明大多数激活检测与细胞活力和循环、细胞毒性以及细胞增殖和能量学相关，与文献中的体内和体外靶向研究一致。 6PPDQ、TP 249 和 TP 259_2 的广义跨读和其他基于 QSAR 的模型显示很少或没有统计显着性预测，但确实提出了一组针对所有成分的共同分析，具有一致的细胞和器官目标，包括主要在肾脏和肝脏中表达的基因的核受体激活和细胞能量学，支持文献中有限的体内和体外数据。对于暴露于 6PPDQ 后不同鱼类急性死亡率和敏感性的观察差异，尚无明确结论，但可能的细胞和亚细胞目标表明与 DNA 修复、细胞增殖和氧化应激相关的基因被激活。总体而言，现有数据不足以对这些轮胎磨损颗粒对人类健康或环境的潜在影响做出结论性评估。另外，有针对性的研究，例如特定的体外测试和有限的体内测试，以及美国环境保护署 (EPA) 提出的新兴转录组学方法，可以帮助填补这些数据空白。
