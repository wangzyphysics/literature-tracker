## Abstract
arXiv:2512.17948v2 Announce Type: replace Abstract: This volume, \textbf{Physicists Are Still Joking}, serves as a definitive almanac of scientific humor spanning sixty years. It traces the evolution of professional folklore across geopolitical divides and technological eras. \textbf{Part I} restores the classic 1966 anthology \textbf{Physicists Joke}, which originally served as a window for Soviet scientists into the best traditions of Western scientific humor; it consists primarily of articles translated from English, here meticulously restored to their original wording. \textbf{Part II} presents the 1992 sequel, \textbf{Physicists Keep Joking}, which captures the shift toward an original, introspective Russian scientific folklore born during the end of the Cold War and the collapse of the Soviet Union. \textbf{Part III: Still Joking} explores the modern digital age, compiling contemporary science humor from physics, astronomy, biology, computer science and AI research. While the tools of science have evolved from slide rules to neural networks, the tradition of skeptical, self-referential wit remains a constant. Spanning from the "Golden Age" of vacuum tubes to the era of AI and Large Language Models, this collection documents the enduring ability of scientists to laugh at the universe and themselves.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2512.17948v2 公告类型：替换 摘要：这本书，\textbf{物理学家还在开玩笑}，是跨越六十年的科学幽默的权威年鉴。它追溯了专业民间传说跨越地缘政治分歧和技术时代的演变。 \textbf{第一部分}恢复了 1966 年的经典选集 \textbf{物理学家笑话}，它最初是苏联科学家了解西方科学幽默最佳传统的窗口；它主要由英文翻译的文章组成，这里精心恢复了原来的措辞。 \textbf{第二部分}介绍了 1992 年的续集 \textbf{物理学家继续开玩笑}，它捕捉到了冷战结束和苏联解体期间诞生的原创、内省的俄罗斯科学民间传说的转变。 \textbf{第三部分：还在开玩笑}探索现代数字时代，从物理学、天文学、生物学、计算机科学和人工智能研究中汇编当代科学幽默。尽管科学工具已经从计算尺发展到神经网络，但怀疑、自我参照的智慧传统仍然保持不变。从真空管的“黄金时代”到人工智能和大型语言模型的时代，这本书记录了科学家嘲笑宇宙和他们自己的持久能力。
