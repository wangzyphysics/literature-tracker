## Abstract
Early prediction of drug-induced organ toxicity remains a major bottleneck in drug discovery and clinical pharmacotherapy. Most data-driven toxicity models behave as endpoint predictors: they output a label but provide limited transparency about why a compound is risky or which evidence channel dominated the decision. Here, I present a mechanism-aware multimodal framework that fuses three complementary views of each compound: (i) a molecular graph encoder learned from SMILES, (ii) a compact vector of coarse mechanistic proxies (e.g., CYP involvement and reactive-metabolite flags), and (iii) a 384-dimensional text embedding derived from short drug descriptors. A gated fusion network learns sample-specific modality weights and outputs five organ-level toxicity probabilities (hepatotoxicity, nephrotoxicity, cardiotoxicity, mitochondrial toxicity, and hERG/QT risk). On a materialized dataset of 250 drug instances, the full Graph+Mech+Text model achieves strong discrimination on a held-out test split (macro-AUROC ≈ 0.99 in a representative run; hERG/QT AUROC ≈ 0.93). Modality ablations reveal that a Graph-only model performs modestly (macro-AUROC ≈ 0.62), while adding mechanistic proxies improves performance (≈ 0.91), and incorporating drug-specific text pushes macro-AUROC to ≈ 0.98–0.99. To distinguish genuine multimodal synergy from shortcut learning, I perform a diagnostic stress test in which all textual inputs are neutralized. Under this condition, macro-AUROC collapses to 0.533 (95% bootstrap CI 0.490–0.566), approaching chance for several endpoints, and the learned gate concentrates mass almost entirely on the text modality. These findings make two contributions. First, they demonstrate a practical decision-support architecture for organ-specific toxicity prediction that produces interpretable probability profiles rather than hard labels. Second, they introduce a simple but powerful audit methodology—modality ablations plus text neutralization—that exposes when apparent multimodal gains are driven by text-based shortcuts rather than robust structural or mechanistic signals. I discuss implications for dataset design, external validation, and how richer, non-leaky clinical text sources such as FDA label sections could be incorporated in future work.

## 摘要（中文）
药物引起的器官毒性的早期预测仍然是药物发现和临床药物治疗的主要瓶颈。大多数数据驱动的毒性模型都充当终点预测器：它们输出标签，但对于化合物为何有风险或哪个证据渠道主导决策提供的透明度有限。在这里，我提出了一个机制感知的多模态框架，它融合了每种化合物的三个互补视图：（i）从 SMILES 学习的分子图编码器，（ii）粗略机械代理的紧凑向量（例如 CYP 参与和反应性代谢标志），以及（iii）从短药物描述符衍生的 384 维文本嵌入。门控融合网络学习样本特定的模态权重并输出五种器官级毒性概率（肝毒性、肾毒性、心脏毒性、线粒体毒性和 hERG/QT 风险）。在包含 250 个药物实例的物化数据集上，完整的 Graph+Mech+Text 模型对保留的测试分割实现了很强的区分度（代表性运行中的宏观 AUROC ≈ 0.99；hERG/QT AUROC ≈ 0.93）。模态消融表明，仅图模型的性能一般（宏观 AUROC 约 0.62），而添加机械代理可提高性能（约 0.91），并且合并药物特定文本将宏观 AUROC 推至约 0.98-0.99。为了区分真正的多模式协同与捷径学习，我进行了诊断压力测试，其中所有文本输入都被中和。在这种情况下，宏观 AUROC 崩溃至 0.533（95% bootstrap CI 0.490-0.566），接近多个端点的机会，并且学习门几乎完全将质量集中在文本模态上。这些发现有两个贡献。首先，他们展示了一种用于器官特异性毒性预测的实用决策支持架构，该架构产生可解释的概率分布而不是硬标签。其次，他们引入了一种简单但强大的审计方法——模态消融加上文本中和——暴露出明显的多模态增益何时是由基于文本的快捷方式而不是强大的结构或机械信号驱动的。我讨论了数据集设计、外部验证的影响，以及如何将更丰富、无泄漏的临床文本源（例如 FDA 标签部分）纳入未来的工作中。
