## Abstract
Repeat proteins, known for their modularity, are focal points of de novo protein design. This study adopts a curiosity-driven approach to explore and discover proteins with repeated sequences. Leveraging AlphaFold 2, we initially bias our sequence design by integrating amyloid β42 with a random hexapeptide linker, achieving high foldability in over 12% of cases through polymerization. Without targeting a structural specification, we evolve these structures into a mosaic of folds using segmental mutations and genetic algorithms. The majority of these de novo structures demonstrate good stability in silico through molecular dynamics simulation. Additionally, we investigate the compatibility of medial strands to assess potential variations in structural integrity upon mutation. This curiosity-driven approach not only underscores the evolvability and versatility of repeat proteins but also highlights the potential for developing peptide-based pharmaceuticals and functional materials.

## 摘要（中文）
重复蛋白质以其模块化而闻名，是从头蛋白质设计的焦点。本研究采用好奇心驱动的方法来探索和发现具有重复序列的蛋白质。利用 AlphaFold 2，我们最初通过将淀粉样蛋白 β42 与随机六肽接头整合来偏置我们的序列设计，通过聚合在超过 12% 的情况下实现高可折叠性。在不针对结构规范的情况下，我们使用分段突变和遗传算法将这些结构进化为折叠镶嵌。通过分子动力学模拟，大多数这些从头结构在计算机中表现出良好的稳定性。此外，我们研究了内侧链的相容性，以评估突变时结构完整性的潜在变化。这种好奇心驱动的方法不仅强调了重复蛋白的进化性和多功能性，而且还强调了开发基于肽的药物和功能材料的潜力。
