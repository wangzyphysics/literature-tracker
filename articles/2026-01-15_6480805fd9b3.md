## Abstract
arXiv:2601.09596v1 Announce Type: new Abstract: We investigate plasma wake generation via Compton scattering from photon bursts, a non-ponderomotive process relevant when the photon wavelength is smaller than the interparticle distance but larger than the Compton wavelength. In this regime, electrons can reach relativistic velocities. We extend linear theory to the nonlinear regime, showing that plasma waves can reach the wave-breaking limit. Perfectly collimated drivers produce wakes propagating at the speed of light, allowing electron phase-locking (limited by driver depletion). Non-collimated drivers induce subluminal phase velocities, limiting acceleration via dephasing. Two-dimensional simulations reveal unique transverse fields compared to laser wakefields, with a DC magnetic field leading to consistent focusing. The work considers observational prospects in laboratory and astrophysical scenarios such as around highly luminous compact objects (e.g., pulsars, gamma-ray bursts) interacting with tenuous interstellar or intergalactic plasmas, where conditions favor Comptondominated wakefield acceleration.

arXiv:2601.09596v1 公告类型：新 摘要：我们研究了通过光子爆发的康普顿散射产生等离子体尾流，这是当光子波长小于粒子间距离但大于康普顿波长时相关的非质动力过程。在这种情况下，电子可以达到相对论速度。我们将线性理论扩展到非线性领域，表明等离子体波可以达到破波极限。完美准直的驱动器产生以光速传播的尾流，允许电子锁相（受驱动器耗尽的限制）。非准直驱动器会产生亚光速相速度，通过移相限制加速度。二维模拟揭示了与激光尾场相比独特的横向场，直流磁场可实现一致的聚焦。这项工作考虑了实验室和天体物理场景中的观测前景，例如围绕高亮度致密天体（例如脉冲星、伽马射线爆发）与脆弱的星际或星系间等离子体相互作用，其中条件有利于康普顿主导的尾场加速。
