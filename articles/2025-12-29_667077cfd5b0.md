## Abstract
arXiv:2512.20906v2 Announce Type: replace Abstract: Emergent behavior, which arises from local interactions between simple elements, is pervasive in nature. It underlies the energy-efficient computing in our brains. However, realizing such dynamics in artificial materials, particularly under low-energy stimuli, remains a fundamental challenge. While dipole-dipole interactions are typically suppressed in magnetic storage, here we harness and amplify them to construct a strongly dipolar-coupled network of SmCo5 macrospins at wafer scale, which can exhibit intrinsic interaction-driven collective dynamics in response to voltage pulses. The network combines three essential ingredients: strong dipolar coupling by large single-domain macrospin, giant voltage control of coercivity over nearly 1000-fold, and disordered network topology with frustrated Ising-like energy landscape. When stimulated by 1 V pulses, the network enters a regime where interaction-driven magnetic behaviors emerge, including spontaneous demagnetization, greatly enhanced magnetization modulation, reversible freeze and resume evolution and stochastic convergence toward low-energy magnetic configurations. All these behaviors are completely absent at the single-nanomagnet level. Furthermore, by constructing micromagnetic models of the strongly dipolar-coupled macrospin networks, we show that the resulting nonlinear, high-dimensional collective dynamics, intrinsic to strongly-interacting systems, can enable accurate chaotic Mackey-Glass prediction and multiclass drone-signal classification. Our work establishes the voltage-responsive strongly-coupled SmCo5 network as a mesoscopic platform for probing emergent magnetic dynamics previously inaccessible under ambient conditions. It also suggests a fundamental distinct route towards scalable, low-voltage computing, one rooted in native physical interaction-driven collective dynamics at the network level.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2512.20906v2 公告类型：替换 摘要：由简单元素之间的局部交互产生的紧急行为本质上是普遍存在的。它是我们大脑中节能计算的基础。然而，在人造材料中实现这种动力学，特别是在低能量刺激下，仍然是一个根本性的挑战。虽然偶极-偶极相互作用通常在磁存储中受到抑制，但在这里，我们利用并放大它们来构建晶圆级 SmCo5 大自旋的强偶极耦合网络，该网络可以响应电压脉冲而表现出内在的相互作用驱动的集体动力学。该网络结合了三个基本要素：大型单域宏自旋的强偶极耦合、矫顽力近 1000 倍的巨电压控制以及具有受挫的伊辛式能量景观的无序网络拓扑。当受到 1 V 脉冲刺激时，网络进入一种状态，出现相互作用驱动的磁行为，包括自发退磁、大大增强的磁化调制、可逆冻结和恢复演化以及向低能磁配置的随机收敛。所有这些行为在单纳米磁体水平上完全不存在。此外，通过构建强偶极耦合宏自旋网络的微磁模型，我们表明，由此产生的强相互作用系统固有的非线性、高维集体动力学，可以实现精确的混沌麦基-格拉斯预测和多类无人机信号分类。我们的工作建立了电压响应强耦合 SmCo5 网络作为介观平台，用于探测以前在环境条件下无法访问的涌现磁动力学。它还提出了一条通往可扩展的低电压计算的基本独特路线，该路线植根于网络级别的本地物理交互驱动的集体动态。
