## Abstract
arXiv:2601.22827v1 Announce Type: new Abstract: Ferroelectric switching governs the functional properties of ferroelectric perovskites. It is widely accepted that this switching depends on domain nucleation and pinning and that these processes can be controlled by the defect structure. However, an atomistic picture of the influence of one important class of defects - dislocations on ferroelectric switching is missing. This is an important gap in knowledge as dislocations cannot be avoided at interfaces and can also be engineered by plastic deformation at high temperatures. Using atomistic simulations, we show how the cores of $\langle100\rangle$ edge dislocations in BaTiO$_3$ can either act as nucleation centers for ferroelectric switching or pin walls depending on the direction of the applied field. The coupling between electric field and polarization is strongest when the field is applied parallel to the Burgers vector of the dislocation.

arXiv:2601.22827v1 公告类型：新 摘要：铁电开关控制着铁电钙钛矿的功能特性。人们普遍认为，这种转换取决于域成核和钉扎，并且这些过程可以通过缺陷结构来控制。然而，缺少一类重要缺陷——位错对铁电开关影响的原子图。这是一个重要的知识空白，因为位错在界面处无法避免，也可以通过高温下的塑性变形来设计。通过原子模拟，我们展示了 BaTiO$_3$ 中 $\langle100\rangle$ 边缘位错的核心如何根据施加场的方向充当铁电转换的成核中心或针壁。当电场平行于位错的伯格斯矢量施加时，电场和极化之间的耦合最强。
