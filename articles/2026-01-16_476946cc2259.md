## Abstract
arXiv:2512.11650v2 Announce Type: replace Abstract: With the rapid advancement of machine learning techniques for materials simulations, machine-learned force fields (MLFFs) have become a powerful tool that complements first-principles calculations by enabling high-accuracy molecular dynamics simulations over extended timescales. Typically, MLFFs are trained on data generated from density functional theory (DFT) using a specific exchange-correlation (XC) functional, with the goal of reproducing DFT-level properties. However, the uncertainties in MLFF-based simulations--arising from variations in both MLFF model architectures and the choice of XC functionals--remain insufficiently understood. In this work, we construct MLFF models of different architectures trained on DFT data from both semilocal and hybrid functionals to describe Li$^+$ diffusion in the solid-state electrolyte Li$_6$PS$_5$Cl. We systematically investigate how different XC functionals influence the Li$^+$ diffusion coefficient. To reduce statistical uncertainty, the mean squared displacements are averaged over 300 independent molecular dynamics (MD) trajectories of 70 ps each, yielding statistical variations below $1\%$. This enables a clear assessment of the respective influences of the functional and the MLFF model. Due to its tendency to underestimate band gaps and migration barriers, the semilocal functional predicts consistently higher Li$^+$ diffusion coefficients, compared to the hybrid functional. Furthermore, comparisons among various neural network methods reveal that the differences in predicted diffusion coefficients arising from different network architectures are of the same order of magnitude as those caused by different functionals, indicating that the choice of the network model itself substantially influences the MLFF predictions. This observation calls from an urgent need for standardized protocols to minimize model-dependent biases in MLFF-based MD.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2512.11650v2 公告类型：替换 摘要：随着材料模拟机器学习技术的快速发展，机器学习力场 (MLFF) 已成为一种强大的工具，通过在较长时间尺度上实现高精度分子动力学模拟来补充第一性原理计算。通常，MLFF 使用特定的交换相关 (XC) 函数根据密度泛函理论 (DFT) 生成的数据进行训练，目的是重现 DFT 级别的属性。然而，基于 MLFF 的模拟中的不确定性（由 MLFF 模型架构和 XC 泛函的选择的变化引起）仍然没有得到充分理解。在这项工作中，我们构建了不同架构的 MLFF 模型，并根据半局域和混合泛函的 DFT 数据进行训练，以描述固态电解质 Li$_6$PS$_5$Cl 中的 Li$^+$ 扩散。我们系统地研究了不同的 XC 泛函如何影响 Li$^+$ 扩散系数。为了减少统计不确定性，均方位移对 300 个独立的分子动力学 (MD) 轨迹进行平均，每个轨迹为 70 ps，产生低于 $1\%$ 的统计变化。这样可以清楚地评估功能模型和 MLFF 模型各自的影响。由于其倾向于低估带隙和迁移势垒，与混合泛函相比，半局部泛函预测始终较高的 Li$^+$ 扩散系数。此外，各种神经网络方法之间的比较表明，不同网络架构引起的预测扩散系数的差异与不同泛函引起的预测扩散系数的差异处于同一数量级，这表明网络模型本身的选择极大地影响了MLFF预测。这一观察结果表明，迫切需要标准化协议来最大限度地减少基于 MLFF 的 MD 中模型相关的偏差。
