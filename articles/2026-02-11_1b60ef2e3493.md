## Abstract
arXiv:2602.09519v1 Announce Type: new Abstract: Optical computing provides a promising path toward energy-efficient machine learning, yet implementing nonlinear transformations without complex electronics or high-power sources remains challenging. Here, we demonstrate that continuous-wave multimode fiber laser cavities can function as nonlinear optical processors. Input images encoded as phase patterns on a spatial light modulator undergo high-dimensional transformation through the interplay of multimode interference and gain saturation dynamics. The cavity maps input data into spatially stable, class-separable intensity distributions, enabling a simple linear classifier to achieve accuracies of 85--99\% across diverse benchmarks -- including medical imaging and remote sensing -- with orders of magnitude fewer trainable parameters than deep neural networks. Our results establish multimode fiber lasers as compact, low-power physical processors for scalable optical machine learning.
