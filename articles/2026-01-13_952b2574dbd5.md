## Abstract
Staphylococcus aureus (S. aureus) is a major bacterial pathogen and a significant biothreat, representing a leading cause of bacterial mortality worldwide. The challenge is compounded by methicillin-resistant S. aureus (MRSA), a strain resistant to first-line antibiotics, which is associated with a 64% higher mortality rate compared to susceptible strains. Current methods for accurately identifying and localizing occult infections are limited, and there is a critical lack of effective novel treatments for infections that fail to respond to antibiotics. To address this, we pioneered the development of an antimicrobial peptide (AMP)-based radiotheranostic pair for the imaging-guided treatment of S. aureus and MRSA infections. We first screened a library of renowned AMPs for their efficacy against (MR)SA and their suitability for non-invasive, whole-body PET imaging after radiolabeling with Gallium-68. The optimal candidate, [68Ga]QAMP3, demonstrated excellent performance, readily and specifically accumulating in both S. aureus and MRSA infection sites, but not in sterile inflammation or tumors. A pilot first-in-human study confirmed the translatable diagnostic potential and favorable biosafety profile of [68Ga]QAMP3. Furthermore, the corresponding therapeutic agent, [177Lu]QAMP3, effectively reduced bacterial load in rat models of septic arthritis caused by both S. aureus and MRSA following a single systemic administration. Treatment resulted in the complete restoration of joint morphology, comparable to healthy controls. Simultaneously, [68Ga]QAMP3 PET/CT imaging accurately monitored the therapeutic response to both [177Lu]QAMP3 and conventional antibiotic treatment. Finally, molecular dynamics simulations revealed that the stable α-helical conformation of QAMP3 likely confers superior membrane permeability and subsequent internalization into bacterial cells, leading to lethal DNA damage. These findings position [68Ga]/[177Lu]QAMP3 as an innovative theranostic pair for the rapid diagnosis and effective treatment of MRSA infections. This study also paves the way for developing new radio-antibiotics with mechanisms of action that can circumvent conventional antibiotic resistance.

## 摘要（中文）
金黄色葡萄球菌（S. aureus）是一种主要的细菌病原体和重大的生物威胁，是全球细菌死亡的主要原因。耐甲氧西林金黄色葡萄球菌 (MRSA) 是一种对一线抗生素具有耐药性的菌株，与敏感菌株相比，其死亡率高出 64%，这使得这一挑战变得更加复杂。目前准确识别和定位隐匿性感染的方法有限，并且严重缺乏针对抗生素无效感染的有效新疗法。为了解决这个问题，我们率先开发了一种基于抗菌肽 (AMP) 的放射治疗对，用于影像引导下金黄色葡萄球菌和 MRSA 感染的治疗。我们首先筛选了一个著名的 AMP 库，以确定它们对 (MR)SA 的功效以及它们在用 Gallium-68 放射性标记后是否适合非侵入性全身 PET 成像。最佳候选物[68Ga]QAMP3表现出优异的性能，容易且特异性地在金黄色葡萄球菌和MRSA感染部位积聚，但不在无菌炎症或肿瘤中积聚。一项首次人体试验证实了 [68Ga]QAMP3 的可转化诊断潜力和良好的生物安全性。此外，相应的治疗剂[177Lu]QAMP3在单次全身给药后可有效减少由金黄色葡萄球菌和MRSA引起的化脓性关节炎大鼠模型中的细菌负荷。与健康对照相比，治疗导致关节形态完全恢复。同时，[68Ga]QAMP3 PET/CT 成像准确监测对 [177Lu]QAMP3 和常规抗生素治疗的治疗反应。最后，分子动力学模拟表明，QAMP3 稳定的 α 螺旋构象可能具有优异的膜通透性，并随后内化到细菌细胞中，从而导致致命的 DNA 损伤。这些发现将[68Ga]/[177Lu]QAMP3定位为创新的治疗诊断对，用于快速诊断和有效治疗MRSA感染。这项研究还为开发具有可以规避传统抗生素耐药性作用机制的新型放射性抗生素铺平了道路。
