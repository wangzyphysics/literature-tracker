## Abstract
arXiv:2602.13805v1 Announce Type: cross Abstract: Untrained neural networks (UNNs) offer high-fidelity electromagnetic inverse scattering reconstruction but are computationally limited by high-dimensional spatial-domain optimization. We propose a Real-Time Physics-Driven Fourier-Spectral (PDF) solver that achieves sub-second reconstruction through spectral-domain dimensionality reduction. By expanding induced currents using a truncated Fourier basis, the optimization is confined to a compact low-frequency parameter space supported by scattering measurements. The solver integrates a contraction integral equation (CIE) to mitigate high-contrast nonlinearity and a contrast-compensated operator (CCO) to correct spectral-induced attenuation. Furthermore, a bridge-suppressing loss is formulated to enhance boundary sharpness between adjacent scatterers. Numerical and experimental results demonstrate a 100-fold speedup over state-of-the-art UNNs with robust performance under noise and antenna uncertainties, enabling real-time microwave imaging applications.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2602.13805v1 公告类型：cross 摘要：未经训练的神经网络 (UNN) 提供高保真电磁逆散射重建，但在计算上受到高维空间域优化的限制。我们提出了一种实时物理驱动的傅里叶谱（PDF）求解器，通过谱域降维实现亚秒级重建。通过使用截断傅立叶基扩展感应电流，优化被限制在散射测量支持的紧凑低频参数空间内。该求解器集成了收缩积分方程 (CIE) 以减轻高对比度非线性，并集成了对比度补偿算子 (CCO) 以校正光谱引起的衰减。此外，还制定了桥抑制损耗以增强相邻散射体之间的边界锐度。数值和实验结果表明，与最先进的 UNN 相比，速度提高了 100 倍，并且在噪声和天线不确定性下具有强大的性能，从而实现了实时微波成像应用。
