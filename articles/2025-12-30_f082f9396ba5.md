## Abstract
arXiv:2512.22923v1 Announce Type: new Abstract: Cellular organization and mechanotransduction pathways are crucial regulators of tissue morphogenesis, whereas their dysregulation contributes to pathologies. Overactive fibroblasts, or myofibroblasts, are key drivers of fibrosis, yet how their presence alters collective cellular ordering remains unclear. Inspired by the analogy between liquid crystals and cells, we investigate how topological defects influence reorganization in dense monolayers of co-cultured fibroblasts and myofibroblasts. Owing to steric interactions, these elongated cells exhibit local order; topological defects, where alignment is disrupted, have been postulated to serve as mechanical centers. In this study, we examine how the incorporation of contractile myofibroblasts impacts defect relaxation. The behavior is reminiscent of active nematics with quenched disorder: myofibroblast concentration modulates the disorder strength; increasing their fraction slows defect recombination. Higher myofibroblast concentrations similarly reduce the overall cell alignment on microgrooved surfaces, as myofibroblasts interfere with monolayer reorganization. This observation highlights the potential of a simple, quantitative assay for diagnosing disease progression. Furthermore, we found that myofibroblasts preferentially localize at negatively charged -1/2 defects, compared to fibroblasts, which tend to be localized in +1/2 defects. Consequently, the slowdown of recombination dynamics can be partially attributed to the reduced velocity of the more mobile +1/2 defects. Our study suggests that myofibroblasts can exploit negatively charged defects by avoiding regions of compressive stress and evading apoptosis. Combining live-cell imaging and immunofluorescence studies, this work provides insights into the role of topological defects in fibrotic disease progression.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2512.22923v1 公告类型：新 摘要：细胞组织和机械转导途径是组织形态发生的关键调节因子，而它们的失调会导致病理。过度活跃的成纤维细胞或肌成纤维细胞是纤维化的关键驱动因素，但它们的存在如何改变集体细胞排序仍不清楚。受液晶和细胞之间类比的启发，我们研究了拓扑缺陷如何影响共培养的成纤维细胞和肌成纤维细胞的致密单层的重组。由于空间相互作用，这些细长的细胞表现出局部有序性；排列被破坏的拓扑缺陷被认为是机械中心。在这项研究中，我们研究了收缩性肌成纤维细胞的掺入如何影响缺损松弛。这种行为让人想起具有猝灭无序的活性向列相：肌成纤维细胞浓度调节无序强度；增加它们的分数会减慢缺陷复合。较高的肌成纤维细胞浓度同样会降低微纹表面上的整体细胞排列，因为肌成纤维细胞会干扰单层重组。这一观察结果凸显了一种简单的定量检测方法在诊断疾病进展方面的潜力。此外，我们发现肌成纤维细胞优先定位于带负电的-1/2 缺陷处，而成纤维细胞则倾向于定位于+1/2 缺陷处。因此，复合动态的减慢可部分归因于更易移动的+1/2缺陷的速度降低。我们的研究表明，肌成纤维细胞可以通过避开压缩应力区域并逃避细胞凋亡来利用带负电的缺陷。这项工作结合活细胞成像和免疫荧光研究，深入了解拓扑缺陷在纤维化疾病进展中的作用。
