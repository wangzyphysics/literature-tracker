## Abstract
arXiv:2512.22507v1 Announce Type: new Abstract: Understanding and controlling the chemical states both in the bulk and at the interfaces of complex oxide thin films is essential for engineering a wide range of electronic, optical, and magnetic functionalities, which arise through emergent phenomena such as two-dimensional electron gases, interfacial magnetism, and associated phase transitions. Here, we demonstrate the use of in situ Auger Electron Spectroscopy (AES) as a powerful tool for probing oxidation states and dynamic chemical processes during the growth of complex oxide heterostructures. By leveraging the chemical sensitivity of AES to subtle changes in valence electron populations, we show that this technique can distinguish distinct oxidation states in multivalent perovskite manganate and vanadate systems with high fidelity during deposition. Furthermore, we show evidence for dynamic chemical phenomena, specifically charge transfer processes at the polar-nonpolar LaMnO3/SrTiO3 interface. Our results establish in situ AES as a powerful diagnostic tool for monitoring and controlling interfacial chemistry during thin film growth, offering a pathway toward the atomic-scale engineering of chemical states in functional oxide heterostructures.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2512.22507v1 公告类型：新 摘要：理解和控制复杂氧化物薄膜的本体和界面处的化学状态对于设计广泛的电子、光学和磁性功能至关重要，这些功能是通过二维电子气、界面磁性和相关相变等涌现现象而产生的。在这里，我们演示了使用原位俄歇电子能谱（AES）作为探测复杂氧化物异质结构生长过程中氧化态和动态化学过程的强大工具。通过利用 AES 对价电子群细微变化的化学敏感性，我们证明该技术可以在沉积过程中以高保真度区分多价钙钛矿锰酸盐和钒酸盐体系中的不同氧化态。此外，我们还展示了动态化学现象的证据，特别是极性-非极性 LaMnO3/SrTiO3 界面处的电荷转移过程。我们的研究结果确立了原位 AES 作为监测和控制薄膜生长过程中界面化学的强大诊断工具，为功能性氧化物异质结构中化学态的原子级工程提供了一条途径。
