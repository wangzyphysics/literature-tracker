## Abstract
arXiv:2511.10368v2 Announce Type: replace Abstract: Point defects give rise to sharp modifications in the structures and electronic properties of two-dimensional metals, offering an atomic-level platform for fundamental studies and potential applications. In this work, we investigate atomic-scale defects in a two-dimensional silver monolayer intercalated between epitaxial graphene and SiC using scanning tunneling microscopy. Dark and bright defects are identified as vacancies or substitutional impurities within the silver monolayer, each hosting a localized electronic state. Remarkably, under tunneling electron excitation at negative bias, the bright defects exhibit dynamic behaviors characterized by inelastic switching between two states. The switching can be reversibly controlled by the microscope tip, enabling the defects to function as atomic-scale two-level conductance switches. Analysis of defect switching reveals possible defect origins and the relationship between dark and bright defect species. Our findings establish a pathway to precise manipulation of defects in two-dimensional metals and uncover previously unexplored dynamics with potential use in nanoelectronics.

arXiv:2511.10368v2 公告类型：替换 摘要：点缺陷导致二维金属的结构和电子性质发生急剧变化，为基础研究和潜在应用提供了原子级平台。在这项工作中，我们使用扫描隧道显微镜研究了插入外延石墨烯和 SiC 之间的二维银单层中的原子级缺陷。暗缺陷和亮缺陷被识别为银单层内的空位或替代杂质，每个缺陷都具有局部电子态。值得注意的是，在负偏压下的隧道电子激发下，亮缺陷表现出以两种状态之间的非弹性切换为特征的动态行为。这种开关可以由显微镜尖端可逆地控制，使缺陷能够充当原子级两级电导开关。缺陷切换分析揭示了可能的缺陷起源以及暗缺陷和亮缺陷种类之间的关系。我们的研究结果建立了一条精确操纵二维金属缺陷的途径，并揭示了以前未探索的动力学在纳米电子学中的潜在用途。
