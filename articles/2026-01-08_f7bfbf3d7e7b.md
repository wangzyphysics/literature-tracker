## Abstract
arXiv:2601.03877v1 Announce Type: cross Abstract: Many biological networks grow by elongation of filaments that can branch and fuse -- typical examples include fungal mycelium or slime mold. These networks must simultaneously perform multiple tasks such as transport, exploration, and robustness under finite resources. Yet, how such multi-task architectures emerge from local growth processes remains poorly understood. Here, we introduce a minimal model of spatial network morphogenesis based solely on stochastic branching, fusion, and stopping, during elongation. Despite the absence of global optimization or feedback, the model generates a broad morphospace from tree-like, to loopy, as well as hybrid architectures. By quantifying multiple functional objectives, we show that (i) these synthetic structures occupy similar regions of performance space than evolved empirical fungal networks, and (ii) that their Pareto front of optimal trade-offs lies close to that of these same fungal networks. Our results show that biological architectures approaching multi-objective optimality can arise from simple local growth rules, and identify branching and fusion as fundamental ingredients shaping the architecture of living transport networks.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2601.03877v1 公告类型：cross 摘要：许多生物网络通过可以分支和融合的细丝伸长来生长——典型的例子包括真菌菌丝体或粘菌。这些网络必须同时执行多项任务，例如传输、探索和有限资源下的鲁棒性。然而，这种多任务架构如何从局部增长过程中产生仍然知之甚少。在这里，我们引入了一个仅基于伸长过程中的随机分支、融合和停止的空间网络形态发生的最小模型。尽管缺乏全局优化或反馈，该模型仍生成了从树状到循环以及混合架构的广泛形态空间。通过量化多个功能目标，我们表明（i）这些合成结构与进化的经验真菌网络相比占据了相似的性能空间区域，并且（ii）它们的最优权衡的帕累托前沿与这些相同的真菌网络的帕累托前沿接近。我们的结果表明，接近多目标最优性的生物架构可以从简单的局部增长规则中产生，并将分支和融合确定为塑造生命运输网络架构的基本成分。
