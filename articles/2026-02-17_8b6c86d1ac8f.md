## Abstract
arXiv:2602.14205v1 Announce Type: new Abstract: Determining the structure and following the structural evolution of molecules undergoing chemical reactions is one of the key goals of ultrafast molecular physics and chemistry. Recently, Coulomb explosion imaging has emerged as a promising technique for imaging the evolving structure of individual molecules in the gas phase. However, its practical application for structure determination is hampered by the lack of suitable algorithms to directly retrieve the molecular structure from the measured fragment-ion momentum data. Here, we propose a scheme to solve the underlying inverse problem by employing neural networks to infer the initial atomic positions from the final ion momenta on an event-by-event basis. Using this scheme, we retrieve the structure of several polyhalomethane isomers from simulated Coulomb explosion imaging data with an average per-atom position error of approximately 0.1 atomic units, i.e., to within 5% of the typical bond lengths. This development paves the way for an automated structure retrieval from Coulomb explosion data one molecule at a time, making it ideally suitable for analyzing pump-probe experiments where several products are formed that need to be distinguished.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2602.14205v1 公告类型：新 摘要：确定发生化学反应的分子的结构并跟踪其结构演化是超快分子物理和化学的关键目标之一。最近，库仑爆炸成像已成为一种有前途的技术，用于对气相中单个分子的演化结构进行成像。然而，由于缺乏合适的算法来直接从测量的碎片离子动量数据中检索分子结构，其在结构测定方面的实际应用受到阻碍。在这里，我们提出了一种方案，通过采用神经网络逐个事件地从最终离子动量推断初始原子位置来解决潜在的反问题。使用该方案，我们从模拟库仑爆炸成像数据中检索了几种多卤甲烷异构体的结构，平均每个原子位置误差约为 0.1 个原子单位，即典型键长的 5% 以内。这一发展为一次从库仑爆炸数据中自动检索一个分子的结构铺平了道路，使其非常适合分析泵浦探针实验，在这些实验中形成了多种需要区分的产物。
