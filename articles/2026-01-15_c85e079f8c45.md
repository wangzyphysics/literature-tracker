## Abstract
arXiv:2505.19998v3 Announce Type: replace-cross Abstract: Urban-induced changes in local microclimate, such as urban heat islands and air pollution, are known to vary with city size, leading to distinctive relations between average climate variables and city-scale quantities (e.g., total population). However, these approaches suffer from biases related to the choice of city boundaries and they neglect intra-urban variations of urban characteristics. Here, we use high-resolution data of urban temperatures, air quality, population, and street networks from 142 cities worldwide and show that their marginal and joint probability distributions collapse onto a set of general scaling functions. Using a logarithmic relation between urban spatial features and climate variables, we find that average street network properties are sufficient to characterize the entire variability of the temperature and air pollution fields observed within and across cities. These findings provide a unified statistical framework for characterizing intra-urban climate variability, with important implications for climate modeling and urban planning.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2505.19998v3 公告类型：replace-cross 摘要：众所周知，城市引起的局部小气候变化，例如城市热岛和空气污染，会随着城市规模的变化而变化，从而导致平均气候变量与城市规模数量（例如总人口）之间存在独特的关系。然而，这些方法存在与城市边界选择相关的偏差，并且忽略了城市特征的城市内差异。在这里，我们使用来自全球 142 个城市的城市温度、空气质量、人口和街道网络的高分辨率数据，并表明它们的边际和联合概率分布崩溃到一组通用标度函数上。利用城市空间特征和气候变量之间的对数关系，我们发现平均街道网络属性足以表征城市内部和城市之间观察到的温度和空气污染场的整体变化。这些发现为描述城市内气候变化提供了一个统一的统计框架，对气候建模和城市规划具有重要意义。
