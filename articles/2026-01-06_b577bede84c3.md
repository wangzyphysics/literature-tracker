## Abstract
arXiv:2601.00982v1 Announce Type: new Abstract: Magnetic skyrmions are nanometric swirling spin textures that exhibit remarkable stability at finite temperatures, making them promising candidates for spintronic applications. Achieving controllable stability and transitions between distinct topological structures is crucial for practical implementations. In this work, we investigate the effect of uniaxial mechanical strain on a magnetic model on the kagome lattice, focusing on skyrmion stability and emergent topological phases. To this end, we consider a Heisenberg model that includes exchange interactions and both in-plane and out-of-plane Dzyaloshinskii-Moriya interactions. Using a combination of Spin-Lattice Dynamics and Monte Carlo simulations, we explore uniaxial strain variations in the range of $-10\%$ to $10\%$, showing important effects on the phase diagram. For compressive strain, we find that the density of skyrmions in the skyrmion gas (SkG) phase can be tuned and that the stability of this phase extends to higher temperatures. Tensile strain, in contrast, reduces the number of skyrmions and promotes transitions to other magnetic states. Within this regime, strain levels of about ($\sim4-6\%$) lead to a change in topological charge, turning skyrmions ($Q=-1$) into antiskyrmions ($Q=+1$). We also examine how strain affects other phases commonly appearing in skyrmion-hosting systems, such as the helical and fully polarized states, showing that mechanical deformation alters their stability and characteristic properties. Finally, we compare these results with the strain response of a more conventional skyrmion model, in order to clarify the role of the different interactions involved. Our results identify strain as an experimentally accessible route for engineering topological spin textures.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2601.00982v1 公告类型：新 摘要：磁性斯格明子是纳米级旋转自旋纹理，在有限温度下表现出显着的稳定性，使其成为自旋电子应用的有希望的候选者。实现不同拓扑结构之间的可控稳定性和过渡对于实际实现至关重要。在这项工作中，我们研究了单轴机械应变对 Kagome 晶格磁模型的影响，重点关注斯格明子稳定性和涌现拓扑相。为此，我们考虑一个包含交换相互作用以及面内和面外 Dzyaloshinskii-Moriya 相互作用的海森堡模型。结合自旋晶格动力学和蒙特卡罗模拟，我们探索了 $-10\%$ 到 $10\%$ 范围内的单轴应变变化，显示了对相图的重要影响。对于压缩应变，我们发现斯格明子气相（SkG）中斯格明子的密度可以调节，并且该相的稳定性延伸到更高的温度。相反，拉伸应变减少了斯格明子的数量并促进向其他磁性状态的转变。在这种情况下，约 ($\sim4-6\%$) 的应变水平会导致拓扑电荷发生变化，将斯格明子 ($Q=-1$) 转变为反斯格明子 ($Q=+1$)。我们还研究了应变如何影响斯格明子宿主系统中常见的其他相，例如螺旋态和完全极化态，表明机械变形改变了它们的稳定性和特性。最后，我们将这些结果与更传统的斯格明子模型的应变响应进行比较，以阐明所涉及的不同相互作用的作用。我们的结果将应变确定为工程拓扑自旋纹理的实验可行途径。
