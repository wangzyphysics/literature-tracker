## Abstract
Atomic-scale manipulation of chemical bond cleavage and formation offers significant advantages of extending the reaction controllability to the single-molecule regime and enabling the elucidation of fundamental reaction mechanisms. Herein, we drive reactions between two chemical species, open-shell monomeric radicals and closed-shell dimers in single-molecule junctions. Both monomers and dimers form a C-Au covalently-linked highly conducting junction in situ, with the conductance about 100 ́ higher than that of the dimer junction. First- principles calculations suggest that the substitution groups on the carbon in the C(sp3)-Au linkage bond effectively tunes the molecular junction conductance. Notably, captodative radicals enable the formation of weakly bonded spin-spin interactions in solution, yielding diamagnetic dimers, and we can reversibly switch between the dimer and the weakly- bonded radical pair by mechanically controlling the gap between the two electrodes.

化学键断裂和形成的原子尺度操控具有将反应可控性扩展到单分子范围并能够阐明基本反应机制的显着优势。在这里，我们在单分子连接中驱动两种化学物质、开壳单体自由基和闭壳二聚体之间的反应。单体和二聚体均在原位形成C-Au共价连接的高导电连接，其电导比二聚体连接高约100 ́。第一原理计算表明，C(sp3)-Au 连接键中碳上的取代基有效地调节了分子结电导。值得注意的是，俘获自由基能够在溶液中形成弱键合的自旋-自旋相互作用，产生抗磁性二聚体，并且我们可以通过机械控制两个电极之间的间隙在二聚体和弱键合自由基对之间可逆地切换。
