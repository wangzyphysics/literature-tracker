## Abstract
arXiv:2601.10483v1 Announce Type: cross Abstract: We study the high-dimensional training dynamics of a shallow neural network with quadratic activation in a teacher-student setup. We focus on the extensive-width regime, where the teacher and student network widths scale proportionally with the input dimension, and the sample size grows quadratically. This scaling aims to describe overparameterized neural networks in which feature learning still plays a central role. In the high-dimensional limit, we derive a dynamical characterization of the gradient flow, in the spirit of dynamical mean-field theory (DMFT). Under l2-regularization, we analyze these equations at long times and characterize the performance and spectral properties of the resulting estimator. This result provides a quantitative understanding of the effect of overparameterization on learning and generalization, and reveals a double descent phenomenon in the presence of label noise, where generalization improves beyond interpolation. In the small regularization limit, we obtain an exact expression for the perfect recovery threshold as a function of the network widths, providing a precise characterization of how overparameterization influences recovery.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2601.10483v1 公告类型：cross 摘要：我们研究了师生设置中具有二次激活的浅层神经网络的高维训练动态。我们关注扩展宽度机制，其中教师和学生网络宽度与输入维度成比例缩放，并且样本大小呈二次方增长。这种缩放旨在描述过度参数化的神经网络，其中特征学习仍然发挥着核心作用。在高维极限下，我们本着动态平均场理论（DMFT）的精神推导了梯度流的动态表征。在 l2 正则化下，我们长时间分析这些方程并表征所得估计器的性能和谱特性。这一结果提供了对过度参数化对学习和泛化的影响的定量理解，并揭示了在标签噪声存在的情况下的双下降现象，其中泛化的改进超出了插值。在小的正则化限制下，我们获得了完美恢复阈值作为网络宽度函数的精确表达式，从而提供了过度参数化如何影响恢复的精确表征。
