## Abstract
Enantioselective hydrogen atom transfer (HAT) has great synthetic potential, but the de novo design of chiral HAT catalysts is challenging. We report a conceptually distinct approach in which chiral HAT catalysts are generated in situ through non-covalent self-assembly of privileged chiral phosphoric acids and commercial 2-mercaptopyridines. The phosphoric acid serves as a modular chiral source that renders the achiral thiol effectively chiral, enabling a previously inaccessible combinatorial space of chiral HAT catalysts. This platform enabled the photochemical deracemization of 2-aryl pyrrolidines, a prevalent scaffold in active pharmaceutical ingredients. Optical enrichment proceeds via enantioselective hydrogen atom relay, in which a single chiral assembly orchestrates both hydrogen atom abstraction and delivery. This concept of relaying chiral information via non-covalent assembly holds broad potential for asymmetric radical catalysis.

## 摘要（中文）
对映选择性氢原子转移（HAT）具有巨大的合成潜力，但手性 HAT 催化剂的从头设计具有挑战性。我们报告了一种概念上不同的方法，其中通过优先手性磷酸和商业 2-巯基吡啶的非共价自组装原位生成手性 HAT 催化剂。磷酸作为模块化手性源，使非手性硫醇有效地具有手性，从而实现了以前无法达到的手性 HAT 催化剂的组合空间。该平台能够实现 2-芳基吡咯烷（活性药物成分中常见的支架）的光化学去消旋作用。光学富集通过对映选择性氢原子中继进行，其中单个手性组装协调氢原子的提取和传递。这种通过非共价组装传递手性信息的概念在不对称自由基催化方面具有广阔的潜力。
