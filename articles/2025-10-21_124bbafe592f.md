## Abstract
ABSTRACT Miniature magnetic untethered soft robots offer promising opportunities for biomedical applications due to their tissue compatibility, functionalizable dimensions and flexible locomotion capabilities. However, their small size creates a mismatch between coverage area and lesion regions, limiting drug delivery efficacy. Here, inspired by natural fish migration and foraging behaviors, we present an approach to overcome these limitations through swarm coordination of fish-like magnetic soft robots. Individual robots with simplified designs for scalable production can perform constrained six-degrees-of-freedom fish-like maneuvers under an oscillating magnetic field and gradient magnetic field. By exploiting the unique property of the constant component of the oscillating magnetic field dominating the swimming direction when the actuation frequency approaches the robot’s natural frequency, we achieved differentiated control of individual swimming directions under global magnetic field actuation through programming spatial distribution patterns of the constant field component. This enables our robotic swarm to demonstrate active spatial aggregation toward specific lesion areas and shape-adaptive attachment capabilities upon reaching the target. This work provides a new pathway for efficient therapeutic payload delivery to lesions through coordinated miniature magnetic soft robot swarms.

## 摘要（中文）
摘要：微型磁性无绳软机器人由于其组织兼容性、功能化尺寸和灵活的运动能力，为生物医学应用提供了有前景的机会。然而，它们的小尺寸造成覆盖区域和病变区域之间的不匹配，限制了药物输送功效。在这里，受鱼类自然迁徙和觅食行为的启发，我们提出了一种通过类鱼磁性软机器人的群体协调来克服这些限制的方法。具有可扩展生产的简化设计的单个机器人可以在振荡磁场和梯度磁场下执行受限的六自由度鱼状动作。利用当驱动频率接近机器人固有频率时振荡磁场的恒定分量主导游泳方向的独特性质，我们通过对恒定场分量的空间分布模式进行编程，实现了全局磁场驱动下个体游泳方向的差异化控制。这使得我们的机器人群能够展示针对特定病变区域的主动空间聚合以及到达目标后的形状自适应附着能力。这项工作为通过协调的微型磁性软机器人群有效地将治疗有效载荷输送到病变部位提供了一条新途径。
