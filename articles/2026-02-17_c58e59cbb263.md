## Abstract
arXiv:2505.11155v2 Announce Type: replace Abstract: Purpose: Interpretability is essential for the clinical adoption of state-of-the-art machine learning (ML) methods in magnetic resonance imaging (MRI). Conventional evaluation of ML reconstructions relies heavily on aggregate image metrics that require fully sampled references. These metrics, inherited from classical image processing and natural image ML, often overlook the critical challenge of noise amplification specific to medical image reconstruction. This study aims to analyze the influence of nonlinear activations on spatial noise variance distribution of k-space interpolation networks (RAKI) and to provide a framework for incorporating variance maps during network training. Methods: We present an analytical framework that decomposes pixel-level noise variance into components reflecting linear and nonlinear characteristics of RAKI. By applying automatic differentiation on the image-space equivalent of the network, variance maps are computed during each training iteration, enabling runtime quality assessment beyond data consistency. We introduce apparent blurring, quantifying nonlinear signal mixing without dependence on reference images. By incorporating variance maps into the traning loss as regularizers, our self-informed RAKI architecture (G-factor-informed RAKI, GIF-RAKI) can directly integrate updated noise characteristics during runtime. Results: Experimental results demonstrate that variance components quantitatively explain network behavior. GIF-RAKI outperforms conventional RAKI variants in image fidelity and noise suppression. Conclusion: Our methodology advances practical and theoretical aspects of ML-based MRI reconstruction by reinstating reconstruction noise characterization as a cornerstone for performance evaluation, eliminating the need for fully sampled references. GIF-RAKI also enables optimization of the trade-off between denoising and apparent blurring.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2505.11155v2 公告类型：替换 摘要：目的：可解释性对于临床采用磁共振成像 (MRI) 中最先进的机器学习 (ML) 方法至关重要。机器学习重建的传统评估很大程度上依赖于需要完全采样参考的聚合图像指标。这些指标继承自经典图像处理和自然图像机器学习，通常忽视了医学图像重建特有的噪声放大的关键挑战。本研究旨在分析非线性激活对 k 空间插值网络 (RAKI) 空间噪声方差分布的影响，并提供在网络训练期间合并方差图的框架。方法：我们提出了一个分析框架，将像素级噪声方差分解为反映 RAKI 线性和非线性特征的分量。通过在网络的图像空间等效项上应用自动微分，在每次训练迭代期间计算方差图，从而实现超越数据一致性的运行时质量评估。我们引入了明显的模糊，量化了非线性信号混合，而不依赖于参考图像。通过将方差图作为正则化器合并到训练损失中，我们的自我通知 RAKI 架构（G 因子通知 RAKI、GIF-RAKI）可以在运行时直接集成更新的噪声特征。结果：实验结果表明方差分量定量地解释了网络行为。 GIF-RAKI 在图像保真度和噪声抑制方面优于传统的 RAKI 变体。结论：我们的方法通过恢复重建噪声表征作为性能评估的基石，消除了对完全采样参考的需要，从而推进了基于 ML 的 MRI 重建的实践和理论方面。 GIF-RAKI 还可以优化去噪和明显模糊之间的权衡。
