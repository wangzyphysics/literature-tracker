## Abstract
arXiv:2602.14824v1 Announce Type: new Abstract: This paper presents experimental validation of the homogenized harmonic balance finite element method (HomHBFEM), which we have developed as a dedicated simulation technique for magnets with fast excitation cycles, in particular the fast corrector (FC) magnets for PETRA IV at DESY. The HomHBFEM allows efficient three-dimensional nonlinear eddy-current simulations of laminated magnets at elevated frequencies with a relatively coarse finite element (FE) mesh and without computationally expensive time-stepping. This is achieved by combining a frequency-domain-based homogenization technique with the harmonic balance FE method. The simulation results for the magnetic flux density along the axis of the FC magnets as a function of frequency and the resulting integrated transfer function (ITF) are compared to Hall probe and search coil measurements of the first prototype FC magnet for PETRA IV. A good agreement between simulated and measured ITFs is achieved for excitation frequencies from 10 Hz to 10 kHz.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2602.14824v1 公告类型：新 摘要：本文介绍了均质谐波平衡有限元法 (HomHBFEM) 的实验验证，该方法是我们开发的一种专用于快速励磁周期磁体的仿真技术，特别是 DESY 的 PETRA IV 的快速校正器 (FC) 磁体。 HomHBFEM 允许使用相对粗糙的有限元 (FE) 网格在升高的频率下对层压磁体进行高效的三维非线性涡流模拟，并且无需计算成本高昂的时间步进。这是通过将基于频域的均匀化技术与谐波平衡有限元方法相结合来实现的。将沿 FC 磁体轴的磁通密度作为频率函数的仿真结果以及由此产生的积分传递函数 (ITF) 与 PETRA IV 的第一个原型 FC 磁体的霍尔探头和搜索线圈测量结果进行比较。对于 10 Hz 至 10 kHz 的激励频率，模拟和测量的 ITF 之间具有良好的一致性。
