# Local atomic structural descriptor and crystal field determined Ru 1− x Ir x O 2 as oxygen evolution catalyst unifying stability and activity

## Abstract
Ru/Ir oxides represent the promising catalysts of oxygen evolution reaction (OER) with better stability than Ru oxides and lower overpotentials than Ir oxides. To elucidate the activity and stability mechanisms of Ru 1− x Ir x O 2 at an acceptable computation cost, the proposed local atomic structural descriptors are used to predicate OER intermediate adsorption ability and solvation effect accurately, and the cluster expansion (CE) method is applied to determine the ground state at given Ir concentration. Further analysis shows that Ru–O–Ir local configuration is the atomic fingerprint of Ru 1− x Ir x O 2 structures, unifying both OER activity and stability. Crystal field analysis reveals that the Ru atom makes the dz2 band center of the Ir site atom upshift to achieve weak *OOH adsorption for high OER activity, while Ir atoms effectively lower the t 2g orbital of the Ru atom to stabilize the structures. Our work not only addresses the activity–stability trade-off in Ru/Ir oxides but also advances the fundamental understanding of descriptor-based and CE models.

## 摘要（中文）
Ru/Ir氧化物是有前途的析氧反应（OER）催化剂，其稳定性比Ru氧化物更好，过电势比Ir氧化物更低。为了以可接受的计算成本阐明Ru 1− x Ir x O 2 的活性和稳定性机制，所提出的局部原子结构描述符用于准确预测OER中间吸附能力和溶剂化效应，并应用簇扩展（CE）方法来确定给定Ir浓度下的基态。进一步分析表明，Ru-O-Ir 局部构型是 Ru 1− x Ir x O 2 结构的原子指纹，统一了 OER 活性和稳定性。晶场分析表明，Ru原子使Ir位原子的dz2能带中心上移，实现弱*OOH吸附，从而实现高OER活性，而Ir原子有效降低Ru原子的t 2g轨道以稳定结构。我们的工作不仅解决了 Ru/Ir 氧化物的活性与稳定性的权衡问题，而且增进了对基于描述符和 CE 模型的基本理解。
