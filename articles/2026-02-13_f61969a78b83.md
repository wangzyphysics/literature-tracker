## Abstract
arXiv:2602.12037v1 Announce Type: new Abstract: Markov state models (MSMs) are a powerful tool to analyze and coarse-grain complex dynamical data into interpretable kinetic processes. This capability is particularly important in heterogeneous catalysis, where a medley of reactants and intermediates interact on surfaces that might simultaneously experience structural fluctuations. For these very complex systems, standard transition state theory (TST) approaches are no longer appropriate, motivating alternative approaches that can retain dynamical complexity while providing physical insight. With machine learned interatomic potentials being more and more ubiquitous, directly simulating complex catalytic systems with molecular dynamics (MD) is becoming increasingly feasible. Extending MSMs to dynamically coarse grain MD simulation data of catalytic processes, we analyze hydrogen dynamics on rhodium catalysts with slab and nanoparticle geometries over a range of hydrogen surface concentrations. Somewhat counterintuitively, nanoparticle features, such as corners and edges, effectively slow down the association/dissociation process, and the cooperative behavior of hydrogen-hydrogen interactions leads to a non-monotonic concentration dependence of the rates, which would not be predicted with standard TST.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2602.12037v1 公告类型：新 摘要：马尔可夫状态模型 (MSM) 是一种强大的工具，用于分析粗粒度复杂的动态数据并将其转化为可解释的动力学过程。这种能力在多相催化中尤其重要，其中反应物和中间体的混合物在可能同时经历结构波动的表面上相互作用。对于这些非常复杂的系统，标准过渡态理论（TST）方法不再适用，这就催生了可以保留动态复杂性同时提供物理洞察力的替代方法。随着机器学习的原子间势越来越普遍，利用分子动力学（MD）直接模拟复杂的催化系统变得越来越可行。我们将 MSM 扩展到催化过程的动态粗粒 MD 模拟数据，分析了在一定氢表面浓度范围内具有板状和纳米粒子几何形状的铑催化剂上的氢动力学。有点违反直觉的是，纳米粒子的特征，例如角和边缘，有效地减慢了缔合/解离过程，并且氢-氢相互作用的协同行为导致速率的非单调浓度依赖性，这是标准 TST 无法预测的。
