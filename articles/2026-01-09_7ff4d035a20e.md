## Abstract
We demonstrate reliable triple-level cell operation in HfZrO 2 -based ferroelectric memristors by precisely modulating partial-switching voltages to define eight distinct polarization states. Although stable multi-bit storage is achieved, endurance degradation arising from polarization fatigue remains a major limitation. To mitigate this issue, we introduce a domain-recovery strategy that employs interleaved high-voltage pulses combined with electrically quiescent break intervals. This approach restores polarization states by alleviating domain-wall pinning, enabling endurance exceeding 10 6 cycles. Notably, we find that spontaneous domain relaxation during short idle periods further contributes to fatigue recovery, whereas excessively long breaks induce domain disorientation. These findings reveal the critical roles of both electrical and time-controlled recovery in stabilizing ferroelectric switching behavior. Our results provide a practical route to enhancing the reliability of high-density ferroelectric memory and yield new physical insights into fatigue dynamics under multi-level operation.

我们通过精确调制部分切换电压来定义八种不同的极化状态，展示了 HfZrO 2 基铁电忆阻器中可靠的三级单元操作。尽管实现了稳定的多位存储，但极化疲劳引起的耐久性下降仍然是主要限制。为了缓解这个问题，我们引入了一种域恢复策略，该策略采用交错的高压脉冲与电气静态中断间隔相结合。这种方法通过减轻畴壁钉扎来恢复偏振态，从而实现超过 10 6 个周期的耐久性。值得注意的是，我们发现短暂空闲期间的自发域松弛进一步有助于疲劳恢复，而过长的休息会导致域定向障碍。这些发现揭示了电恢复和时间控制恢复在稳定铁电开关行为中的关键作用。我们的研究结果为提高高密度铁电存储器的可靠性提供了一条实用途径，并为多级操作下的疲劳动力学提供了新的物理见解。
