## Abstract
Alzheimer's disease (AD) is a neurodegenerative disorder caused by multiple factors that require the development of multi-target directed ligands (MTDL) to treat its pathophysiology. Using Hyperoside, a major Himalayan plant extract, this study will report on the potential use of this compound as a therapeutic agent for treating the main amyloidogenetic (amyloid precursor protein) and neurochemical (toxic metabolism of neurotransmitters) drivers of the progression of AD through reverse pharmacology and molecular modeling (in silico) docking studies. Hyperoside was identified as a potential inhibitor of BACE1 (1FKN) and MAO-B (2V5Z) through the use of in silico molecular docking studies, with reported binding affinities of -8.8 and -8.2 kcal/mol, respectively. Additionally, Hyperoside exhibited significant inhibitory potential against both beta-amyloid production and oxidative neurotransmitter degradation. To evaluate the drug-like properties and potential for pharmacokinetic use of Hyperoside as a potential treatment for AD, the lead compound underwent extensive ADMET profiling. The BOILED-Egg model (SwissADME) indicated Hyperoside had good gastrointestinal absorption and was not predicted to passively cross the blood-brain barrier (BBB) because of the compound's high Topological Polar Surface Area (TPSA = ~191 Å^2). In addition, molecular docking studies performed on the lead compound for its interaction with Human Serum Albumin (HSA) protein (1AO6) provided a docking score of -8.0 kcal/mol, indicating systemic transport via blood proteins is likely. These results indicate that the use of Hyperoside as an MTDL may provide a strong starting point for the development of a multi-targeted lead scaffold for the treatment of AD; however, in order for Hyperoside to be an effective treatment in the central nervous system, additional work may be needed to improve the delivery of Hyperoside into the CNS using nanocarrier techniques. The research presented here provides a comprehensive computational basis for confirming that Himalayan Traditional Medicine possesses qualities that may translate into the development of new treatment options for Alzheimer's disease.

## 摘要（中文）
阿尔茨海默病（AD）是一种由多种因素引起的神经退行性疾病，需要开发多靶点定向配体（MTDL）来治疗其病理生理学。本研究将使用金丝桃苷（一种主要的喜马拉雅植物提取物），通过反向药理学和分子建模（计算机模拟）对接研究，报告该化合物作为治疗剂的潜在用途，用于治疗 AD 进展的主要淀粉样蛋白生成（淀粉样前体蛋白）和神经化学物质（神经递质的毒性代谢）驱动因素。通过计算机分子对接研究，金丝桃苷被鉴定为 BACE1 (1FKN) 和 MAO-B (2V5Z) 的潜在抑制剂，据报道结合亲和力分别为 -8.8 和 -8.2 kcal/mol。此外，金丝桃苷对 β-淀粉样蛋白的产生和氧化神经递质的降解表现出显着的抑制潜力。为了评估金丝桃苷的药物样特性和药代动力学用途作为 AD 潜在治疗方法的潜力，对先导化合物进行了广泛的 ADMET 分析。煮鸡蛋模型 (SwissADME) 表明金丝桃苷具有良好的胃肠道吸收性，并且由于该化合物具有高拓扑极性表面积 (TPSA = ~191 Å^2)，因此预计不会被动穿过血脑屏障 (BBB)。此外，对先导化合物与人血清白蛋白 (HSA) 蛋白 (1AO6) 相互作用进行的分子对接研究提供了 -8.0 kcal/mol 的对接分数，表明可能通过血液蛋白进行全身转运。这些结果表明，使用金丝桃苷作为 MTDL 可能为开发治疗 AD 的多靶点先导支架提供强有力的起点；然而，为了使金丝桃苷成为中枢神经系统的有效治疗方法，可能需要进行额外的工作来改善使用纳米载体技术将金丝桃苷输送到中枢神经系统。这里介绍的研究提供了全面的计算基础，以确认喜马拉雅传统医学具有可转化为阿尔茨海默病新治疗方案开发的品质。
