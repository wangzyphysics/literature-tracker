## Abstract
arXiv:2601.03818v1 Announce Type: cross Abstract: Thermal electron measurements in space plasmas typically suffer at low energies from spacecraft emissions of photo- and secondary electrons and from charging of the spacecraft body. We examine these effects by use of numerical simulations in the context of electron measurements acquired by the Electron Analyser System (SWA-EAS) on board the Solar Orbiter mission. We employed the Spacecraft Plasma Interaction Software to model the interaction of the Solar Orbiter spacecraft with solar wind plasma and we implemented a virtual detector to simulate the measured electron energy spectra as observed in situ by the SWA-EAS experiment. Numerical simulations were set according to the measured plasma conditions at 0.3~AU. We derived the simulated electron energy spectra as detected by the virtual SWA-EAS experiment for different electron populations and compared these with both the initial plasma conditions and the corresponding real SWA-EAS data samples. We found qualitative agreement between the simulated and real data observed in situ by the SWA-EAS detector. Contrary to other space missions, the contamination by cold electrons emitted from the spacecraft is seen well above the spacecraft potential energy threshold. A detailed analysis of the simulated electron energy spectra demonstrates that contamination above the threshold is a result of cold electron fluxes emitted from distant spacecraft surfaces. The relative position of the break in the simulated spectrum with respect to the spacecraft potential slightly deviates from that in the real observations. This may indicate that the real potential of the SWA-EAS detector with respect to ambient plasma differs from the spacecraft potential value measured on board. The overall contamination is shown to be composed of emissions from a number of different sources and their relative contribution varies with the ambient plasma conditions.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2601.03818v1 公告类型：cross 摘要：空间等离子体中的热电子测量通常会受到航天器光电子和二次电子发射以及航天器主体充电的低能量影响。我们通过在太阳轨道飞行器任务上的电子分析系统（SWA-EAS）获得的电子测量的背景下使用数值模拟来检查这些影响。我们使用航天器等离子体相互作用软件来模拟太阳轨道航天器与太阳风等离子体的相互作用，并实现了一个虚拟探测器来模拟 SWA-EAS 实验现场观察到的测量电子能谱。根据测量的等离子体条件设定0.3~AU的数值模拟。我们导出了虚拟 SWA-EAS 实验检测到的不同电子群体的模拟电子能谱，并将其与初始等离子体条件和相应的真实 SWA-EAS 数据样本进行比较。我们发现 SWA-EAS 探测器现场观察到的模拟数据和真实数据之间存在定性一致性。与其他太空任务相反，航天器发射的冷电子造成的污染远高于航天器势能阈值。对模拟电子能谱的详细分析表明，高于阈值的污染是由遥远的航天器表面发射的冷电子通量造成的。模拟光谱中断裂相对于航天器电势的相对位置与实际观测中的位置略有偏差。这可能表明 SWA-EAS 探测器相对于环境等离子体的实际电势与在船上测量的航天器电势值不同。总体污染由许多不同来源的排放物组成，其相对贡献随周围等离子体条件的变化而变化。
