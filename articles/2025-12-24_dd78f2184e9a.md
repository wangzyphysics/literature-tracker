## Abstract
Solving the time-dependent Schrödinger equation (TDSE) is pivotal for modeling nonadiabatic electron dynamics, a key process in ultrafast spectroscopy and laser–matter interactions. However, exact solutions to the TDSE remain computationally prohibitive for most realistic systems, as the Hilbert space expands exponentially with dimensionality. In this work, we propose an approach integrating the stochastic representation framework with a neural network wavefunction ansatz, a flexible model capable of approximating time-evolving quantum wavefunctions. We first validate the method on one-dimensional, single-electron systems, focusing on ionization dynamics under intense laser fields, a critical process in attosecond physics. Our results demonstrate that the approach accurately reproduces key features of quantum evolution, including the energy and dipole evolution during ionization. We further show the feasibility of extending this approach to three-dimensional systems. Due to the increased complexity of real-time simulations in higher dimensions, these results remain at an early stage and highlight the need for more advanced stabilization strategies.

## 摘要（中文）
求解瞬态薛定谔方程 (TDSE) 对于非绝热电子动力学建模至关重要，而非绝热电子动力学是超快光谱和激光与物质相互作用的关键过程。然而，对于大多数现实系统来说，TDSE 的精确解在计算上仍然令人望而却步，因为希尔伯特空间随维数呈指数级扩展。在这项工作中，我们提出了一种将随机表示框架与神经网络波函数 ansatz 集成的方法，神经网络波函数 ansatz 是一种能够逼近时间演化量子波函数的灵活模型。我们首先在一维单电子系统上验证该方法，重点关注强激光场下的电离动力学，这是阿秒物理学的一个关键过程。我们的结果表明，该方法准确地再现了量子演化的关键特征，包括电离过程中的能量和偶极子演化。我们进一步展示了将这种方法扩展到三维系统的可行性。由于更高维度的实时模拟的复杂性增加，这些结果仍处于早期阶段，凸显了对更先进的稳定策略的需求。
