## Abstract
arXiv:2509.18080v2 Announce Type: replace-cross Abstract: Optical non-Gaussian states hold great promise as a pivotal resource for advanced optical quantum information processing and fault-tolerant long-distance quantum communication. Establishing their faithful transmission in a real-world communication channel, therefore, marks an important milestone. In this study, we experimentally demonstrate the distribution of such non-Gaussian states in a functioning telecommunication channel that connects separate buildings within the DTU campus premises. We send photon-subtracted squeezed states, exhibiting pronounced Wigner negativity, through 300 m of deployed optical fibers to a distant building. Using quantum homodyne tomography, we fully characterize the states upon arrival. Our results show the survival of the Wigner function negativity after transmission when correcting for detection losses, indicating that the established link can potentially facilitate the violation of Bell's inequality and enable quantum steering. This achievement not only validates the practical feasibility of distributing non-Gaussian states in real-world settings, but also provides an exciting impetus towards realizing fully coherent quantum networks for high-dimensional, continuous-variable quantum information processing.

arXiv:2509.18080v2 公告类型：replace-cross 摘要：光学非高斯态作为先进光学量子信息处理和容错长距离量子通信的关键资源具有巨大的前景。因此，在现实世界的通信渠道中建立忠实的传输标志着一个重要的里程碑。在这项研究中，我们通过实验证明了此类非高斯状态在连接 DTU 校园内不同建筑物的正常电信通道中的分布。我们通过 300 m 的部署光纤将光子压缩态（表现出明显的维格纳负性）发送到远处的建筑物。使用量子零差断层扫描，我们充分描述了到达时的状态。我们的结果表明，在校正检测损失时，传输后维格纳函数负性仍然存在，这表明所建立的链接可能会促进贝尔不等式的违反并实现量子转向。这一成果不仅验证了在现实环境中分布非高斯态的实际可行性，而且为实现高维、连续变量量子信息处理的完全相干量子网络提供了令人兴奋的推动力。
