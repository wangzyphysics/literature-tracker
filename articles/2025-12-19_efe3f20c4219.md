## Abstract
Author(s): Fabrizio Falasca This work argues, through simplified dynamical systems, that the skill of data-driven climate models critically depends on nonunique choices of coarse-grained representations and stochastic parametrizations. Reduced-order models, tailored to specific scales and processes, are highlighted as valid alternatives to general-purpose emulators. Linear-response theory is proposed as a principled framework to evaluate neural models beyond stationary statistics and to probe causal mechanisms. A “real-world” application is presented by constructing a coarse-grained data-driven model that can be used for causal inference through forced responses. [Phys. Rev. Research 7, 043314] Published Fri Dec 19, 2025
