## Abstract
Vitrimers are the new class of covalent adaptable networks (CANs) capable of topological rearrangement through dynamic bond exchange, enabling recyclability, self-healing and stress relaxation while maintaining network integrity. The ring opening of epoxides by carboxylic acids is a fundamental epoxy–acid esterification reaction underlying vitrimer network formation, yet its atomistic mechanism remains challenging to simulate as experimental time scales for crosslinking reactions in polymers range from minutes to hours—far beyond the reach of atomistic molecular dynamics (MD) simulations. In this work, we employ biased MD using the Collective Variables (COLVARS) module in LAMMPS combined with a reactive ReaxFF potential to promote the ring-opening reaction between Diglycidyl Ether of Bisphenol A (DGEBA) and oxalic acid. Three coupled collective variables were designed to represent epoxide C–O bond cleavage, C(epoxide)–O(acid) bond formation, and proton transfer between acid and epoxide oxygens. This external bias promotes nucleophilic addition of acid-derivedcarboxylate oxygen to the epoxide carbon, resulting in ring opening, followed by proton transfer to yield the β-hydroxy ester functionality characteristic of vitrimer networks. This method allows simulating cross-linking at realistic, low, temperatures which helps to mimic chemical reactions and avoids unwanted high-temperature side reactions. Since not all accelerated events lead to the desired product, careful selection of initial configurations, molecular orientations, and suitable external bias parameters increases the probability of meaningful simulation results. Furthermore, the stability of the resulting vitrimer was evaluated in the absence of any external bias, and its thermomechanical properties were subsequently analyzed. Results show that the biased MD approach successfully captures the atomistic steps of the esterification reaction and the resulting vitrimer network can be used for accurate prediction of thermomechanical properties.

## 摘要（中文）
Vitrimer 是新型共价适应性网络 (CAN)，能够通过动态键交换进行拓扑重排，实现可回收性、自我修复和应力松弛，同时保持网络完整性。羧酸对环氧化物的开环是 vitrimer 网络形成的基本环氧-酸酯化反应，但其原子机制仍然难以模拟，因为聚合物中交联反应的实验时间尺度从几分钟到几小时不等，远远超出了原子分子动力学 (MD) 模拟的范围。在这项工作中，我们利用 LAMMPS 中的集体变量 (COLVARS) 模块结合反应性 ReaxFF 电位，采用偏向 MD 来促进双酚 A 缩水甘油醚 (DGEBA) 和草酸之间的开环反应。设计了三个耦合的集体变量来表示环氧化物C-O键断裂、C（环氧化物）-O（酸）键形成以及酸和环氧化物氧之间的质子转移。这种外部偏压促进了酸衍生的羧酸盐氧与环氧化物碳的亲核加成，导致开环，随后进行质子转移以产生vitrimer网络的β-羟基酯官能团特征。该方法允许在真实的低温下模拟交联，这有助于模拟化学反应并避免不必要的高温副反应。由于并非所有加速事件都会产生所需的产物，因此仔细选择初始配置、分子取向和合适的外部偏置参数可以增加有意义的模拟结果的可能性。此外，在没有任何外部偏置的情况下评估了所得玻璃体的稳定性，并随后分析了其热机械性能。结果表明，偏向 MD 方法成功捕获了酯化反应的原子步骤，所得的 vitrimer 网络可用于准确预测热机械性能。
