## Abstract
arXiv:2505.10531v3 Announce Type: replace Abstract: We uncover a new class of dynamical quantum instability in driven magnets leading to emergent enhancement of antiferromagnetic correlations even for purely ferromagnetic microscopic couplings. A primary parametric amplification creates a frequency-tuned nested magnon distribution in momentum space, which seeds a secondary instability marked by the emergence of enhanced antiferromagnetic correlations, mirroring the instability of nested Fermi surfaces in electronic systems. In sharp contrast to the fermionic case, however, the magnon-driven instability is intrinsically non-equilibrium and fundamentally inaccessible in thermal physics. Its quantum mechanical origin sets it apart from classical instabilities such as Faraday and modulation instabilities, which underlie several instances of dynamical behavior observed in magnetic and cold-atom systems.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2505.10531v3 公告类型：替换 摘要：我们发现了驱动磁体中的一类新的动态量子不稳定性，导致反铁磁相关性的突然增强，甚至对于纯铁磁微观耦合也是如此。一次参数放大在动量空间中创建了频率调谐的嵌套磁振子分布，这引发了以增强的反铁磁相关性的出现为标志的二次不稳定性，反映了电子系统中嵌套费米表面的不稳定性。然而，与费米子情况形成鲜明对比的是，磁振子驱动的不稳定性本质上是非平衡的，并且在热物理学中根本上无法实现。它的量子力学起源使其有别于法拉第和调制不稳定性等经典不稳定性，后者是在磁和冷原子系统中观察到的动力学行为的几个实例的基础。
