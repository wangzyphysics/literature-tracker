## Abstract
arXiv:2512.13222v2 Announce Type: replace Abstract: Controlling the deposition of filaments, such as nanowires and nanotubes, from evaporating droplets is critical for the performance of emerging technologies like flexible sensors and printed electronics. The final deposit morphology strongly governs functional properties, such as electrical conductivity, yet remains challenging to control. In this work, we numerically investigate how filament length, stiffness, and concentration affect deposition patterns during the drying process. We compare reaction-limited and diffusion-limited evaporation regimes, demonstrating that their distinct velocity fields and flow magnitudes fundamentally alter filament arrangement. While diffusion-limited evaporation drives the ``coffee-ring effect", compromising network uniformity, reaction-limited evaporation suppresses edge accumulation, promoting centered conductive deposits. We map out the spatial variation of filament alignment - tangential at the contact line, radial in the intermediate region, and random near the center. Longer filaments tend to favour more tangential alignment overall and suppress edge accumulation. We find that by tuning the evaporation regime, filament deposition can lead to significantly lower percolation thresholds and significantly higher conductivity exponents. These results quantify the link between evaporation kinetics and microstructure, providing guidelines for optimizing conductive network formation in printed electronics.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2512.13222v2 公告类型：替换 摘要：控制蒸发液滴中的细丝（例如纳米线和纳米管）的沉积对于柔性传感器和印刷电子产品等新兴技术的性能至关重要。最终的沉积形态强烈控制着功能特性，例如电导率，但控制起来仍然具有挑战性。在这项工作中，我们通过数值研究了细丝长度、刚度和浓度如何影响干燥过程中的沉积模式。我们比较了反应限制和扩散限制的蒸发方式，证明它们不同的速度场和流量大小从根本上改变了灯丝的排列。虽然扩散限制蒸发驱动“咖啡环效应”，损害网络均匀性，但反应限制蒸发抑制边缘积累，促进中心导电沉积。我们绘制了细丝排列的空间变化 - 接触线处的切向、中间区域的径向和中心附近的随机。较长的细丝往往有利于总体上更切向排列并抑制边缘积累。我们发现，通过调整蒸发状态，细丝沉积可以显着降低渗滤阈值并显着提高电导率这些结果量化了蒸发动力学和微观结构之间的联系，为优化印刷电子产品中的导电网络形成提供了指导。
