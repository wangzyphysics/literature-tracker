## Abstract
Author(s): S. Mohanty, S. Guchhait, S. S. Islam, Surya P. Patra, M. P. Saravanan, J. A. Krieger, T. J. Hicken, H. Luetkens, D. T. Adroja, Gøran J. Nilsen, M. D. Le, and R. Nath Here, the authors report the magnetic properties, crystal electric field excitations, and spin dynamics of a distorted honeycomb magnet BiErGeO 5 . Inelastic neutron scattering and μSR experiments reveal the effect of crystal electric field leading to a Kramers doublet ground state and anisotropic interactions. Despite an anomaly at T N ≃ 0.4 K in heat capacity, μSR measurements rule out a magnetic long-range order, and instead suggest persistent spin fluctuations down to 30 mK, highlighting unconventional magnetism in this spin-orbit coupled honeycomb system.
