## Abstract
Quantitative Structure Activity Relationship (QSAR) remains an effective tool for early-stage chemical modelling and virtual screening in drug design. The advancements in this field are led by two core paradigms, 1) descriptor engineering, where complex fixed-length vectors of compounds are generated and conventional ML methods are applied to those representations and 2) raw chemical inputs (e.g., SMILES, 2D-graph) are provided to complex deep learning neural network models which construct their own internal representations of molecules to quantify their biological activity. Conventional ML uses simple, computationally efficient algorithms and achieves state of the art accuracy on dataset sizes typical of practical QSAR applications. NN-based ML may not be as accurate for these dataset sizes but is inherently flexible and can learn iteratively. Here we present the Tsetlin Machine as a new potential QSAR method - which combines the accuracy and easy-use of existing rule-based QSAR ML methods (e.g., RF and XGBoost) with the continuous learning aspect and flexibility of NN algorithms. The Tsetlin Machine uses teams of finite-state automata which capture frequent patterns as propositional logic (clauses) in a reinforcement learning based game. We compare the TM's performance to existing rule-based QSAR methods using a benchmarking pipeline developed by the Polaris group. It uses repeated measures ANOVA and Tukey’s HSD to measure the difference in mean performance distributions. Across all tested targets, compound groups and descriptor types, the TM consistently achieved high ROC AUC and PPV scores - making it well-suited for QSAR-based compound prioritization, especially in early stages of virtual screening pipelines. SOTA results were achieved with the TM for MOR (ROC-AUC = 0.87, PRC-AUC = 0.77) and CYPA4 (ROC-AUC = 0.92, PRC-AUC = 0.63), demonstrating comparable and in certain cases superior performance of TM over the established SOTA models. Additionally, this work contains practical guidelines for applying TMs to QSAR tasks including descriptor selection, model parameters and number of training epochs that were used to produce the reported SOTA results.

## 摘要（中文）
定量构效关系 (QSAR) 仍然是药物设计中早期化学建模和虚拟筛选的有效工具。该领域的进步由两个核心范式引领，1）描述符工程，其中生成化合物的复杂固定长度向量，并将传统的机器学习方法应用于这些表示；2）向复杂的深度学习神经网络模型提供原始化学输入（例如 SMILES、2D 图），该模型构建自己的分子内部表示以量化其生物活性。传统的 ML 使用简单、计算高效的算法，并在实际 QSAR 应用中典型的数据集大小上实现了最先进的精度。基于神经网络的机器学习对于这些数据集大小可能不那么准确，但本质上是灵活的并且可以迭代学习。在这里，我们将 Tsetlin 机器作为一种新的潜在 QSAR 方法提出，它将现有基于规则的 QSAR ML 方法（例如 RF 和 XGBoost）的准确性和易用性与 NN 算法的连续学习方面和灵活性结合起来。 Tsetlin 机器使用有限状态自动机团队，在基于强化学习的游戏中捕获频繁模式作为命题逻辑（子句）。我们使用 Polaris 小组开发的基准测试管道将 TM 的性能与现有的基于规则的 QSAR 方法进行比较。它使用重复测量方差分析和 Tukey 的 HSD 来衡量平均绩效分布的差异。在所有测试的目标、化合物组和描述符类型中，TM 始终获得高 ROC AUC 和 PPV 分数 - 使其非常适合基于 QSAR 的化合物优先级排序，尤其是在虚拟筛选流程的早期阶段。 MOR（ROC-AUC = 0.87，PRC-AUC = 0.77）和 CYPA4（ROC-AUC = 0.92，PRC-AUC = 0.63）的 TM 获得了 SOTA 结果，表明 TM 的性能与已建立的 SOTA 模型相当，并且在某些情况下更优越。此外，这项工作还包含将 TM 应用于 QSAR 任务的实用指南，包括描述符选择、模型参数和用于生成报告的 SOTA 结果的训练时期数。
