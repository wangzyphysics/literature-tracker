# Predicting magnetism with first-principles AI

利用第一原理 AI 预测磁性

## Abstract
arXiv:2602.09093v1 Announce Type: new Abstract: Computational discovery of magnetic materials remains challenging because magnetism arises from the competition between kinetic energy and Coulomb interaction that is often beyond the reach of standard electronic-structure methods. Here we tackle this challenge by directly solving the many-electron Schr\"odinger equation with neural-network variational Monte Carlo, which provides a highly expressive variational wavefunction for strongly correlated systems. Applying this technique to transition metal dichalcogenide moir\'e semicondutors, we predict itinerant ferromagnetism in WSe$_2$/WS$_2$ and an antiferromagnetic insulator in twisted $\Gamma$-valley homobilayer, using the same neural network without any physics input beyond the microscopic Hamiltonian. Crucially, both types of magnetic states are obtained from a single calculation within the $S_z=0$ sector, removing the need to compute and compare multiple $S_z$ sectors. This significantly reduces computational cost and paves the way for faster and more reliable magnetic material design.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2602.09093v1 公告类型：新 摘要：磁性材料的计算发现仍然具有挑战性，因为磁性是由动能和库仑相互作用之间的竞争产生的，而这通常超出了标准电子结构方法的范围。在这里，我们通过使用神经网络变分蒙特卡罗直接求解多电子 Schr\"odinger 方程来解决这一挑战，该方程为强相关系统提供了高度表达的变分波函数。将该技术应用于过渡金属二硫化物云纹半导体，我们预测了 WSe$_2$/WS$_2$ 中的流动铁磁性和扭曲 $\Gamma$-valley 同双层中的反铁磁绝缘体，使用至关重要的是，两种类型的磁性状态都是通过 $S_z=0$ 扇区内的一次计算获得的，无需计算和比较多个 $S_z$ 扇区，这显着降低了计算成本，并为更快、更可靠的磁性材料设计铺平了道路。
