## Abstract
A major challenge in chiral sensing technologies is the reliance on enantiopure materials to differentiate between enantiomers, as their interactions with a chiral system produce distinct detection signals. However, synthesizing and isolating enantiopure materials is often costly and time-consuming due to the difficulty of separating enantiomers. Here, we demonstrate that racemic materials can also function effectively as chiral sensors when the system exhibits non-inversion enantioselectivity. Fluorescent Cd(II) metal-organic frameworks (MOFs) were synthesised using enantiopure and racemic H8-BINOL-based ligands and their ability to enantioselectively sense aspartic acid dimethyl ester (Asp-diME) evaluated. Remarkably, the MOF synthesized from the racemic ligand retained measurable enantioselectivity, comparable to its enantiopure counterparts, highlighting its potential as a cost-effective and accessible alternative for chiral sensing. We further investigated the crystallization mechanism and found that secondary nucleation templated by the initial crystal plays a significant role in chiral induction during MOF formation, posing a challenge in obtaining a truly racemic MOF. These findings provide a promising strategy for designing chiral sensors based on racemic materials and underscore the importance of chiral induction in chiral MOF synthesis.

## 摘要（中文）
手性传感技术的一个主要挑战是依赖对映体纯材料来区分对映体，因为它们与手性系统的相互作用会产生不同的检测信号。然而，由于分离对映体的困难，合成和分离对映体纯材料通常成本高昂且耗时。在这里，我们证明，当系统表现出非反转对映选择性时，外消旋材料也可以有效地充当手性传感器。使用对映体纯和外消旋 H8-BINOL 基配体合成荧光 Cd(II) 金属有机框架 (MOF)，并评估其对映选择性检测天冬氨酸二甲酯 (Asp-diME) 的能力。值得注意的是，与对映体纯的对应物相比，由外消旋配体合成的 MOF 保留了可测量的对映选择性，突显了其作为具有成本效益且易于使用的手性传感替代品的潜力。我们进一步研究了结晶机制，发现以初始晶体为模板的二次成核在 MOF 形成过程中的手性诱导中起着重要作用，这对获得真正的外消旋 MOF 提出了挑战。这些发现为设计基于外消旋材料的手性传感器提供了一种有前途的策略，并强调了手性诱导在手性 MOF 合成中的重要性。
