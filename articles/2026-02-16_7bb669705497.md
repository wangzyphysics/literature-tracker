## Abstract
arXiv:2602.12378v1 Announce Type: new Abstract: We discuss electronic and magnetic properties of Na$_x$Fe$_{1/2}$Mn$_{1/2}$O$_2$, a promising Na-ion battery cathode material. Using x-ray Compton scattering, SQUID magnetometry, and density-functional-theory based modeling, we probe how electrons and spins evolve during sodiation. By comparing Compton profiles of sodiated and desodiated samples, we show that oxygen 2$p$ orbitals drive the redox process, while transition-metal 3$d$ electrons become more delocalized, explaining the metallic phase at $x=2/3$. These profile differences define a quantitative descriptor for the sodiation range associated with improved conductivity. Electron holes on oxygen, reflected in oxygen magnetization, confirm the important role of oxygen in the electrochemical activity of the cathode.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2602.12378v1 公告类型：新 摘要：我们讨论了 Na$_x$Fe$_{1/2}$Mn$_{1/2}$O$_2$ 的电子和磁性特性，这是一种很有前景的钠离子电池正极材料。使用 X 射线康普顿散射、SQUID 磁力测量和基于密度泛函理论的建模，我们探讨了电子和自旋在钠化过程中如何演化。通过比较钠化和去钠化样品的康普顿分布，我们表明氧 2$p$ 轨道驱动氧化还原过程，而过渡金属 3$d$ 电子变得更加离域，解释了 $x=2/3$ 处的金属相。这些分布差异定义了与改善的电导率相关的钠化范围的定量描述符。氧上的电子空穴反映在氧磁化强度中，证实了氧在阴极电化学活性中的重要作用。
