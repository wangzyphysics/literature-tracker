## Abstract
arXiv:2512.21490v1 Announce Type: new Abstract: Graphene oxide (GO) exhibits rich chemical heterogeneity that strongly influences its structural, thermal, and mechanical properties, yet quantitatively linking reduction chemistry to heat transport remains challenging. In this work, we develop a machine-learned neuroevolution potential (NEP) trained on an existing density functional theory dataset (\textit{Angew.\ Chem.\ Int.\ Ed.}, \textbf{63} , e202410088 (2024)), achieving reasonable accuracy at a computational cost much lower than the existing machine-learned and empirical potentials. Leveraging this potential, we perform large-scale molecular dynamics (MD) simulations to model the thermal reduction of GO across realistic structural domains. Using the homogeneous nonequilibrium MD method with a proper quantum-statistical correction scheme, we find that reduced GO exhibits strongly suppressed thermal conductivities, ranging from a few to tens of Wm$^{-1}$K$^{-1}$, substantially lower than pristine GO without defects and far below graphene. Moreover, the thermal conductivity of reduced GO increases moderately with increasing OH/O ratio, except at the highest oxidation level (O/C=0.5) where this trend inverts, while decreasing significantly with increasing O/C ratio, a trend strongly correlated with the fraction of recovered graphene-like structures. Our work provides a computationally tractable and predictive atomistic machine learning framework for exploring how chemical structure governs heat transport in heterogeneous carbon materials.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2512.21490v1 公告类型：新 摘要：氧化石墨烯 (GO) 表现出丰富的化学异质性，强烈影响其结构、热和机械性能，但定量地将还原化学与热传输联系起来仍然具有挑战性。在这项工作中，我们开发了一种在现有密度泛函理论数据集（\textit{Angew.\ Chem.\ Int.\ Ed.}、\textbf{63}、e202410088 (2024)）上训练的机器学习神经进化势（NEP），以远低于现有机器学习和经验势的计算成本实现了合理的精度。利用这一潜力，我们进行大规模分子动力学 (MD) 模拟，以模拟 GO 在实际结构域中的热还原。使用齐次非平衡MD方法和适当的量子统计校正方案，我们发现还原的GO表现出强烈抑制的热导率，范围从几个到几十Wm$^{-1}$K$^{-1}$，大大低于没有缺陷的原始GO，并且远低于石墨烯。此外，还原GO的热导率随着OH/O比的增加而适度增加，除了在最高氧化水平（O/C=0.5）时，这种趋势发生反转，同时随着O/C比的增加而显着下降，这一趋势与恢复的类石墨烯结构的比例密切相关。我们的工作提供了一个计算上易于处理和预测的原子机器学习框架，用于探索化学结构如何控制异质碳材料中的热传输。
