## Abstract
LC-HRMS is widely used in forensic toxicology for broad-scope screening. When a newly emerging or rarely encountered compound is tentatively identified, toxicologists must decide whether it may be relevant to the case and, if so, quantify it. Acquiring reference material for quantification is costly and time-consuming. A fast semi-quantitative estimate would help prioritize only compounds above the toxic threshold. This study presents a machine-learning framework that estimates drug concentrations in whole blood using molecular structure information and LC-HRMS signal intensities. Using a dataset of 191 drugs spiked into whole blood at multiple concentration levels, we trained and evaluated several machine-learning models. Standard models, including random forests, achieved moderate performance. In contrast, a recently published graph neural network (GNN) leveraging atomic features and global molecular properties consistently produced the highest accuracy. Under cross-validation, the GNN predicted signal-to-concentration ratio for 79\% of all molecules, corresponding to concentration estimates between 50-200\% of true value. Toxicological thresholds often span multiple orders of magnitude, making this precision acceptable for application. The GNN model was additionally evaluated on an external benchmark dataset of ionization efficiencies (logIE), where it outperformed the current state of the art. Overall, the results demonstrate the feasibility of using graph-based machine learning to estimate drug concentrations in whole blood without reference material for prioritization. This is a practical and implementation-ready machine learning tool that can support decision-making in toxicological evaluation, particularly for newly emerging or rarely encountered drugs. The GNN model is open source and the dataset used for training and testing the models is publicly available.

## 摘要（中文）
LC-HRMS 广泛用于法医毒理学中的广泛筛查。当初步鉴定出新出现的或罕见的化合物时，毒理学家必须确定它是否与案件相关，如果是，则对其进行量化。获取量化参考材料既昂贵又耗时。快速的半定量估计将有助于仅优先考虑高于毒性阈值的化合物。这项研究提出了一个机器学习框架，可使用分子结构信息和 LC-HRMS 信号强度来估计全血中的药物浓度。我们使用包含 191 种不同浓度水平的药物的数据集来训练和评估多个机器学习模型。标准模型，包括随机森林，取得了中等的性能。相比之下，最近发布的利用原子特征和全局分子特性的图神经网络（GNN）始终能够产生最高的准确度。在交叉验证下，GNN 预测了所有分子中 79% 的信号浓度比，对应于真实值的 50-200% 之间的浓度估计值。毒理学阈值通常跨越多个数量级，使得这种精度对于应用来说是可以接受的。 GNN 模型还根据电离效率 (logIE) 的外部基准数据集进行了评估，其性能优于当前的技术水平。总体而言，结果证明了使用基于图形的机器学习来估计全血中药物浓度的可行性，而无需参考材料进行优先级排序。这是一种实用且易于实施的机器学习工具，可以支持毒理学评估中的决策，特别是对于新出现的或罕见的药物。 GNN 模型是开源的，用于训练和测试模型的数据集是公开的。
