## Abstract
Author(s): Lin Huang, Joseph Barker, Lekshmi Kailas, Soumyarup Hait, Simon D. Connell, Gavin Burnell, and Christopher H. Marrows Magnetic domains are known to move along temperature gradients, a phenomenon for which various mechanisms have been theoretically proposed. Here, the authors use magnetic force microscopy to quantify this effect for domains in a perpendicularly magnetized Pt/CoB/Ir multilayer. The domains always moved towards the hottest region. Quantitative evaluation of the different effects that could drive the domain motion shows that entropic forces, related to the spatial gradient in domain wall energy, dominate over spin-transfer torques arising from currents of magnons or electron spins. [Phys. Rev. B 113, 014428] Published Tue Jan 20, 2026
