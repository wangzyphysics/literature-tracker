## Abstract
arXiv:2602.12737v1 Announce Type: new Abstract: The recent discovery of robust quantum anomalous Hall (QAH) effect in rhombohedral multilayer graphene (RMG) aligned with hexagonal boron nitride (hBN) has established a highly versatile platform for correlated topological matter. This review synthesizes the experimental and theoretical progress in understanding these interaction-driven topological phases. Experimentally, the landscape has rapidly expanded from initial Chern insulators in trilayer systems to fully quantized QAH states in thicker systems. Theoretically, it is believed that moir\'e potential and electron-electron interaction cooperate and produce the QAH effect in such systems. Theoretical calculations also bring interesting questions, such as the formation of an interaction-driven topological phase known as an anomalous Hall crystal (AHC). This review comprehensively covers the experimental hallmarks, the theoretical frameworks, including continuum models and many-body approaches, and the ensuing physical picture that reconciles the roles of interactions, displacement fields, and the moir\'e potentials. We conclude by outlining outstanding open questions and future directions, positioning RMG/hBN systems at the forefront of topological quantum matter.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2602.12737v1 公告类型：新 摘要：最近在与六方氮化硼 (hBN) 对齐的菱形多层石墨烯 (RMG) 中发现了鲁棒的量子反常霍尔 (QAH) 效应，为相关拓扑物质建立了一个高度通用的平台。这篇综述综合了理解这些相互作用驱动的拓扑相方面的实验和理论进展。在实验上，情况已经从三层系统中最初的陈绝缘体迅速扩展到更厚系统中的完全量化的 QAH 状态。理论上，人们相信莫尔势和电子-电子相互作用在此类系统中协同产生QAH效应。理论计算也带来了有趣的问题，例如称为反常霍尔晶体（AHC）的相互作用驱动的拓扑相的形成。这篇综述全面涵盖了实验标志、理论框架，包括连续介质模型和多体方法，以及随后协调相互作用、位移场和莫尔势的作用的物理图片。最后，我们概述了悬而未决的问题和未来方向，将 RMG/hBN 系统定位在拓扑量子物质的前沿。
