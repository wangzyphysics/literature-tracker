## Abstract
arXiv:2508.09551v2 Announce Type: replace Abstract: We theoretically investigate the superconducting phase and perpendicular Josephson supercurrent in twisted three-dimensional (3D) superconductors, where two layered 3D materials are stacked with a relative twist. We formulate the Bogoliubov-de Gennes Hamiltonian and develop a self-consistent method to calculate the superconducting order parameter and the resulting supercurrent. Applying this framework to a toy model with Fermi surfaces located near the Brillouin zone corners, we demonstrate a phase discontinuity at the twisted interface, indicating that a Josephson junction is formed purely by the twist. Our calculations reveal that the interface supports a finite critical current even when the Fermi surfaces of the two superconductors are completely separated, unlike in the case of a twisted normal-metal interface. We further show that the critical current can be effectively controlled by the twist angle, transitioning from a high-transparency regime at small angles to a low-transparency regime at larger angles.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2508.09551v2 公告类型：替换 摘要：我们从理论上研究了扭曲三维 (3D) 超导体中的超导相和垂直约瑟夫森超电流，其中两层 3D 材料以相对扭曲的方式堆叠在一起。我们制定了 Bogoliubov-de Gennes 哈密顿量，并开发了一种自洽方法来计算超导序参数和由此产生的超电流。将此框架应用于费米面位于布里渊区角附近的玩具模型，我们证明了扭曲界面处的相位不连续性，表明约瑟夫森结纯粹是由扭曲形成的。我们的计算表明，即使两个超导体的费米表面完全分离，该界面也能支持有限的临界电流，这与扭曲的普通金属界面不同。我们进一步表明，临界电流可以通过扭转角有效控制，从小角度的高透明度状态过渡到大角度的低透明度状态。
