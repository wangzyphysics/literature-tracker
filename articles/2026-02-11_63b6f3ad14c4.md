## Abstract
arXiv:2508.13640v2 Announce Type: replace Abstract: Optical metrology has progressed beyond the Abbe-Rayleigh limit, unlocking (sub)atomic precision by leveraging nonlinear phenomena, statistical accumulation, and AI estimators trained on measurand variations. Here, we show that Fisher information, which defines the fundamental precision limit, can be viewed as a physical entity that propagates through space, and we derive a wave equation for sensitivity fields describing its flow, which can resonate, diffract, and interfere. We reveal how material composition, geometry, and environmental design dictate where information is generated and how it travels, analogous to antennas and metasurfaces sculpting electromagnetic energy. Plasmonic and dielectric resonances enhance information flow, while gratings and near-field structures reshape radiation patterns. This perspective reframes metrology as a discipline in which resolution can be engineered by tailoring information sources and flow for applications in atomic-scale diagnostics and beyond, including optimisation of Light Detection and Ranging (LiDAR), remote sensing, and radar technologies.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2508.13640v2 公告类型：替换 摘要：光学计量学已经超越了阿贝-瑞利极限，通过利用非线性现象、统计累积和经过被测量变化训练的人工智能估计器来解锁（亚）原子精度。在这里，我们证明了定义基本精度极限的费希尔信息可以被视为通过空间传播的物理实体，并且我们推导了描述其流动的敏感场的波动方程，它可以共振、衍射和干涉。我们揭示了材料成分、几何形状和环境设计如何决定信息在何处生成以及如何传播，类似于天线和超表面塑造电磁能。等离子体和介电共振增强了信息流，而光栅和近场结构重塑了辐射模式。这种观点将计量学重新定义为一门学科，在该学科中，可以通过定制信息源和流程来设计分辨率，以应用于原子级诊断及其他应用，包括光探测和测距（LiDAR）、遥感和雷达技术的优化。
