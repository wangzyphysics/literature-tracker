## Abstract
arXiv:2510.21226v2 Announce Type: replace Abstract: We propose a coupled dynamical model of resource allocation and epidemic spread, inspired by the hierarchical structure of real-world therapeutic resource allocation. In this framework, network nodes are assigned distinct roles as either resource allocators or resource recipients. As the average number of links per recipient from allocators increases, the prevalence exhibits one of four distinct response patterns across conditions: monotonically increasing, monotonically decreasing, U-shaped trend, or a sudden decrease with large fluctuations. A mechanistic analysis uncovers three central insights: (i) a trade-off between efficient resource allocation and infection risk faced by allocators, (ii) the critical need to avoid resource redundancy when therapeutic efficiency is high, and (iii) the emergence of cascade-induced bistability in the coupled system.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2510.21226v2 公告类型：替换 摘要：受现实世界治疗资源分配的层次结构的启发，我们提出了资源分配和流行病传播的耦合动态模型。在此框架中，网络节点被分配不同的角色，作为资源分配者或资源接收者。随着来自分配器的每个接收者的平均链接数增加，流行度在不同条件下表现出四种不同的响应模式之一：单调增加、单调减少、U 形趋势或伴随大波动的突然减少。机制分析揭示了三个核心见解：（i）有效资源分配和分配者面临的感染风险之间的权衡，（ii）当治疗效率高时避免资源冗余的迫切需要，以及（iii）耦合系统中级联引起的双稳态的出现。
