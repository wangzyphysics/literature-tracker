## Abstract
Large language modules (LLMs) such as Chat-GPT have been widely adopted by chemistry undergraduate university students as a learning tool, but few methods exist to measure the scope of their influence on essay writing. This report introduces a protocol based on monitoring the frequency of specific words used excessively by LLMs amongst a sample of around 1000 chemistry student essays between 2018 and 2025. More than 50 key words known to be favored by LLMs were found to have simultaneously increased in frequency amongst essays submitted during the last two years. When the lists of key words were applied to specific essays, the findings indicated 13-29% of essays submitted during 2025 relied heavily on text generated by LLMs. This protocol offers a simple method to generate an approximate scope of LLM uptake amongst a cohort, with potential applications in defining higher education AI policy and assessment strategies.

## 摘要（中文）
Chat-GPT 等大型语言模块 (LLM) 已被化学本科生广泛采用作为学习工具，但很少有方法来衡量其对论文写作的影响范围。本报告介绍了一项协议，该协议基于监测法学硕士在 2018 年至 2025 年间约 1000 篇化学学生论文样本中过度使用特定单词的频率。发现在过去两年提交的论文中，已知超过 50 个受法学硕士青睐的关键词的频率同时增加。当将关键词列表应用于特定论文时，研究结果表明，2025 年期间提交的论文中有 13-29% 严重依赖于法学硕士生成的文本。该协议提供了一种简单的方法来生成队列中 LLM 吸收的大致范围，在定义高等教育人工智能政策和评估策略方面具有潜在的应用。
