## Abstract
Author(s): Nabil Atlam, Swati Chaudhary, Arpit Raj, Matthew Matzelle, Barun Ghosh, Gregory A. Fiete, and Arun Bansil The bulk photogalvanic effect generates direct electrical current from incident light. Here, the authors demonstrate that a bilayer graphene film with an imprinted electric superlattice potential can produce a large and tunable photogalvanic current that surpasses previous predictions for twisted bilayer graphene. This response occurs in the far-infrared regime and it can be electrically controlled by tuning both the displacement field and the superlattice moiré potential strength, opening new pathways for applications including infrared sensors and detectors. [Phys. Rev. B 112, 245409] Published Tue Dec 09, 2025
