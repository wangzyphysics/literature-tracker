## Abstract
arXiv:2601.10884v2 Announce Type: replace Abstract: 'Closeness' is well-defined as a quantitative measure of centrality in social network analysis (SNA), but it is not as well defined qualitatively as a description of social relationships. This paper presents a qualitative analysis of 'closeness' as it is defined both implicitly and explicitly in interviews with 100 women and/or LGBTQ+ PhD physicists. The interviews include a social network construction component, and we define a quantitative network parameter that serves as a proxy for closeness, which we examine in relation to attributes of network members. We find that physicists in this sample see trust, relaxed boundaries, reliance, and support as concepts that most directly define closeness in their relationships. Consistent interaction, positive affect, and commonalities are also often present in (and in some cases, defining of) these relationships. From the quantitative analysis, we find that these physicists tend to view family and partners, friends, and professional friends as comprising their closest relationships. These results are consistent with other studies which have sought to define closeness qualitatively, but the prevalence of trust in this dataset in particular suggests that these physicists see the ability to confide as uniquely important in their relationships. We make recommendations to institutions supporting and employing physicists on how to better support gender and sexual minority (GSM) physicists going forward, and we believe these results help us gain a better understanding of how GSM physicists find success in physics despite the barriers they face due to their identities.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2601.10884v2 公告类型：替换 摘要：“亲密度”在社交网络分析（SNA）中被明确定义为中心性的定量度量，但它在定性上的定义不如社会关系的描述那么明确。本文对 100 名女性和/或 LGBTQ+ 博士物理学家的访谈中隐含和明确定义的“亲密度”进行了定性分析。访谈包括社交网络构建部分，我们定义了一个定量网络参数，作为亲密程度的代理，我们根据网络成员的属性来检查该参数。我们发现，该样本中的物理学家将信任、宽松的界限、依赖和支持视为最直接定义其关系亲密程度的概念。这些关系中也经常存在一致的互动、积极的情感和共性（在某些情况下，定义这些关系）。从定量分析中，我们发现这些物理学家倾向于将家人和合作伙伴、朋友和职业朋友视为他们最亲密的关系。这些结果与其他试图定性定义亲密关系的研究一致，但该数据集中普遍存在的信任表明，这些物理学家认为倾诉的能力在他们的关系中尤为重要。我们就如何更好地支持性别和性少数（GSM）物理学家的未来向支持和雇用物理学家的机构提出建议，我们相信这些结果有助于我们更好地了解 GSM 物理学家如何在物理学领域取得成功，尽管他们因身份而面临障碍。
