## Abstract
arXiv:2602.13765v1 Announce Type: new Abstract: The efficient manipulation of thermodynamic states within the finite time is fundamentally constrained by the intrinsic dissipative cost. While the slow-driving regime is well-characterized by a universal $1/\tau$-scaling of irreversibility, the physics governing fast, non-adiabatic transitions remains elusive. Here, we propose the polytropic steering protocols that provide an exact analytical bridge between the isothermal and adiabatic limits for Brownian particles far-from-equilibrium. We demonstrate that for any protocol duration $\tau$, the system can be precisely steered along a prescribed polytropic trajectory, revealing a striking non-monotonic dependence of irreversibility on the driving rate. Contrary to the near-equilibrium paradigm where faster driving necessitates higher energetic costs, we identify a most-irreversible timescale, beyond which dissipation is anomalously suppressed by rapid driving. By mapping these protocols onto a broad class of controllable thermodynamic cycle, we establish power-efficiency tradeoffs and position the polytropic index as a genuine thermodynamic control knob for the rational design of high-speed, high-performance microscopic thermal machines.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2602.13765v1 公告类型：新 摘要：有限时间内热力学状态的有效操纵从根本上受到内在耗散成本的限制。虽然慢速驱动机制的特点是普遍的不可逆性 $1/\tau$ 尺度，但控制快速、非绝热转变的物理原理仍然难以捉摸。在这里，我们提出了多方转向协议，该协议为远离平衡的布朗粒子的等温和绝热极限之间提供了精确的分析桥梁。我们证明，对于任何协议持续时间$\tau$，系统都可以沿着规定的多方轨迹精确引导，揭示了不可逆性对驱动速率的惊人的非单调依赖性。与接近平衡范式相反，更快的驾驶需要更高的能量成本，我们确定了一个最不可逆的时间尺度，超过这个时间尺度，耗散会被快速驾驶异常抑制。通过将这些协议映射到广泛的可控热力学循环上，我们建立了功率效率权衡，并将多变指数定位为真正的热力学控制旋钮，用于合理设计高速、高性能的微观热机。
