## Abstract
arXiv:2510.11730v2 Announce Type: replace-cross Abstract: This study demonstrates the first realization of wireless strain, temperature and crack growth sensing within 3D-printed metallic structures using standard electromagnetic inspection hardware. This establishes a path toward need-based maintenance for parts operating in harsh environments driven by accurate, real-time damage assessments instead of relying on regularly scheduled maintenance teardowns. To this end, we encapsulate and embed magnetoelastic and thermomagnetic materials during additive manufacturing. Mechanical and thermal stimuli affect the magnetic permeability of the embedded materials, which modulates the flux through a coil placed on or near the part's surface. We demonstrate strain sensing accurate to +/-27x10-6 and temperature sensing accurate to +/-0.75 oC. We highlight these sensors' capabilities by detecting the onset of plasticity and fatigue-driven crack growth thousands of cycles before critical failure.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2510.11730v2 公告类型：replace-cross 摘要：这项研究首次展示了使用标准电磁检测硬件在 3D 打印金属结构中实现无线应变、温度和裂纹扩展传感。这为在恶劣环境中运行的零件建立了一条基于需求的维护途径，通过准确、实时的损坏评估来驱动，而不是依赖于定期安排的维护拆卸。为此，我们在增材制造过程中封装并嵌入磁弹性和热磁材料。机械和热刺激会影响嵌入材料的磁导率，从而调节通过放置在零件表面或附近的线圈的通量。我们展示了精确至 +/-27x10-6 的应变传感和精确至 +/-0.75 oC 的温度传感。我们通过在严重故障发生前数千个周期检测塑性和疲劳驱动的裂纹扩展的发生来强调这些传感器的功能。
