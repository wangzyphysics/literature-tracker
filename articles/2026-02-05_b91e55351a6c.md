## Abstract
arXiv:2512.19051v3 Announce Type: replace-cross Abstract: A recent experiment by Sharoglazova {\it et al.} was interpreted by the authors as a challenge to the basic idea of Bohmian mechanics, the phase-velocity relation $\vec{v}(\vec{r},t)=\frac{\hbar}{m}\vec{\nabla}S(\vec{r},t)$. Here, we reinterpret their experiment, highlighting the role of the transient regime before the onset of the stationary state. In this way, we show that the experimental data can naturally fit the Bohmian framework when the energy in the evanescent regime is identified with the quantum potential. It is, however, also possible to interpret the same energy as a kinetic energy, in the framework of Nelson's stochastic quantum mechanics. In conclusion, far from challenging the Bohmian mechanics, we find that the experimental results of Sharoglazova {\it et al.} can be interpreted in either ontologies and are therefore not conclusive in selecting one or the other.
