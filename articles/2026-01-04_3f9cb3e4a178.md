## Abstract
Lead-free potassium-sodium niobate (K0.5Na0.5NbO₃, KNN) ceramics represent a promising alternative to replace lead-based piezoelectrics in energy harvesting and actuator applications due to their high Curie temperature and favorable ferroelectric properties. This study investigates the structural, dielectric, ferroelectric, and piezoelectric properties of pure KNN and multi-element doped compositions: (K0.4Na0.48Bi0.1La0.02)(Nb0.9Ti0.1)O3 (KNN-BiLaTi) and (K0.4Na0.56La0.02Y0.02)(Nb0.9V0.05Sb0.05)O3 (KNN-LaYVSb), synthesized via conventional solid-state methods. Structural analysis (XRD/Rietveld) revealed single-phase orthorhombic perovskite structures (space group Amm2) for all samples, with no detectable secondary phases. XPS, Raman, SEM, and TEM revealed that the dopants tune defect chemistry, suppress surface alkali degradation, and refine the microstructure, especially in dense, low-porosity KNN-LaYVSb, which has pronounced lattice distortion for favorable domain wall motion. Dielectric experiments demonstrated lower orthorhombic–tetragonal transition temperatures and wider, more diffuse phase transitions. However, Curie temperatures remained high, indicating reliable operation over a wide range of temperatures. At optimal poling, KNN-LaYVSb attained the highest piezoelectric coefficient (d₃₃ = 221 pC/N) and excellent thermal stability up to 275 °C, retaining over 90% of room-temperature value and over 80% at elevated temperatures near 450 °C. The improved temperature stability arises from diffuse phase transitions, defect-dipole stabilization, and nanoscale heterogeneity due to multi-element doping. The remnant polarization increased from 3.49 µC/cm² (Pure-KNN) to 25.3 µC/cm² (KNN-LaYVSb) with significantly reduced coercive field (6.10 kV/cm). These findings illustrate that selective multi-element co-doping at A- and B-sites effectively modifies polymorphic phase boundaries near room temperature by improving defect chemistry through controlled vacancy formation and optimizing microstructure. This results in robust, high-performance lead-free piezoceramics for advanced energy harvesting and sensing applications operating across wide temperature ranges.

## 摘要（中文）
无铅铌酸钾钠 (K0.5Na0.5NbO₃，KNN) 陶瓷由于其高居里温度和良好的铁电特性，成为能量收集和执行器应用中替代铅基压电材料的有前景的替代品。本研究研究了纯 KNN 和多元素掺杂成分的结构、介电、铁电和压电特性：(K0.4Na0.48Bi0.1La0.02)(Nb0.9Ti0.1)O3 (KNN-BiLaTi) 和 (K0.4Na0.56La0.02Y0.02)(Nb0.9V0.05Sb0.05)O3 (KNN-LaYVSb)，通过传统固态方法合成。结构分析 (XRD/Rietveld) 显示所有样品均为单相斜方钙钛矿结构（Amm2 空间群），没有可检测到的第二相。 XPS、拉曼、SEM 和 TEM 揭示了掺杂剂可调节缺陷化学性质、抑制表面碱降解并细化微观结构，特别是在致密、低孔隙率的 KNN-LaYVSb 中，其具有明显的晶格畸变，有利于磁畴壁运动。介电实验证明了较低的斜方-四方转变温度和更宽、更扩散的相变。然而，居里温度仍然很高，表明在很宽的温度范围内都能可靠运行。在最佳极化下，KNN-LaYVSb 获得了最高的压电系数（d₃₃ = 221 pC/N）和高达 275 °C 的出色热稳定性，保留了超过 90% 的室温值，在接近 450 °C 的高温下保留了超过 80%。温度稳定性的改善源于多元素掺杂导致的扩散相变、缺陷偶极子稳定性和纳米级异质性。残余极化从 3.49 µC/cm²（Pure-KNN）增加到 25.3 µC/cm²（KNN-LaYVSb），矫顽场显着降低（6.10 kV/cm）。这些发现表明，A 位和 B 位的选择性多元素共掺杂可通过控制空位形成和优化微观结构来改善缺陷化学，从而有效地改变室温附近的多晶型相边界。这产生了坚固、高性能的无铅压电陶瓷，适用于在宽温度范围内运行的先进能量收集和传感应用。
