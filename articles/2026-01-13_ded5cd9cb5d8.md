## Abstract
arXiv:2511.06869v2 Announce Type: replace Abstract: Understanding the sodium-storage mechanism in hard carbon (HC) anodes is crucial for advancing sodium-ion battery (SIB) technology. However, the intrinsic complexity of HC microstructures and their interactions with sodium remain not fully elucidated. We present a multiscale methodology that integrates grand-canonical Monte Carlo (GCMC) simulations with a machine-learning interatomic potential based on the Gaussian approximation potential (GAP) framework to investigate sodium insertion mechanisms in hard carbons with different levels of porosity, achieved by simulating structural models with densities ranging from 0.7 to 1.9 g cm$^{-3}$. Structural and thermodynamic analyses reveal the interplay between pore size and accessibility and the relative contributions of adsorption, intercalation, and pore filling to the overall storage capacity. Low-density carbons favor pore-filling, achieving extremely high capacities at near-zero voltages, whereas high-density carbons primarily store sodium through adsorption and intercalation, leading to lower but more stable capacities. Intermediate-density carbons ($1.3-1.6$ g cm$^{-3}$) provide the most balanced performance, combining moderate capacity (480 and 310 mAh g$^{-1}$), safe operating voltages, and minimal volume expansion ($<10$\%). These findings establish a direct correlation between carbon density and electrochemical behavior, providing atomic-scale insight into how hard carbon morphology governs sodium-storage. The proposed framework offers a rational design principle for optimizing HC-based SIB anodes toward high energy density and long-term cycling stability.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2511.06869v2 公告类型：替换 摘要：了解硬碳 (HC) 阳极的钠存储机制对于推进钠离子电池 (SIB) 技术至关重要。然而，HC 微观结构的内在复杂性及其与钠的相互作用仍未完全阐明。我们提出了一种多尺度方法，将大正则蒙特卡罗 (GCMC) 模拟与基于高斯近似势 (GAP) 框架的机器学习原子间势相结合，以研究不同孔隙率硬碳中的钠插入机制，通过模拟密度范围为 0.7 至 1.9 g cm$^{-3}$ 的结构模型来实现。结构和热力学分析揭示了孔径和可及性之间的相互作用以及吸附、插层和孔隙填充对总体存储容量的相对贡献。低密度碳有利于孔隙填充，在接近零电压下实现极高的容量，而高密度碳主要通过吸附和插层来存储钠，导致容量较低但更稳定。中等密度碳 ($1.3-1.6$ g cm$^{-3}$) 提供最平衡的性能，结合了中等容量 (480 和 310 mAh g$^{-1}$)、安全工作电压和最小的体积膨胀 ($<10$\%)。这些发现建立了碳密度和电化学行为之间的直接相关性，为硬碳形态如何控制钠存储提供了原子尺度的见解。所提出的框架为优化基于HC的SIB阳极以实现高能量密度和长期循环稳定性提供了合理的设计原则。
