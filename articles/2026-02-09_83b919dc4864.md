## Abstract
arXiv:2602.06143v1 Announce Type: new Abstract: Understanding how an instructional approach shapes student's cognitive resources and engagement is central to improving undergraduate physics education especially for novice learners. This study examines how three instructional modalities (Inquiry-based, Design-based, and Game-based learning) affect cognitive load and situational interest in physics laboratories for non-STEM majors. Guided by the revised Cognitive Load Theory framework, two experiments were conducted across two physics domains: mechanics and electrical circuits. In each experiment, students completed three laboratory sessions, one in each instructional modality, followed by surveys measuring cognitive load and situational interest. One-way ANOVA analyses revealed significant differences across the three modalities in both experiments. Game-based laboratories consistently yielded the lowest cognitive load and the highest situational interest, while inquiry-based and design-based labs imposed higher cognitive demands, with their relative effects varying by domain. Overall, situational interest exhibited an inverse relationship with cognitive load, suggesting that reduced cognitive demands support greater engagement. These findings emphasize the value of strategically selecting and combining instructional modalities to balance cognitive load and foster meaningful engagement in physics laboratories for novice learners.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2602.06143v1 公告类型：新 摘要：了解教学方法如何塑造学生的认知资源和参与度对于改善本科物理教育（特别是对于新手学习者）至关重要。本研究探讨了三种教学模式（基于探究、基于设计和基于游戏的学习）如何影响非 STEM 专业物理实验室的认知负荷和情境兴趣。在修订后的认知负荷理论框架的指导下，在机械和电路两个物理领域进行了两项实验。在每个实验中，学生完成了三个实验室课程，每种教学模式各一个，然后进行测量认知负荷和情景兴趣的调查。单向方差分析揭示了两项实验中三种模式的显着差异。基于游戏的实验室始终产生最低的认知负荷和最高的情境兴趣，而基于探究和基于设计的实验室提出了更高的认知要求，其相对效果因领域而异。总体而言，情景兴趣与认知负荷呈反比关系，这表明认知需求的减少支持更大的参与度。这些发现强调了战略性选择和组合教学方式的价值，以平衡认知负荷并促进新手学习者有意义地参与物理实验室。
