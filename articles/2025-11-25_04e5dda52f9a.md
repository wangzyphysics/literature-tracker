## Abstract
Author(s): L. Beaudoin, A. Verrier, Y.A. Bioud, M. Massicotte, B. Reulet, and J.A. Quilliam Nuclear magnetic resonance is a valuable technique for studying a wide variety of quantum materials, but the small magnetic moments involved make it particularly challenging to apply to thin films or two-dimensional materials. This work implements a superconducting meander-line surface coil that achieves a high filling factor, even for very thin samples, and enables one to obtain an NMR signal and perform spin-echo measurements on a sample of boron just 150 nm thick. The article also provides a roadmap for improving this approach to achieve measurements in the single-layer limit, which is quite promising for the study of magnetism and correlated electrons in two-dimensional systems. [Phys. Rev. Applied 24, 054076] Published Tue Nov 25, 2025
