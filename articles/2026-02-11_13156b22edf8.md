## Abstract
arXiv:2509.16243v2 Announce Type: replace Abstract: The Any Light Particle Search II (ALPS II) is a light shining through a wall experiment probing the existence of axions and axion-like particles using a 1064 nm laser source. While ALPS II is already taking data using a heterodyne based detection scheme, cryogenic transition edge sensor (TES) based single-photon detectors are planned to expand the detection system for cross-checking the potential signals, for which a sensitivity on the order of $10^{-24}$ W is required. In order to reach this goal, we have investigated the use of convolutional neural networks (CNN) as binary classifiers to distinguish the experimentally measured 1064 nm photon triggered (light) pulses from background (dark) pulses. Despite extensive hyperparameter optimization, the CNN based binary classifier did not outperform our previously optimized cut-based analysis in terms of detection significance. This suggests that the used approach is not generally suitable for background suppression and improving the energy resolution of the TES. We partly attribute this to the training confusion induced by near-1064 nm black-body photon triggers in the background, which we identified as the limiting background source as concluded in our previous works. However, we argue that the problem ultimately lies in the binary classification based approach and believe that regression models would be better suitable for addressing the energy resolution. Unsupervised machine learning models, in particular neural network based autoencoders, should also be considered potential candidates for the suppression of noise in time traces. While the presented results and associated conclusions are obtained for TES designed to be used in the ALPS II experiment, they should hold equivalently well for any device whose output signal can be considered as a univariate time trace.

arXiv:2509.16243v2 公告类型：替换 摘要：任意光粒子搜索 II (ALPS II) 是一项光透过墙壁的实验，使用 1064 nm 激光源探测轴子和类轴子粒子的存在。虽然 ALPS II 已经使用基于外差的检测方案获取数据，但计划使用基于低温跃迁边缘传感器 (TES) 的单光子探测器来扩展检测系统以交叉检查潜在信号，为此需要 10^{-24}$ W 数量级的灵敏度。为了实现这一目标，我们研究了使用卷积神经网络 (CNN) 作为二元分类器来区分实验测量的 1064 nm 光子触发（光）脉冲和背景（暗）脉冲。尽管进行了广泛的超参数优化，但基于 CNN 的二元分类器在检测显着性方面并未优于我们之前优化的基于剪切的分析。这表明所使用的方法通常不适合背景抑制和提高 TES 的能量分辨率。我们部分地将其归因于背景中近 1064 nm 黑体光子触发器引起的训练混乱，正如我们在之前的工作中得出的结论，我们将其确定为限制背景源。然而，我们认为问题最终在于基于二元分类的方法，并相信回归模型更适合解决能量分辨率问题。无监督机器学习模型，特别是基于神经网络的自动编码器，也应被视为抑制时间轨迹中噪声的潜在候选模型。虽然所提出的结果和相关结论是针对设计用于 ALPS II 实验的 TES 获得的，但对于输出信号可被视为单变量时间轨迹的任何设备来说，它们应该同样适用。
