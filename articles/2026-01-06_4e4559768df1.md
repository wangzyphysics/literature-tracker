## Abstract
arXiv:2601.02187v1 Announce Type: new Abstract: We develop a fractional-order Ginzburg-Landau (GL) framework for nonreciprocal superconducting transport in Josephson junctions formed by fractal superconductors or superconducting media with nonlocal correlations, separated by a noncentrosymmetric normal layer. We show that nonreciprocity and the superconducting diode effect arise from the interplay between the Lifshitz invariant and fractional kinetics, with the latter serving as an effective, symmetry-consistent representation of fractal geometry and finite-range memory. Two complementary approaches are pursued. In a fractional integral GL formulation, spatial integration on a fractal space yields analytic solutions and reveals how rectification scales with the dimensionality of the fractal media and the strength of the Lifshitz-like drift. In a fractional derivative-based formulation derived via the Agrawal variational principle with left/right Caputo operators, we obtain a gauge-invariant free energy, the corresponding GL equations, and a current density. We use fractional orders as effective parameters that represent nonlocal and memory effects induced by fractal microstructure. Within a two-mode plane-wave approximation we derive a compact current-phase relation and an expression for the diode efficiency, and we map the rectification amplitude across the fractional kinetic and the Lifshitz/memory order. An exact single-sided solution in terms of Prabhakar functions further confirms robust, tunable nonreciprocity, including a near-ideal diode response. This identifies a pathway to near-perfect superconducting diodes by engineering fractal (fractional-kinetic) transport achieved by tuning the fractional orders and Lifshitz strength without invoking magnetic fields or geometric ratchets. In the integer limit of local kinetics and Lifshitz-like drift, both constructions reduce to the standard $\varphi_0$ Josephson junction.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2601.02187v1 公告类型：新 摘要：我们开发了一种分数阶 Ginzburg-Landau (GL) 框架，用于由分形超导体或具有非局域相关性的超导介质形成的约瑟夫森结中的非互易超导输运，并由非中心对称正常层分隔。我们证明了不可逆性和超导二极管效应是由 Lifshitz 不变量和分数动力学之间的相互作用产生的，后者是分形几何和有限范围记忆的有效、对称一致的表示。采取了两种互补的方法。在分数积分 GL 公式中，分形空间上的空间积分产生解析解，并揭示了校正如何随分形介质的维数和类 Lifshitz 漂移的强度而缩放。在通过 Agrawal 变分原理和左/右 Caputo 算子导出的基于分数阶导数的公式中，我们获得了规范不变的自由能、相应的 GL 方程和电流密度。我们使用分数阶作为有效参数来表示分形微观结构引起的非局部效应和记忆效应。在二模平面波近似中，我们推导了紧凑的电流相位关系和二极管效率的表达式，并且我们绘制了分数动力学和 Lifshitz/记忆阶上的整流幅度。普拉巴卡函数的精确单侧解决方案进一步证实了稳健、可调的非互易性，包括近乎理想的二极管响应。这确定了一条通向近乎完美的超导二极管的途径，通过在不调用磁场或几何棘轮的情况下调整分数阶和 Lifshitz 强度来实现工程分形（分数动力学）输运。在局部动力学和类 Lifshitz 漂移的整数极限下，两种结构都简化为标准 $\varphi_0$ 约瑟夫森结。
