## Abstract
arXiv:2503.14437v3 Announce Type: replace-cross Abstract: Chemical reaction networks underpin biological and physical phenomena across scales, from microbial interactions to planetary atmosphere dynamics. Bacterial communities exhibit complex competitive interactions for resources, human organs and tissues demonstrate specialized biochemical functions, and planetary atmospheres can display diverse organic and inorganic chemical processes. Despite their complexities, comparing these networks methodically remains a challenge due to the vast underlying degrees of freedom. In biological systems, comparative genomics has been pivotal in tracing evolutionary trajectories and classifying organisms via DNA sequences. However, purely genomic classifications often fail to capture functional roles within ecological systems. Metabolic changes driven by nutrient availability highlight the need for classification schemes that integrate metabolic information. Here we introduce and apply a computational framework for a classification scheme of organisms that compares matrix representations of chemical reaction networks using the Grassmann distance, corresponding to measuring distances between the nullspaces of stoichiometric matrices. Applying this framework to human gut microbiome data confirms that metabolic distances are distinct from phylogenetic distances, underscoring the limitations of genetic information in metabolic classification. Importantly, our analysis of metabolic distances reveals functional groups of organisms enriched or depleted in specific metabolic processes and shows robustness to metabolically silent genetic perturbations. The generalizability of metabolic Grassmann distances is illustrated by application to chemical reaction networks in human tissue and planetary atmospheres, highlighting its potential for advancing functional comparisons across diverse chemical reaction systems.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2503.14437v3 公告类型：replace-cross 摘要：化学反应网络支撑着从微生物相互作用到行星大气动力学的跨尺度生物和物理现象。细菌群落表现出复杂的资源竞争相互作用，人体器官和组织表现出专门的生化功能，行星大气可以表现出多种有机和无机化学过程。尽管它们很复杂，但由于潜在的自由度巨大，有条不紊地比较这些网络仍然是一个挑战。在生物系统中，比较基因组学在追踪进化轨迹和通过 DNA 序列对生物体进行分类方面发挥着关键作用。然而，纯粹的基因组分类通常无法捕捉生态系统中的功能作用。由营养物质可用性驱动的代谢变化凸显了整合代谢信息的分类方案的必要性。在这里，我们介绍并应用一种生物体分类方案的计算框架，该框架使用格拉斯曼距离（对应于测量化学计量矩阵的零空间之间的距离）来比较化学反应网络的矩阵表示。将该框架应用于人类肠道微生物组数据，证实代谢距离与系统发育距离不同，强调了遗传信息在代谢分类中的局限性。重要的是，我们对代谢距离的分析揭示了在特定代谢过程中富集或耗尽的生物体功能群，并显示了对代谢沉默遗传扰动的鲁棒性。通过将代谢格拉斯曼距离应用于人体组织和行星大气中的化学反应网络，说明了代谢格拉斯曼距离的普遍性，突出了其在不同化学反应系统之间推进功能比较的潜力。
