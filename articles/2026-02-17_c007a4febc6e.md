## Abstract
arXiv:2602.13895v1 Announce Type: cross Abstract: One-dimensional chains of coupled spins are minimal models of strongly correlated quantum matter, and have been proposed as wires for transporting quantum information. In liquids, rapid molecular tumbling averages anisotropic dipolar couplings and leaves effective isotropic scalar $J$-coupling Hamiltonians. At zero- to ultralow-field (ZULF) conditions, differences in frequency between nuclear spins of different types are quenched and the internal Hamiltonians can be closely approximated by an isotropic Heisenberg model. In this work, we present [U-$^{13}$C,$^{15}$N]-butyronitrile as a chemically engineered nuclear spin chain whose full spin-spin coupling network can be determined and validated by combining high-field NMR detection with evolution at ultralow fields. Starting from high-field (16.4 T) NMR spectra of $^1$H, $^{13}$C, and $^{15}$N nuclei, we extract all relevant $J$-couplings within a 12-spin network (four $^{13}$C, one $^{15}$N, and seven $^1$H). We then employ a mechanical field-cycling apparatus to prepolarize the spins at high field, shuttle them into a magnetically shielded region for evolution at <50 nT, and detect signals after returning to high field. Fourier analysis of the ultralow-field evolution yields indirect $J$-spectra that are conceptually analogous to ZULF NMR spectra but measured by a high-field NMR spectrometer. We observe clear spectral features at $J$, 1.5$J$, and 2$J$, in good agreement with simulations using the extracted coupling matrix. Finally, we demonstrate 2D experiments that correlate high-field chemical shifts and, thus, fully map interactions within the molecular spin chain. Our results establish [U-$^{13}$C,$^{15}$N]-butyronitrile as an extremely well-characterized spin chain model system and provide a quantitative Hamiltonian benchmark for future hyperpolarization and quantum-control studies.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2602.13895v1 公告类型：cross 摘要：一维耦合自旋链是强相关量子物质的最小模型，并已被提议作为传输量子信息的线路。在液体中，快速分子翻滚平均各向异性偶极耦合并留下有效的各向同性标量 $J$ 耦合哈密顿量。在零到超低场（ZULF）条件下，不同类型核自旋之间的频率差异被淬灭，并且内部哈密顿量可以通过各向同性海森堡模型来近似近似。在这项工作中，我们提出了[U-$^{13}$C,$^{15}$N]-丁腈作为化学工程核自旋链，其完整的自旋-自旋耦合网络可以通过将高场核磁共振检测与超低场演化相结合来确定和验证。从 $^1$H、$^{13}$C 和 $^{15}$N 核的高场 (16.4 T) NMR 谱开始，我们提取 12 个自旋网络内所有相关的 $J$ 耦合（四个 $^{13}$C、一个 $^{15}$N 和七个 $^1$H）。然后，我们采用机械场循环装置在高场下预极化自旋，将它们穿梭到磁屏蔽区域中，以 <50 nT 的速度演化，并在返回高场后检测信号。超低场演化的傅立叶分析产生间接 $J$ 谱，其概念上类似于 ZULF NMR 谱，但由高场 NMR 谱仪测量。我们在 $J$、1.5$J$ 和 2$J$ 处观察到清晰的光谱特征，与使用提取的耦合矩阵进行的模拟非常一致。最后，我们演示了与高场化学位移相关的二维实验，从而完全映射分子自旋链内的相互作用。我们的结果将 [U-$^{13}$C,$^{15}$N]-丁腈建立为一种极其良好表征的自旋链模型系统，并为未来的超极化和量子控制研究提供了定量的哈密顿基准。
