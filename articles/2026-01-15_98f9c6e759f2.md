## Abstract
Quantum annealing provides a powerful platform for simulating magnetic materials and realizing statistical physics models, presenting a compelling alternative to classical Monte Carlo methods. We demonstrate that quantum annealers can accurately reproduce phase diagrams and simulate critical phenomena without suffering from the critical slowing down that often affects classical algorithms. To illustrate this, we study the piled-up dominoes model, which interpolates between the ferromagnetic 2D Ising model and Villain’s fully frustrated “odd model”. We map out its phase diagram and employ finite-size scaling and Binder cumulants on a quantum annealer to study critical exponents for thermal phase transitions. Our method achieves systematic temperature control by tuning the energy scale of the Hamiltonian, eliminating the need to adjust the physical temperature of the quantum hardware. This work demonstrates how, through fine-tuning and calibration, a quantum annealer can be employed to apply sophisticated finite-size scaling techniques from statistical mechanics. Our results establish quantum annealers as robust statistical physics simulators, offering a novel pathway for studying phase transitions and critical behavior.

## 摘要（中文）
量子退火为模拟磁性材料和实现统计物理模型提供了强大的平台，为经典蒙特卡罗方法提供了一种引人注目的替代方案。我们证明量子退火器可以准确地再现相图并模拟关键现象，而不会受到通常影响经典算法的关键减速的影响。为了说明这一点，我们研究了堆积的多米诺骨牌模型，该模型在铁磁二维伊辛模型和恶棍完全受挫的“奇怪模型”之间进行插值。我们绘制了它的相图，并在量子退火器上采用有限尺寸缩放和粘结剂累积量来研究热相变的关键指数。我们的方法通过调整哈密顿量的能量尺度来实现系统的温度控制，无需调整量子硬件的物理温度。这项工作演示了如何通过微调和校准，使用量子退火器来应用统计力学中复杂的有限尺寸缩放技术。我们的研究结果将量子退火器确立为强大的统计物理模拟器，为研究相变和关键行为提供了一条新途径。
