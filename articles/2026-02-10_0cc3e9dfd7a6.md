## Abstract
arXiv:2504.07848v3 Announce Type: replace-cross Abstract: Understanding the emergence of inequality in complex systems requires attention to both structural dynamics and intrinsic heterogeneity. In the context of opinion dynamics, traditional models relied on static snapshots or assumed homogeneous agent behavior, overlooking how diverse cognitive dispositions shape belief evolution. While some recent models introduce behavioral heterogeneity, they typically focus on macro-level patterns, neglecting the unequal and individualized dynamics that unfold at the agent level. In this study, we analyze an adaptive social network model where each agent exhibits one of three behavioral tendencies-homophily, neophily (attention to novelty), or social conformity-and measure the complexity of individual opinion trajectories using normalized Lempel-Ziv complexity. We find that the dynamics are often counterintuitive-homophilic agents, despite seeking similarity, become increasingly unpredictable; neophilic agents, despite pursuing novelty, exhibit constrained exploration; and conformic agents display a two-phase trajectory, transitioning from early stability to later unpredictability. Moreover, these patterns remain similar across diverse network settings, suggesting that internal behavioral dispositions - rather than external environment alone - play a central role in shaping long-term opinion unpredictability. The broader implication is that individuals' experiences of ideological volatility, uncertainty, or stability are not merely environmental, but may be endogenously self-structured through their own cognitive tendencies. These results establish a novel individual-level lens on opinion dynamics, where the behavioral identity of agents serves as a dynamical fingerprint in the evolution of belief systems, and gives rise to persistent disparities in dynamical experience within self-organizing social systems, even in structurally similar environments.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2504.07848v3 公告类型：replace-cross 摘要：理解复杂系统中不平等的出现需要同时关注结构动力学和内在异质性。在舆论动态的背景下，传统模型依赖于静态快照或假设的同质代理行为，忽视了不同的认知倾向如何塑造信念演化。虽然最近的一些模型引入了行为异质性，但它们通常关注宏观层面的模式，而忽略了代理人层面上展开的不平等和个性化的动态。在本研究中，我们分析了一个自适应社交网络模型，其中每个代理表现出三种行为倾向之一——同质性、新奇性（关注新奇）或社会从众——并使用归一化 Lempel-Ziv 复杂性来衡量个人意见轨迹的复杂性。我们发现，动态往往是违反直觉的——同质性代理，尽管寻求相似性，却变得越来越难以预测；尽管追求新奇，但新事物的代理人表现出有限的探索；适形剂表现出两相轨迹，从早期的稳定过渡到后来的不可预测性。此外，这些模式在不同的网络环境中仍然相似，这表明内部行为倾向（而不仅仅是外部环境）在形成长期舆论不可预测性方面发挥着核心作用。更广泛的含义是，个人对意识形态波动、不确定性或稳定性的体验不仅仅是环境因素，而且可能是通过自己的认知倾向内生地自我构建的。这些结果建立了一种新颖的个体层面的舆论动态视角，其中主体的行为身份充当了信仰系统演化中的动态指纹，并导致自组织社会系统内的动态体验持续存在差异，即使在结构相似的环境中也是如此。
