## Abstract
Modern computing powers applications from data analysis to artificial intelligence but now faces limitations. The slowdown of device scaling and the bottleneck between memory and processors motivate architectures that unify computation and data storage. Convolution is a core operation in learning, vision, and signal processing, yet its conventional implementation incurs high energy, high latency, and limited scalability. Magnetic systems that host spin textures, such as domain walls, offer dynamic behaviors that enable computation beyond traditional logic. Here we introduce a compute-in-memory platform that performs convolution by sequentially shifting magnetic domains and sensing the resulting signals. Information is written directly into domain patterns, processed through controlled motion, and read electrically, forming a nonvolatile structure suited for convolution tasks. This approach supports applications including Fourier analysis, neural networks, and image processing, achieving 103to 105improvements in area, energy, and throughput over existing technologies, marking a concrete advance in spintronic computing.

## 摘要（中文）
现代计算为从数据分析到人工智能的应用提供了动力，但现在面临着局限性。设备扩展的放缓以及内存和处理器之间的瓶颈激发了统一计算和数据存储的架构。卷积是学习、视觉和信号处理中的核心运算，但其传统实现会导致高能耗、高延迟和有限的可扩展性。承载自旋纹理的磁性系统（例如畴壁）提供动态行为，使计算能够超越传统逻辑。在这里，我们介绍了一个内存计算平台，该平台通过顺序移动磁域并感测所得信号来执行卷积。信息直接写入域模式，通过受控运动进行处理，并以电方式读取，形成适合卷积任务的非易失性结构。该方法支持傅里叶分析、神经网络和图像处理等应用，与现有技术相比，在面积、能量和吞吐量方面实现了 103 至 105 倍的改进，标志着自旋电子计算领域的具体进步。
