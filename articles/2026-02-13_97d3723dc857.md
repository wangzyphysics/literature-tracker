## Abstract
arXiv:2503.17600v3 Announce Type: replace Abstract: Vascular remodelling is inherent to the pathogenesis of many diseases including cancer, neurodegeneration, fibrosis, hypertension, and diabetes. In this paper, a new susceptibility-contrast based MRI approach is established to analyse intravoxel vessel size distribution (VSD) enabling more comprehensive and quantitative assessment of vascular remodelling than existing clinical imaging modalities. We use segmented vascular structures from light-sheet fluorescence microscopy images of whole rodent brain to simulate gradient echo sampling of free induction decay and spin echo sequence (GESFIDE) and train a deep learning model to predict cerebral blood volume (CBV) and VSD from the simulated GESFIDE signal. The results from ex vivo experiments showed strong correlation (r=0.96) between the true and predicted CBV. Also, high similarity between true and predicted VSDs was observed with mean Bhattacharya Coefficient being 0.92. With further in vivo validation, intravoxel VSD imaging could become a transformative clinical tool for interrogating disease and treatment induced vascular remodelling.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2503.17600v3 公告类型：替换 摘要：血管重塑是许多疾病固有的发病机制，包括癌症、神经退行性变、纤维化、高血压和糖尿病。在本文中，建立了一种新的基于磁敏感对比度的 MRI 方法来分析体素内血管尺寸分布（VSD），从而能够比现有的临床成像方式更全面和定量地评估血管重塑。我们使用整个啮齿动物大脑的光片荧光显微镜图像中的分段血管结构来模拟自由感应衰减和自旋回波序列（GESFIDE）的梯度回波采样，并训练深度学习模型以根据模拟的 GESFIDE 信号预测脑血量（CBV）和 VSD。离体实验结果显示真实 CBV 与预测 CBV 之间存在很强的相关性 (r=0.96)。此外，观察到真实 VSD 与预测 VSD 之间的高度相似性，平均巴塔查里亚系数为 0.92。通过进一步的体内验证，体素内 VSD 成像可以成为一种变革性的临床工具，用于研究疾病和治疗引起的血管重塑。
