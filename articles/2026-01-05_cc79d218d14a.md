## Abstract
arXiv:2410.16005v3 Announce Type: replace-cross Abstract: Metastatic prostate cancer is one of the leading causes of cancer-related morbidity and mortality worldwide. It is characterized by a high mortality rate and a poor prognosis. In this work, we explore how a clinical oncologist can apply a Stackelberg game-theoretic framework to prolong metastatic prostate cancer survival, or even make it chronic in duration. We utilize a Bayesian optimization approach to identify the optimal adaptive chemotherapeutic treatment policy for a single drug (Abiraterone) to maximize the time before the patient begins to show symptoms. We show that, with precise adaptive optimization of drug delivery, it is possible to significantly prolong the cancer suppression period, potentially converting metastatic prostate cancer from a terminal disease to a chronic disease for most patients, as supported by clinical and analytical evidence. We suggest that clinicians might explore the possibility of implementing a high-level tight control (HLTC) treatment, in which the trigger signals (i.e. biomarker levels) for drug administration and cessation are both high and close together, typically yield the best outcomes, as demonstrated through both computation and theoretical analysis. This simple insight could serve as a valuable guide for improving current adaptive chemotherapy treatments in other hormone-sensitive cancers.

arXiv:2410.16005v3 公告类型：replace-cross 摘要：转移性前列腺癌是全球癌症相关发病率和死亡率的主要原因之一。其特点是死亡率高、预后差。在这项工作中，我们探索临床肿瘤学家如何应用 Stackelberg 博弈论框架来延长转移性前列腺癌的生存期，甚至使其成为慢性病。我们利用贝叶斯优化方法来确定单一药物（阿比特龙）的最佳适应性化疗治疗策略，以最大限度地延长患者开始出现症状之前的时间。我们表明，通过精确的适应性优化药物输送，可以显着延长癌症抑制期，有可能将大多数患者的转移性前列腺癌从晚期疾病转变为慢性疾病，这得到了临床和分析证据的支持。我们建议临床医生可以探索实施高水平严格控制（HLTC）治疗的可能性，其中给药和停药的触发信号（即生物标志物水平）都很高且接近，通常会产生最佳结果，如计算和理论分析所证明的那样。这一简单的见解可以作为改善当前其他激素敏感癌症的适应性化疗治疗的宝贵指南。
