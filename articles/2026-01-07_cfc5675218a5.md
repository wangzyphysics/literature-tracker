## Abstract
Nanoclusters enriched in coordinatively unsaturated metal sites usually exhibit distinct geometric and electronic properties and outperform bulk catalysts. However, identifying their atomic structures remains challenging due to vast configurational spaces to explore. In this work, we coupled genetic algorithm (GA) with retrained machine-learning potential (MLP) to accelerate configurational sampling and global minima optimization. Mo₂C nanoclusters in the gas phase and on a graphene support were used as a prototypical model system. The original and fine-tuned EquiformerV2 MLPs using Mo2C-nanoncluster DFT data were assessed for GA-MLP workflow using Kendall’s τ as a ranking metric. EquiformerV2 fine-tuned using targeted DFT data can accelerate GA-driven globle configuration exploration, provided that model evaluation focuses on correct energetic ordering (Kendall’s τ).

## 摘要（中文）
富含配位不饱和金属位点的纳米团簇通常表现出独特的几何和电子特性，并且优于本体催化剂。然而，由于需要探索的构型空间巨大，识别它们的原子结构仍然具有挑战性。在这项工作中，我们将遗传算法 (GA) 与重新训练的机器学习潜力 (MLP) 结合起来，以加速配置采样和全局最小值优化。气相中和石墨烯载体上的 Mo2C 纳米团簇被用作原型模型系统。使用 Kendall 的 τ 作为排名指标，对使用 Mo2C-nanoncluster DFT 数据的原始和微调的 EquiformerV2 MLP 进行了 GA-MLP 工作流程的评估。使用目标 DFT 数据进行微调的 EquiformerV2 可以加速 GA 驱动的全局配置探索，前提是模型评估侧重于正确的能量排序（Kendall τ）。
