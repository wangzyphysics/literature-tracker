## Abstract
arXiv:2501.14359v4 Announce Type: replace-cross Abstract: This study investigates the dynamics of quantum information and computational resources using a tractable model of coupled harmonic oscillators. We precisely characterize the interplay between mutual information, synchronization, and circuit complexity, demonstrating that they serve as complementary yet distinct measures of quantum correlations. Our analysis reveals how coupling strength, detuning, and external magnetic fields modulate these quantities, with synchronization and mutual information exhibiting marked divergence in nonlinear regimes. By employing exact Gaussian methods, we compute the circuit depth required to prepare target states and connect increased fidelity to more regular dynamical behavior. Furthermore, we analyze single-ion transport in a harmonic trap, comparing sudden and adiabatic protocols. We introduce a nonadiabaticity metric to quantify the fidelity-complexity trade-off, showing that smooth control sequences significantly minimize operational errors by suppressing excitations. These results provide a refined understanding of quantum correlations and offer concrete principles for optimizing control strategies in quantum technologies.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2501.14359v4 公告类型：replace-cross 摘要：这项研究使用耦合谐振子的易处理模型来研究量子信息和计算资源的动力学。我们精确地描述了互信息、同步和电路复杂性之间的相互作用，证明它们可以作为量子相关性的互补但独特的度量。我们的分析揭示了耦合强度、失谐和外部磁场如何调制这些量，同步和互信息在非线性状态下表现出明显的发散。通过采用精确的高斯方法，我们计算准备目标状态所需的电路深度，并将增加的保真度与更规则的动态行为联系起来。此外，我们分析了谐波陷阱中的单离子传输，比较了突发协议和绝热协议。我们引入了非绝热性度量来量化保真度-复杂性权衡，表明平滑控制序列通过抑制激励来显着最小化操作错误。这些结果提供了对量子相关性的深入理解，并为优化量子技术中的控制策略提供了具体原则。
