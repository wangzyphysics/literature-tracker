## Abstract
arXiv:2601.08910v1 Announce Type: new Abstract: Real-time data filtering and selection -- or trigger -- systems at high-throughput scientific facilities such as the experiments at the Large Hadron Collider (LHC) must process extremely high-rate data streams under stringent bandwidth, latency, and storage constraints. Yet these systems are typically designed as static, hand-tuned menus of selection criteria grounded in prior knowledge and simulation. In this work, we further explore the concept of a self-driving trigger, an autonomous data-filtering framework that reallocates resources and adjusts thresholds dynamically in real-time to optimize signal efficiency, rate stability, and computational cost as instrumentation and environmental conditions evolve. We introduce a benchmark ecosystem to emulate realistic collider scenarios and demonstrate real-time optimization of a menu including canonical energy sum triggers as well as modern anomaly-detection algorithms that target non-standard event topologies using machine learning. Using simulated data streams and publicly available collision data from the Compact Muon Solenoid (CMS) experiment, we demonstrate the capability to dynamically and automatically optimize trigger performance under specific cost objectives without manual retuning. Our adaptive strategy shifts trigger design from static menus with heuristic tuning to intelligent, automated, data-driven control, unlocking greater flexibility and discovery potential in future high-energy physics analyses.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2601.08910v1 公告类型：新 摘要：大型强子对撞机 (LHC) 实验等高通量科学设施中的实时数据过滤和选择（或触发）系统必须在严格的带宽、延迟和存储限制下处理极高速率的数据流。然而，这些系统通常被设计为基于先验知识和模拟的静态、手动调整的选择标准菜单。在这项工作中，我们进一步探索了自动驾驶触发器的概念，这是一种自主数据过滤框架，可以实时重新分配资源并动态调整阈值，以随着仪器和环境条件的变化优化信号效率、速率稳定性和计算成本。我们引入了一个基准生态系统来模拟现实的对撞机场景，并演示菜单的实时优化，包括规范能量和触发器以及使用机器学习针对非标准事件拓扑的现代异常检测算法。使用来自紧凑型介子螺线管 (CMS) 实验的模拟数据流和公开可用的碰撞数据，我们展示了在特定成本目标下动态自动优化触发性能的能力，而无需手动重新调整。我们的自适应策略将触发设计从具有启发式调整的静态菜单转变为智能、自动化、数据驱动的控制，从而在未来的高能物理分析中释放更大的灵活性和发现潜力。
