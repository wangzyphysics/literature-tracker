## Abstract
arXiv:2601.00492v1 Announce Type: new Abstract: Aluminum-Copper (Al-Cu) alloys are essential materials for weight reduction critical structures in the aerospace and automotive industries, yet achieving their maximum ultrahigh-strength potential remains limited by nanoscale defect control during powder metallurgy processing. We employ large-scale molecular dynamics simulations on Al-6.8%Cu nanoparticles to explore atomic-scale mechanisms governing the full thermal sintering cycle. We demonstrate that while the sintering temperature primarily initiates neck formation, the subsequent cooling rate is the dominant kinetic parameter dictating the final microstructure. Fast cooling rates trap a significantly higher density of stacking faults and can unexpectedly lead to the formation of an amorphous phase at the interparticle interfaces, a feature critically dependent on the rate of thermal dissipation. We confirm a clear shift in the coalescence mechanism from plastic deformation (dislocation slip) at low temperatures (300 K and 450 K) to mass transport via atomic diffusion at high temperatures (600 K). These findings provide essential, atomic-scale guidelines for controlling thermal processing, particularly cooling rates, to design defect-stabilized, high-performance Al-Cu components.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2601.00492v1 公告类型：新 摘要：铝铜 (Al-Cu) 合金是航空航天和汽车行业关键结构减重的重要材料，但实现其最大超高强度潜力仍然受到粉末冶金加工过程中纳米级缺陷控制的限制。我们对 Al-6.8%Cu 纳米颗粒进行大规模分子动力学模拟，以探索控制整个热烧结循环的原子尺度机制。我们证明，虽然烧结温度主要引发颈部形成，但随后的冷却速率是决定最终微观结构的主要动力学参数。快速冷却速率会捕获明显更高密度的堆垛层错，并可能意外地导致在颗粒间界面处形成非晶相，这一特征严重依赖于散热速率。我们证实了聚结机制从低温（300 K 和 450 K）下的塑性变形（位错滑移）到高温（600 K）下通过原子扩散进行的质量传输的明显转变。这些发现为控制热处理（特别是冷却速率）提供了必要的原子尺度指导，以设计缺陷稳定的高性能铝铜组件。
