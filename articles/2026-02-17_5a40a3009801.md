## Abstract
arXiv:2602.14249v1 Announce Type: new Abstract: Efficient transport of cold atoms is essential for continuous operation, enabling applications ranging from atomic lasers to continuously operated qubits. However, deep potentials required to overcome vibrations, axial trap nonuniformity and insufficient cooling have limited transport of cold atoms near quantum degeneracy. Here we demonstrate rapid optical transport of cold atoms to Bose-Einstein condensation using a moving optical lattice formed by two Bessel beams. A gas of $3 \times 10^5$ ytterbium atoms at a temperature of $340\,$nK is transported over $34\,$cm in $350\,$ms with efficiency over $60\%$. Furthermore, a degenerate gas of $1 \times 10^5$ atoms with a $40\%$ condensate fraction emerges from the phase synchronization process driven by atomic interactions. This demonstration enables the fast preparation of ultracold atomic beams and large-scale atom arrays for quantum sensing, simulation and computing.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2602.14249v1 公告类型：新 摘要：冷原子的高效传输对于连续运行至关重要，可实现从原子激光器到连续运行量子位的各种应用。然而，克服振动所需的深电势、轴向陷阱不均匀性和冷却不足限制了冷原子在量子简并附近的传输。在这里，我们使用由两个贝塞尔光束形成的移动光学晶格演示了冷原子到玻色-爱因斯坦凝聚的快速光学传输。温度为 $340\,$nK 的 $3 \times 10^5$ 镱原子气体在 $350\,$ms 内传输超过 $34\,$cm，效率超过 $60\%$。此外，由原子相互作用驱动的相位同步过程中出现了由 $1 \times 10^5$ 原子组成的简并气体，其中凝析油分数为 $40\%$。该演示能够快速制备用于量子传感、模拟和计算的超冷原子束和大规模原子阵列。
