## Abstract
arXiv:2602.12050v1 Announce Type: new Abstract: Objective. Long-term potentiation (LTP) is a fundamental mechanism underlying learning and memory, yet its investigation at the network level in vitro remains challenging, particularly when optogenetic stimulation is used. The objective of this work is to develop a robust experimental and analytical framework for inducing and quantifying optogenetically driven LTP in neuronal cultures recorded with microelectrode arrays (MEAs). Approach. We first systematically investigate the effect of widefield optogenetic stimulation frequency on evoked neuronal activity, to identify a test-stimulus that reliably probes network responses without inducing activity modulation. By analyzing spike-rate dynamics during repeated stimulation, we characterize frequency-dependent response adaptation consistent with Channelrhodopsin-2 photocycle kinetics. Based on these results, an optimized low-frequency test-stimulus is selected and combined with a spatially confined tetanic optogenetic stimulation to induce LTP. Network responses are quantified using post-stimulus time histograms and a normalized efficacy metric, enabling electrode-wise and network-level analysis of plasticity. Main results. Low-frequency optical stimulation (<= 0.2 Hz) preserves stable evoked responses, whereas higher frequencies induce a pronounced sigmoid-like decay in firing rate. Following tetanic stimulation, a subset of electrodes exhibits robust and long-lasting potentiation, persisting for several hours. Significance. This work provides a systematic methodology for studying activity-dependent plasticity in optogenetically driven neuronal networks.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2602.12050v1 公告类型：新 摘要：目标。长时程增强（LTP）是学习和记忆的基本机制，但其在体外网络水平的研究仍然具有挑战性，特别是在使用光遗传学刺激时。这项工作的目标是开发一个强大的实验和分析框架，用于在用微电极阵列 (MEA) 记录的神经元培养物中诱导和量化光遗传学驱动的 LTP。方法。我们首先系统地研究宽场光遗传学刺激频率对诱发神经元活动的影响，以确定可靠地探测网络反应而不诱导活动调节的测试刺激。通过分析重复刺激期间的尖峰速率动态，我们描述了与 Channelrhodopsin-2 光周期动力学一致的频率依赖性响应适应。基于这些结果，选择优化的低频测试刺激并与空间限制的强直光遗传学刺激相结合以诱导 LTP。使用刺激后时间直方图和标准化功效指标来量化网络响应，从而实现电极级和网络级可塑性分析。主要结果。低频光刺激（<= 0.2 Hz）可保持稳定的诱发反应，而较高频率会引起放电率明显的 S 形衰减。强直刺激后，一部分电极表现出强大而持久的增强作用，可持续数小时。意义。这项工作为研究光遗传学驱动的神经元网络中的活动依赖性可塑性提供了系统的方法。
