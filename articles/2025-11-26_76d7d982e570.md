## Abstract
Author(s): Matthew A. Wright and Ram Seshadri Every time a battery is cycled between charged and discharged states, ions and electrons are shuttled between electrodes. In this process, beyond storing charge, the addition and removal of ions in electrodes provides a reversible and tunable handle over the electronic structure and magnetic order. This Research Update paper connects the chemistry of battery electrode materials with the physics of emergent phenomena, including insulator–metal transitions, geometric magnetic frustration, charge and vacancy ordering, and cooperative Jahn–Teller effects. Ion insertion is suggested as being a potentially overlooked handle for the design of functional materials, and open experimental and theoretical challenges are highlighted. [Phys. Rev. Materials 9, 110302] Published Wed Nov 26, 2025

作者：Matthew A. Wright 和 Ram Seshadri 每次电池在充电和放电状态之间循环时，离子和电子都会在电极之间穿梭。在此过程中，除了存储电荷之外，电极中离子的添加​​和去除还提供了对电子结构和磁序的可逆且可调的处理。这篇研究更新论文将电池电极材料的化学与新兴现象的物理学联系起来，包括绝缘体-金属转变、几何磁挫败、电荷和空位排序以及协同杨-泰勒效应。离子插入被认为是功能材料设计中一个可能被忽视的方法，并且强调了开放的实验和理论挑战。 [物理。 Rev. Materials 9, 110302] 2025 年 11 月 26 日星期三发布
