## Abstract
arXiv:2602.01504v3 Announce Type: replace Abstract: Three-dimensional spiral injection enables beam storage in ultra-compact rings with nanosecond revolution periods. We report first storage of a $297 \, \mathrm{keV/}c$ electron beam in a $22 \,\mathrm{cm}$ weak-focusing ring with a $4.7\,\mathrm{ns}$ revolution period using a $140\,\mathrm{ns}$ kicker pulse. A scintillating-fiber detector observes signals $>5\sigma$ above noise for $\geq 1\, \mathrm{\mu s}$, and varying the weak-focusing field potential shifts the stored-beam region, consistent with Monte Carlo predictions, validating beam storage. This proof-of-principle opens a path to ultra-compact storage rings for next-generation precision measurements.
