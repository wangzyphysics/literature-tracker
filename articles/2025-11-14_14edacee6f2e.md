## Abstract
Author(s): Yuanfei Fan, Haoran Chen, Hongyue Xu, Tong Wu, Yunzhuo Wu, Yizi Feng, Yue Chen, Zhe Yuan, and Yizheng Wu This work reveals a giant, current-direction-dependent anisotropic magnetoresistance (AMR) in single-crystal bcc FeNi alloys. The authors demonstrate that the AMR ratio for current along the [100] versus [110] crystal directions reaches a record 87 at 5 K. This effect exhibits a dramatic, nearly tenfold enhancement upon cooling—unlike the temperature-stable AMR observed in CoFe alloys. They further show that this material concurrently exhibits ultralow Gilbert damping (~2.3×10 − 3 ) with negligible dependence on magnetization orientation. The unique combination of a giant, tunable magnetoresistance and low damping establishes bcc FeNi as a promising candidate for advanced magnetic memory and sensing technologies. [Phys. Rev. Materials 9, 114411] Published Fri Nov 14, 2025
