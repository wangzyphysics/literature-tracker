## Abstract
arXiv:2512.21994v1 Announce Type: new Abstract: In this work we investigate the dissociation of CO$_2$ on Cu(110) by performing density functional theory calculations using the vdW-DF2 exchange-correlation functional, with a potential energy surface parameterized using artificial neural networks. We computed quasi-classical trajectory calculations of molecular and dissociative adsorption probabilities as a function of the initial impact energy of the molecules and surface temperature, by comparing our results with available supersonic molecular beam experimental data for normal incidence. Concerning the general dependence of the molecular and dissociative adsorption probabilities on the initial translational energy of the molecules, our theoretical results agree with experiments. Also in agreement with experiments, we have found that dissociative adsorption is not affected by surface temperature between 50 and 400 K, for impact energies for which the dissociation probability is larger than $\sim 10^{-3}$. We have investigated the influence of impact energy and surface temperature on the final state of the dissociation products by extending the time integration of the reactive trajectories up to 10 ps. We have found that above $\sim 2.5$ eV and close to or above room temperature, CO$_2$ dissociation induces strong surface distortions including final structures involving Cu adatoms. The creation of Cu vacancy-adatom pairs is stimulated by the presence of both CO$_{ads}$ and O$_{ads}$ which interact strongly with the Cu adatoms and even give rise to unexpected (O-Cu-CO)$_{ads}$ linear moieties anchored to the surface by the dissociated O atom and involving a Cu adatom almost detached from the surface. These surface distortions produced by dissociation products of high energy CO$_2$ molecules at and above room temperature might explain recent experiments that have found a greater saturation oxygen coverage for high energy molecules.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2512.21994v1 公告类型：新 摘要：在这项工作中，我们通过使用 vdW-DF2 交换相关函数进行密度泛函理论计算，研究 CO$_2$ 在 Cu(110) 上的解离，并使用人工神经网络参数化势能面。通过将我们的结果与可用的法向入射超音速分子束实验数据进行比较，我们计算了分子和解离吸附概率的准经典轨迹计算，作为分子初始冲击能量和表面温度的函数。关于分子和解离吸附概率对分子初始平动能的一般依赖性，我们的理论结果与实验一致。同样与实验一致的是，我们发现，对于解离概率大于 $\sim 10^{-3}$ 的冲击能量，解离吸附不受 50 至 400 K 之间的表面温度的影响。我们通过将反应轨迹的时间积分延长至 10 ps，研究了冲击能量和表面温度对解离产物最终状态的影响。我们发现，在 $\sim 2.5$ eV 以上且接近或高于室温时，CO$_2$ 解离会引起强烈的表面变形，包括涉及铜吸附原子的最终结构。 CO$_{ads}$ 和 O$_{ads}$ 的存在刺激了 Cu 空位-吸附原子对的产生，它们与 Cu 吸附原子强烈相互作用，甚至产生意想不到的 (O-Cu-CO)$_{ads}$ 线性部分，通过解离的 O 原子锚定在表面，并涉及几乎与表面分离的 Cu 吸附原子。在室温及以上温度下，高能 CO$_2$ 分子的解离产物产生的这些表面变形可能可以解释最近的实验，这些实验发现高能分子具有更大的饱和氧覆盖率。
