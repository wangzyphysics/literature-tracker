## Abstract
arXiv:2601.01092v1 Announce Type: new Abstract: Traditional cellular force-sensing techniques, such as traction force microscopy (TFM), are predominantly limited to measuring linear tractions, overlooking and technically unable to capture the nanoscale torsional forces that are critical in cell-matrix interactions. Here, we introduce a nanodiamond-enabled torsion microscopy (DTM) that integrates nitrogen-vacancy (NV) centers as orientation markers with micropillar arrays to decouple and quantify nanoscale rotational and translational motions induced by cells. This approach achieves high precision (~1.47 degree rotational accuracy and ~3.13*10-15 Nm torque sensitivity), enabling reconstruction of cellular torsional force fields and twisting energy distributions previously underestimated. Our findings reveal the widespread presence of torsional forces in cell-matrix interactions, introducing "cellular mechanical modes" where different adhesion patterns dictate the balance between traction- and torque- mediated mechanical energy transferred to the substrate. Notably, in immune cells like macrophages that generally exert low linear tractions, torque overwhelmingly dominates traction, highlighting a unique mechanical output for specific cellular functions. By uncovering these differential modes, DTM provides a versatile tool to advance biomechanical investigations, with potential applications in disease diagnostics and therapeutics.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2601.01092v1 公告类型：新 摘要：传统的细胞力传感技术，例如牵引力显微镜 (TFM)，主要局限于测量线性牵引力，忽略了并且在技术上无法捕获在细胞-基质相互作用中至关重要的纳米级扭转力。在这里，我们介绍了一种纳米金刚石扭转显微镜（DTM），它将氮空位（NV）中心作为方向标记与微柱阵列集成，以解耦和量化由细胞引起的纳米级旋转和平移运动。这种方法实现了高精度（约 1.47 度的旋转精度和约 3.13*10-15 Nm 的扭矩灵敏度），从而能够重建以前低估的细胞扭转力场和扭转能量分布。我们的研究结果揭示了细胞-基质相互作用中广泛存在的扭转力，引入了“细胞机械模式”，其中不同的粘附模式决定了转移到基底的牵引和扭矩介导的机械能之间的平衡。值得注意的是，在巨噬细胞等通常施加低线性牵引力的免疫细胞中，扭矩压倒性地主导牵引力，突出了特定细胞功能的独特机械输出。通过揭示这些差异模式，DTM 提供了一种通用工具来推进生物力学研究，并在疾病诊断和治疗方面具有潜在的应用。
