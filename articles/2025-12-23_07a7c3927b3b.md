## Abstract
An interpretive graphical framework is presented for rationalizing charge-transfer phenomena at molecule–graphene interfaces. Inspired by the classical van Arkel–Ketelaar diagram, the model unifies n- and p-doping pathways within a single representation by explicitly distinguishing between integer charge transfer (ICT) and partial charge transfer (PCT). By mapping the mean frontier-orbital energy and the molecular energy gap onto a triangular coordinate space, the framework provides a compact visualization of the energetic conditions that govern charge transfer, conditions typically dispersed across multiple band–orbital diagrams constructed separately for each adsorbate–adsorbent pair. This single-diagram approach enables the qualitative interpretation of a virtually limitless number of energetic scenarios, initially considering ideal physicochemical conditions. It is further shown that the graphical topology is preserved under non-ideal interface effects, including vacuum-level offsets, interfacial dipoles, structural defects, and finite temperature, which highlights the robustness and broad applicability of the model. Beyond its conceptual value, the framework fulfills a didactic role by offering an intuitive tool for teaching and learning topics related to solid-state chemistry, surface chemistry, interfacial phenomena, doping mechanisms, adsorption, and interfacial charge transfer.

## 摘要（中文）
提出了一个解释性图形框架，用于合理化分子-石墨烯界面的电荷转移现象。受经典 van Arkel-Ketelaar 图的启发，该模型通过明确区分整数电荷转移 (ICT) 和部分电荷转移 (PCT)，将 n 和 p 掺杂路径统一在单一表示中。通过将平均前沿轨道能量和分子能隙映射到三角坐标空间上，该框架提供了控制电荷转移的能量条件的紧凑可视化，这些条件通常分散在为每个吸附物-吸附剂对单独构建的多个带轨道图上。这种单图方法能够对几乎无限数量的能量场景进行定性解释，最初考虑理想的物理化学条件。进一步表明，在非理想界面效应（包括真空级偏移、界面偶极子、结构缺陷和有限温度）下，图形拓扑得以保留，这凸显了该模型的鲁棒性和广泛适用性。除了其概念价值之外，该框架还通过为与固态化学、表面化学、界面现象、掺杂机制、吸附和界面电荷转移相关的教学主题提供直观的工具来发挥教学作用。
