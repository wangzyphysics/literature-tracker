## Abstract
arXiv:2602.09569v1 Announce Type: cross Abstract: Deep learning has revolutionized modern society but faces growing energy and latency constraints. Deep physical neural networks (PNNs) are interconnected computing systems that directly exploit analog dynamics for energy-efficient, ultrafast AI execution. Realizing this potential, however, requires universal training methods tailored to physical intricacies. Here, we present the Physical Information Bottleneck (PIB), a general and efficient framework that integrates information theory and local learning, enabling deep PNNs to learn under arbitrary physical dynamics. By allocating matrix-based information bottlenecks to each unit, we demonstrate supervised, unsupervised, and reinforcement learning across electronic memristive chips and optical computing platforms. PIB also adapts to severe hardware faults and allows for parallel training via geographically distributed resources. Bypassing auxiliary digital models and contrastive measurements, PIB recasts PNN training as an intrinsic, scalable information-theoretic process compatible with diverse physical substrates.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2602.09569v1 公告类型：交叉 摘要：深度学习彻底改变了现代社会，但面临着日益增长的能源和延迟限制。深度物理神经网络 (PNN) 是互连的计算系统，直接利用模拟动力学来实现节能、超快的人工智能执行。然而，要实现这一潜力，需要针对身体复杂性量身定制的通用训练方法。在这里，我们提出了物理信息瓶颈（PIB），这是一个集成信息论和局部学习的通用且高效的框架，使深度 PNN 能够在任意物理动力学下学习。通过将基于矩阵的信息瓶颈分配给每个单元，我们演示了跨电子忆阻芯片和光学计算平台的监督学习、无监督学习和强化学习。 PIB 还适应严重的硬件故障，并允许通过地理分布的资源进行并行训练。绕过辅助数字模型和对比测量，PIB 将 PNN 训练重塑为与不同物理基质兼容的内在的、可扩展的信息理论过程。
