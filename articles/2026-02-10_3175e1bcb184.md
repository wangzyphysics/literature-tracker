## Abstract
arXiv:2602.08247v1 Announce Type: new Abstract: Learning using Computer-Assisted Instruction (CAI) demands a high level of attention given the tendency to be distracted and mind-wander. How does the online STEM instructor know when learners are having attentional problems and the extent to which these problems affect learning? In the present study, the visual attentional and cognitive state of physics graduate students were probed while they went through a multimedia instructional module to refresh their knowledge of Newton's II Law. Data from an eye tracker, webcam, egocentric glasses, screen recording, and mouse and keyboard events were integrated to record learners' attention overt attention to the learning environment (+/-) and thinking about learning content (+/-) to analyze students' attention spans during learning from this module. On average, learners were found to be on-task and on-screen for a vast majority of time, with evidence of mind wandering. The learning module improved the participants efficiency with which they answered the questions correctly on a post-test relative to the pre-test. Further, there is a positive albeit statistically non-significant correlation between the improvement from pre- to post-test efficiency and the time spent on-screen and on-task during the module.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2602.08247v1 公告类型：新 摘要：使用计算机辅助教学 (CAI) 进行学习需要高度集中注意力，因为容易分散注意力和走神。在线 STEM 教师如何知道学习者何时出现注意力问题以及这些问题影响学习的程度？在本研究中，对物理研究生在通过多媒体教学模块更新牛顿第二定律知识时的视觉注意力和认知状态进行了调查。整合来自眼动仪、网络摄像头、自我中心眼镜、屏幕记录以及鼠标和键盘事件的数据，记录学习者对学习环境的明显关注 (+/-) 和对学习内容的思考 (+/-)，以分析学生在学习该模块期间的注意力广度。平均而言，学习者绝大多数时间都在专注于任务和屏幕上，有走神的迹象。学习模块提高了参与者在后测中相对于前测正确回答问题的效率。此外，测试前和测试后效率的提高与模块期间在屏幕上和执行任务所花费的时间之间存在正相关，尽管统计上不显着。
