## Abstract
DNA sequencing using nanopores and a mechanical positioning system with 0.1-0.15 nm movement precision and ~1 μs movement times is proposed. A single strand of DNA (ssDNA) is attached to a homonucleotide header immobilized on a glass slide mounted on a platform in the cis chamber of an electrolytic cell (e-cell) with a nanopore. The header-strand is moved into the trans chamber and retracted mechanically one nucleotide at a time. Two approaches to sequencing are described. In the first the blockade current due to the segment of the header-strand in the pore is measured with a low bandwidth (< 1 KHZ); a Markov-like method identifies the next base from earlier blockade current levels. In principle, with a bandwidth of 1 KHz and an array of ~600 pores with a CMOS detector array a human genome (3 × 109 bases) can be sequenced in < 1.5 hours, or ~5 hours start-to-finish (sample preparation, ligation with header, threading, data acquisition, and post-processing, plus machine-learning-based methods for base calling). This theoretical sequencing speed is comparable to the practical speed of the recently reported Sequencing By Expansion (SBX) method; it remains to be seen if this level of performance can be delivered in practice. The second approach is based on replacing the enzyme motor in the Sequencing-by-Synthesis (SBS) method with a mechanical positioning system and using a header-strand with a hybridized primer. Synthesis consists of elongating the latter with a protected dNTP carrying a fluorescently tagged base that complements the next open base in the strand followed by deprotection of the dNTP and cleaving of the tag, the latter is detected optically with a Total Internal Reflection Fluorescence (TIRF) camera in trans. This electromechanical-optical alternative to SBS provides better control over sequencing by shielding earlier added nucleotides and reducing cross-talk in the neighborhood of the terminal nucleotide being extended. In theory, with a 1000 × 1000 array of DNA header-strands each ~200 bases long and a cycle time of ~90 seconds, a human chromosome can be sequenced in ~5 hours. The possibility of using the method for data retrieval from DNA-based storage is discussed.

## 摘要（中文）
提出使用纳米孔和机械定位系统进行 DNA 测序，其运动精度为 0.1-0.15 nm，运动时间约为 1 μs。将单链 DNA (ssDNA) 连接到固定在载玻片上的同核苷酸标头，载玻片安装在具有纳米孔的电解池 (e-cell) 顺式室的平台上。标题链被移入转室并机械地一次缩回一个核苷酸。描述了两种测序方法。第一种方法是用低带宽 (< 1 KHZ) 测量由于孔中的头部链段而产生的阻塞电流；类似马尔可夫的方法从早期封锁电流水平中识别出下一个碱基。原则上，使用 1 KHz 的带宽和约 600 个孔的阵列以及 CMOS 检测器阵列，可以在 < 1.5 小时或约 5 小时的时间内对人类基因组（3 × 109 个碱基）进行测序（样品制备、标头连接、线程、数据采集和后处理，以及基于机器学习的碱基识别方法）。这一理论测序速度与最近报道的扩展测序（SBX）方法的实际速度相当；实践中能否达到这种水平还有待观察。第二种方法是用机械定位系统替换合成测序（SBS）方法中的酶马达，并使用带有杂交引物的标题链。合成包括用带有荧光标记碱基的受保护 dNTP 延伸后者，该碱基与链中的下一个开放碱基互补，然后对 dNTP 进行脱保护并切割标签，后者通过反式全内反射荧光 (TIRF) 相机进行光学检测。这种 SBS 的机电光学替代方案通过屏蔽早期添加的核苷酸并减少正在延伸的末端核苷酸附近的串扰，可以更好地控制测序。理论上，利用 1000 × 1000 的 DNA 头链阵列（每条约 200 个碱基长）和约 90 秒的循环时间，可以在约 5 小时内对人类染色体进行测序。讨论了使用该方法从基于 DNA 的存储中检索数据的可能性。
