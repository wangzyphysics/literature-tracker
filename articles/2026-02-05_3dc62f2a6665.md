## Abstract
Author(s): J. Dobaczewski, A. E. Stuchbery, G. Danneaux, A. Nagpal, P. L. Sassarini, and H. Wibowo Nuclear moments are fundamental nuclear characteristics such as whether an individual nuclear state is deformed, or whether the nucleus carries its angular momentum as a collective whole or as individual nucleons with contributions from both their orbital motion and their intrinsic spin. However, calculations of nuclear moments have long been carried out mostly for certain classes of nuclei rather than seamlessly across an entire region. The authors report comprehensive calculations of spectroscopic magnetic dipole and electric quadrupole moments in odd- N , even- Z nuclei with their code HFODD. The calculations cover gadolinium to osmium nuclei, ranging from near-spherical to well-deformed nuclei, within nuclear density functional theory with the Skyrme functional UNEDEF1. Comparison with experimental data for 82 states without parameter adjustment demonstrates that the study captures the main features of magnetic dipole and electric quadrupole moments. The holistic approach to an entire region of the nuclear chart provides valuable insight into the evolution of the underlying nuclear structure and will help guide future fine-tuning of the functionals. [Phys. Rev. C 113, 024306] Published Thu Feb 05, 2026
