# Quantum Anomalous Hall Effect in Ferromagnetic Metals

## Abstract
arXiv:2512.23394v1 Announce Type: new Abstract: The quantum anomalous Hall (QAH) effect holds fundamental importance in topological physics and technological promise for electronics. It is generally believed that the QAH effect can only be realized in insulators. In this Letter, we theoretically demonstrate that the QAH effect can also be realized in metallic systems, representing a phase distinct from the conventional QAH phase in insulators. This phase is characterized by the coexistence of chiral edge channels and isotropic bulk conduction channels without a bulk energy gap. Notably, in a six-terminal Hall bar, our calculations show that, the quantized Hall conductivity and nonzero longitudinal conductivity can emerge due to dephasing, despite the Hall resistivity itself never becoming quantized. Furthermore, the quantized Hall conductivity exhibits remarkable robustness against disorder. Our findings not only extend the range of materials capable of hosting the QAH effect from insulators to metals, but also provide insights that may pave the way for the experimental realization of the QAH effect at elevated temperatures.

arXiv:2512.23394v1 公告类型：新 摘要：量子反常霍尔 (QAH) 效应在拓扑物理和电子技术前景中具有根本重要性。一般认为QAH效应只能在绝缘体中实现。在这封信中，我们从理论上证明了 QAH 效应也可以在金属系统中实现，代表了与绝缘体中传统 QAH 相不同的相。该相的特征是手性边缘通道和各向同性体传导通道共存，没有体能隙。值得注意的是，在六端子霍尔棒中，我们的计算表明，尽管霍尔电阻率本身从未被量子化，但量子化霍尔电导率和非零纵向电导率可能由于移相而出现。此外，量子化霍尔电导率对无序表现出显着的鲁棒性。我们的研究结果不仅将能够承载 QAH 效应的材料范围从绝缘体扩展到金属，而且还提供了见解，可能为在高温下实验实现 QAH 效应铺平道路。
