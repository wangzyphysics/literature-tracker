## Abstract
arXiv:2512.08766v2 Announce Type: replace Abstract: Zermelo's navigation problem seeks the trajectory of minimal travel time between two points in a fluid flow. We address this problem for an agent -- such as a micro-robot or active particle -- that is advected by a two-dimensional flow, self-propels at a fixed speed smaller than or comparable to the characteristic flow velocity, and can steer its direction. The flows considered span increasing levels of complexity, from steady solid-body rotation to the Taylor-Green flow and fully developed turbulence in the inverse cascade regime. Although optimal control theory provides time-minimizing trajectories, these solutions become unstable in chaotic regimes realized for complex background flows. To design robust navigation strategies under such conditions, we apply reinforcement learning. Both action-value (Q-learning) and policy-gradient (one-step actor-critic) methods achieve successful navigation with comparable performance. Crucially, we show that agents trained on coarse-grained turbulent flows -- retaining only large-scale features -- generalize effectively to the full velocity field. This robustness to incomplete flow information is essential for practical navigation in real-world oceanic and atmospheric environments.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2512.08766v2 公告类型：替换 摘要：Zermelo 的导航问题寻求流体流动中两点之间的最小旅行时间轨迹。我们为代理（例如微型机器人或活性粒子）解决了这个问题，该代理通过二维流进行平流，以小于或等于特征流速的固定速度自我推进，并且可以操纵其方向。所考虑的流动的复杂程度不断增加，从稳定的固体旋转到泰勒-格林流动，再到逆级联流态中充分发展的湍流。尽管最优控制理论提供了时间最小化轨迹，但这些解决方案在复杂背景流实现的混沌状态中变得不稳定。为了在这种情况下设计稳健的导航策略，我们应用了强化学习。行动价值（Q-学习）和策略梯度（一步行动者批评家）方法都以可比的性能实现了成功的导航。至关重要的是，我们表明，在粗粒度湍流上训练的智能体（仅保留大规模特征）可以有效地推广到完整的速度场。这种对不完整流量信息的鲁棒性对于现实海洋和大气环境中的实际导航至关重要。
