## Abstract
arXiv:2507.20318v2 Announce Type: replace-cross Abstract: Quantum annealing is a promising metaheuristic for solving constrained combinatorial optimization problems. However, parameter tuning difficulties and hardware noise often prevent optimal solutions from being properly encoded as the ground states of the problem Hamiltonian. This study investigates mid-anneal measurement as a mitigation approach for such situations, analyzing its effectiveness and underlying physical mechanisms. We introduce a quantitative metric to evaluate the effectiveness of mid-anneal measurement and apply it to the graph bipartitioning problem and the quadratic knapsack problem. Our findings reveal that mid-anneal measurement is most effective when the energy difference between desired solutions and ground states is small, with effectiveness strongly governed by the energy structure. Furthermore, the effectiveness increases as the Hamming distance between the ground and excited states gets small, highlighting the role of state similarity. Analysis of fully-connected Ising models demonstrates that the effectiveness of mid-anneal measurement persists with increasing system size, indicating its scalability and practical applicability to large-scale quantum annealing.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2507.20318v2 公告类型：replace-cross 摘要：量子退火是解决约束组合优化问题的一种有前景的元启发法。然而，参数调整困难和硬件噪声通常会阻止最优解被正确编码为问题哈密顿量的基态。本研究调查了中间退火测量作为此类情况的缓解方法，分析了其有效性和潜在的物理机制。我们引入了一种定量度量来评估中间退火测量的有效性，并将其应用于图二分问题和二次背包问题。我们的研究结果表明，当所需溶液和基态之间的能量差异很小时，中间退火测量是最有效的，其有效性很大程度上取决于能量结构。此外，随着基态和激发态之间的汉明距离变小，有效性增加，突出了状态相似性的作用。对全连接伊辛模型的分析表明，随着系统尺寸的增加，中间退火测量的有效性仍然存在，表明其可扩展性和对大规模量子退火的实际适用性。
