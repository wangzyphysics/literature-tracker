## Abstract
arXiv:2601.00460v1 Announce Type: new Abstract: Various theoretical and empirical studies have accounted for why humans cooperate in competitive environments. Although prior work has revealed that network structure and multiplex interactions can promote cooperation, most theory assumes that individuals play similar dilemma games in all social contexts. However, real-world agents may participate in a diversity of interactions, not all of which present dilemmas. We develop an evolutionary game model on multilayer networks in which one layer supports the prisoner's dilemma game, while the other follows constant-selection dynamics, representing biased but non-dilemmatic competition, akin to opinion or fad spreading. Our theoretical analysis reveals that coupling a social dilemma layer to a non-dilemmatic constant-selection layer robustly enhances cooperation in many cases, across different multilayer networks, updating rules, and payoff schemes. These findings suggest that embedding individuals within diverse networked settings -- even those unrelated to direct social dilemmas -- can be a principled approach to engineering cooperation in socio-ecological and organizational systems.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2601.00460v1 公告类型：新 摘要：各种理论和实证研究已经解释了人类在竞争环境中合作的原因。尽管先前的工作已经揭示网络结构和多重交互可以促进合作，但大多数理论假设个人在所有社会环境中都会玩类似的困境游戏。然而，现实世界的智能体可能会参与多种交互，但并非所有交互都会带来困境。我们在多层网络上开发了一种进化博弈模型，其中一层支持囚徒困境博弈，而另一层遵循恒定选择动态，代表有偏见但非困境的竞争，类似于舆论或时尚传播。我们的理论分析表明，将社会困境层与非困境常选择层耦合可以在许多情况下强有力地增强跨不同多层网络、更新规则和支付方案的合作。这些发现表明，将个人嵌入不同的网络环境中——即使是那些与直接社会困境无关的环境——可以成为社会生态和组织系统中工程合作的原则性方法。
