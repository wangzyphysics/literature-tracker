## Abstract
arXiv:2512.22529v1 Announce Type: cross Abstract: Aluminum nanoparticles (ANPs) are among the most energy-dense solid fuels, yet the atomic mechanisms governing their transition from passivated particles to explosive reactants remain elusive. This stems from a fundamental computational bottleneck: ab initio methods offer quantum accuracy but are restricted to small spatiotemporal scales (< 500 atoms, picoseconds), while empirical force fields lack the reactive fidelity required for complex combustion environments. Herein, we bridge this gap by employing a "human-in-the-loop" closed-loop framework where self-auditing AI Agents validate the evolution of a machine learning potential (MLP). By acting as scientific sentinels that visualize hidden model artifacts for human decision-making, this collaborative cycle ensures quantum mechanical accuracy while exhibiting near-linear scalability to million-atom systems and accessing nanosecond timescales (energy RMSE: 1.2 meV/atom, force RMSE: 0.126 eV/Angstrom). Strikingly, our simulations reveal a temperature-regulated dual-mode oxidation mechanism: at moderate temperatures, the oxide shell acts as a dynamic "gatekeeper," regulating oxidation through a "breathing mode" of transient nanochannels; above a critical threshold, a "rupture mode" unleashes catastrophic shell failure and explosive combustion. Importantly, we resolve a decades-old controversy by demonstrating that aluminum cation outward diffusion, rather than oxygen transport, dominates mass transfer across all temperature regimes, with diffusion coefficients consistently exceeding those of oxygen by 2-3 orders of magnitude. These discoveries establish a unified atomic-scale framework for energetic nanomaterial design, enabling the precision engineering of ignition sensitivity and energy release rates through intelligent computational design.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2512.22529v1 公告类型：cross 摘要：铝纳米颗粒（ANP）是能量密度最高的固体燃料之一，但控制其从钝化颗粒到爆炸性反应物转变的原子机制仍然难以捉摸。这源于一个基本的计算瓶颈：从头算方法提供了量子精度，但仅限于较小的时空尺度（< 500 个原子，皮秒），而经验力场缺乏复杂燃烧环境所需的反应保真度。在这里，我们通过采用“人机循环”闭环框架来弥补这一差距，其中自我审计的人工智能代理验证机器学习潜力（MLP）的演变。通过充当科学哨兵，将隐藏的模型工件可视化以供人类决策，该协作循环可确保量子力学准确性，同时展现出百万原子系统的近线性可扩展性并访问纳秒时间尺度（能量 RMSE：1.2 meV/原子，力 RMSE：0.126 eV/埃）。引人注目的是，我们的模拟揭示了温度调节的双模式氧化机制：在中等温度下，氧化物壳充当动态“看门人”，通过瞬态纳米通道的“呼吸模式”调节氧化；在中等温度下，氧化物壳充当动态“看门人”，通过瞬态纳米通道的“呼吸模式”调节氧化；当超过临界阈值时，“破裂模式”会引发灾难性的外壳故障和爆炸性燃烧。重要的是，我们通过证明铝阳离子向外扩散而不是氧传输在所有温度范围内主导着传质，并且扩散系数始终超过氧的扩散系数 2-3 个数量级，解决了长达数十年的争议。这些发现为高能纳米材料设计建立了统一的原子尺度框架，通过智能计算设计实现点火灵敏度和能量释放速率的精密工程。
