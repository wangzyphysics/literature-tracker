## Abstract
Author(s): Walter Kutschera, A. J. Timothy Jull, Michael Paul, and Anton Wallner Accelerator mass spectrometry (AMS) is a mass-spectrometric method using entire accelerator systems to measure ultralow traces of long-lived radioisotopes. AMS spectrometers produce an ion beam from a sample of interest and separate ions according to their magnetic, electric, and atomic characteristics. It is thus possible to identify both the mass number and the atomic number of a very rare radioisotope, and count it atom by atom. The review describes the 45-year history since the discovery of AMS, detailed technical aspects, and a wide range of research fields. [Rev. Mod. Phys. 95, 035006] Published Thu Sep 28, 2023

作者：Walter Kutschera、A. J. Timothy Jull、Michael Paul 和 Anton Wallner 加速器质谱 (AMS) 是一种使用整个加速器系统来测量超低痕量长寿命放射性同位素的质谱方法。 AMS 光谱仪从感兴趣的样品中产生离子束，并根据离子的磁、电和原子特性分离离子。因此，可以识别非常稀有的放射性同位素的质量数和原子序数，并逐个原子地对其进行计数。该综述描述了 AMS 自发现以来 45 年的历史、详细的技术方面以及广泛的研究领域。 [牧师。模组。物理。 95, 035006] 于 2023 年 9 月 28 日星期四发布
