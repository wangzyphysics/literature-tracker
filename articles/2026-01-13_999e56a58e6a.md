## Abstract
End-point binding free energy (BFE) methods, such as molecular mechanics Poisson–Boltzmann surface area (MMPBSA), are widely used to estimate protein–ligand binding affinity due to their favorable balance between accuracy and computational efficiency. Their reliability, however, is often limited by approximations in intramolecular interactions and solvation effects. Given the critical role of force field quality in determining accuracy, we developed a hybrid framework named AIQM-PBSA, which integrates the ONIOM scheme with the PBSA model. Within this framework, the AIQM3 machine learning interatomic potential (MLIP) —an advanced Δ-learning quantum mechanical (QM) model—is employed to refine the molecular mechanics (MM) energy term, while polar and non polar solvation contributions are evaluated under the PBSA formalism. Extensive validation across diverse protein–ligand systems demonstrates that AIQM-PBSA significantly improves the correlation with experimental binding affinities compared to MMPBSA based on classical force fields and other MLIPs, with a representative benchmark showing up to 31% higher Pearson correlation relative to the MMPBSA baseline and 16% higher than ANI-2x. Furthermore, incorporating entropic contributions can further provide modest, target-dependent improvements. In summary, AIQM-PBSA offers a robust and transferable framework that combines QM level accuracy with MM level efficiency, substantially enhancing the reliability of endpoint free energy calculations for biomolecular recognition.

## 摘要（中文）
端点结合自由能（BFE）方法，例如分子力学泊松-玻尔兹曼表面积（MMPBSA），由于其在准确性和计算效率之间的良好平衡而被广泛用于估计蛋白质-配体结合亲和力。然而，它们的可靠性常常受到分子内相互作用和溶剂化效应的近似的限制。鉴于力场质量在确定精度方面的关键作用，我们开发了一种名为 AIQM-PBSA 的混合框架，它将 ONIOM 方案与 PBSA 模型集成在一起。在此框架内，采用 AIQM3 机器学习原子间势 (MLIP)（一种先进的 Δ-学习量子力学 (QM) 模型）来细化分子力学 (MM) 能量项，同时在 PBSA 形式下评估极性和非极性溶剂化贡献。跨不同蛋白质-配体系统的广泛验证表明，与基于经典力场和其他 MLIP 的 MMPBSA 相比，AIQM-PBSA 显着提高了与实验结合亲和力的相关性，代表性基准显示相对于 MMPBSA 基线，皮尔逊相关性高出 31%，比 ANI-2x 高出 16%。此外，结合熵贡献可以进一步提供适度的、依赖于目标的改进。总之，AIQM-PBSA 提供了一个强大且可转移的框架，将 QM 级精度与 MM 级效率相结合，大大提高了生物分子识别端点自由能计算的可靠性。
