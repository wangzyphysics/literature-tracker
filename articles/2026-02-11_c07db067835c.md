## Abstract
arXiv:2602.09938v1 Announce Type: new Abstract: Moir\'e superlattices in two-dimensional (2D) materials exhibit rich quantum phenomena, but ab initio modelling of these systems remains computationally prohibitive. Existing machine learning methods for accelerating density-functional theory (DFT) can target the prediction of different quantities and often rely on the locality assumption. Here we train a Gaussian process regression SALTED model exclusively on the electron densities of small displaced bilayer structures and then extrapolate electron density prediction to the large supercells required to describe small twist angles between these bilayers. We show the necessity of long-range descriptors to yield reliable band structures and electrostatic properties of large twisted bilayer structures, when these are derived from predicted densities. We demonstrate that the choice of descriptor determines the distribution of residual density errors, which in turn affects the downstream electronic properties. We apply our models to twisted bilayer graphene, hexagonal boron nitride, and transition metal dichalcogenides, focusing on the model's capacity to predict complex phenomena, including flat band formation, bandwidth narrowing, domain-wall electric fields, and spin-orbit coupling effects. Beyond moir\'e materials, this approach provides a general methodology for electronic structure prediction in large-scale systems with substantial long-range phenomena related to non-local geometric information.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2602.09938v1 公告类型：新 摘要：二维 (2D) 材料中的莫尔超晶格表现出丰富的量子现象，但这些系统的从头建模在计算上仍然令人望而却步。现有的用于加速密度泛函理论（DFT）的机器学习方法可以针对不同数量的预测，并且通常依赖于局域性假设。在这里，我们专门针对小型位移双层结构的电子密度训练高斯过程回归 SALTED 模型，然后将电子密度预测外推到描述这些双层之间的小扭转角所需的大型超级电池。我们展示了长程描述符的必要性，以产生可靠的带结构和大型扭曲双层结构的静电特性，当这些是从预测的密度导出时。我们证明描述符的选择决定了残余密度误差的分布，这反过来又影响下游的电子特性。我们将模型应用于扭曲双层石墨烯、六方氮化硼和过渡金属二硫属化物，重点关注模型预测复杂现象的能力，包括平带形成、带宽变窄、畴壁电场和自旋轨道耦合效应。除了莫尔材料之外，这种方法还为具有与非局部几何信息相关的大量远程现象的大规模系统中的电子结构预测提供了通用方法。
