## Abstract
arXiv:2503.14270v2 Announce Type: replace Abstract: Metallic coatings are used to enhance the durability of metal surfaces by protecting them from corrosion. These protective layers are typically deposited in a fluid state via a liquid film. Controlling instabilities in the liquid film is crucial to achieving uniform, high-quality coatings. This study explores the possibility of controlling liquid films on a moving substrate using a combination of gas jets and electromagnetic actuators. To model the 3D liquid film, we extend existing integral models to incorporate the effects of electromagnetic actuators. The control strategy was developed within a reinforcement learning framework, in which the Proximal Policy Optimisation (PPO) algorithm interacts with the liquid film via pneumatic and electromagnetic actuators to optimise a reward function that accounts for instability-wave amplitude through a trial-and-error process. The PPO identified an optimal control law that reduced interface instabilities via a novel mechanism: gas jets push crests, and electromagnets raise troughs via the Lorentz force.
