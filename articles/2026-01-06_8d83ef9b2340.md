## Abstract
arXiv:2601.02328v1 Announce Type: new Abstract: Sea ice harbours a rich community of well-adapted microorganisms that inhabit liquid micro-spaces where extreme conditions prevail. Currently at risk under climate change, the sea-ice microbiome holds mysteries about evolution of life on Earth and possibly elsewhere, which require methodological innovation to be unravelled. Gaining microscopic insight into the internal structure and biology of sea ice has traditionally been limited to destructive and extrusive ice core sampling methods. Here we present an in situ microscopic imaging system to observe undisturbed living microorganisms directly within sea the ice matrix. The complex and heterogeneous nature of sea ice, including its water crystal lattice, brine channels, air bubbles, and various impurities, presents engineering challenges for the development of this imaging system. Despite the fragile nature of the sea-ice matrix, we could successfully deploy, test and use the new in situ microscope during a recent expedition on the icepack in Arctic. We collected numerous images of live and intact single-celled and colony-forming diatoms, and documented for the first time at such a high resolution some microphysical features of sea ice. The hardware and software design of the endoscope is presented along with acquisition results of the microstructure and diatom images. These findings collectively demonstrate the potential for this new in situ microscopic imaging system to transform the way we study sea ice and to allow a deeper understanding of its complex microstructure and living microorganisms.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2601.02328v1 公告类型：新 摘要：海冰蕴藏着丰富的适应性强的微生物群落，这些微生物栖息在极端条件下的液体微空间中。目前，海冰微生物组面临着气候变化的风险，它蕴藏着地球上乃至其他地方生命进化的谜团，需要方法创新来解开。传统上，对海冰内部结构和生物学的微观了解仅限于破坏性和挤压性冰芯采样方法。在这里，我们提出了一种原位显微成像系统，可以直接观察海洋冰基质内未受干扰的活微生物。海冰的复杂性和异质性，包括其水晶格、盐水通道、气泡和各种杂质，给该成像系统的开发带来了工程挑战。尽管海冰基质脆弱，但我们在最近的北极冰层探险中成功部署、测试和使用了新型原位显微镜。我们收集了大量活体和完整的单细胞和菌落形成硅藻的图像，并首次以如此高分辨率记录了海冰的一些微观物理特征。介绍了内窥镜的硬件和软件设计以及显微结构和硅藻图像的采集结果。这些发现共同证明了这种新的原位显微成像系统有可能改变我们研究海冰的方式，并可以更深入地了解其复杂的微观结构和活微生物。
